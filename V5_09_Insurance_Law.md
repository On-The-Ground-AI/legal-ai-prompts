1. Maps the policy scope of coverage (what perils/risks are covered)
2. Identifies exclusions and limitations (standard exclusions, industry-specific carve-outs)
3. Analyzes conditions precedent to coverage (notice requirements, investigation cooperation)
4. Maps the deductible/retention structure and how it applies
5. Identifies sub-limits that apply to specific coverages (lower limits than overall policy limit)
6. Analyzes any endorsements or amendments that modify coverage
7. Maps definitions crucial to coverage (what constitutes a "claim," "occurrence," "loss")
8. Identifies policy cancellation, non-renewal, and reinstatement provisions
9. Analyzes territorial coverage (is claim location within policy territory?)
10. Maps insurable interest requirements (does insured have proper interest in subject matter?)
11. Identifies any ambiguities in policy language that should be resolved in insured's favor
12. Advises on coverage counseling (whether claim is likely covered under policy terms)
