## Cross-Cutting Features

All prompts share these characteristics:

### Format & Structure
- Full prompt text (copy-paste ready)
- Bracketed placeholders [INSERT DETAILS] for customization
- Context statement (when and why to use)
- Difficulty level (Beginner/Intermediate/Advanced)
- Best AI tool recommendation (Claude/ChatGPT/Copilot)
- Follow-up prompt suggestions for workflow chaining

### Content Depth
- 100-300 words per prompt
- Structured numbered guidance (10-20 actionable steps)
- Singapore regulatory focus with international benchmarking
- Case studies and enforcement precedent references
- Practical implementation frameworks
- Template references and procedure guidance

**Singapore Frameworks:**
- Financial Services Act (FSA) and MAS Notices
- Securities and Futures Act (SFA) and SGX Listing Rules
- Competition Act and CCCS framework
- Insurance Act and insurance intermediary rules
- Payment Systems Act
- Prevention of Corruption Act (PCA)
- Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA)
- Constitution (sanctions and monetary authority authority)
- Companies Act and related corporate governance
- Various MAS, CCCS, PDPC, STB, ACRA guidelines

**International References:**
- FCA (UK) regulatory framework
- SEC (US) regulatory framework
- EU regulations (GDPR, MiFID II, Reg SHO, etc.)
- OECD Anti-Bribery Convention
- FATF Recommendations (AML/CFT)
- ISO standards (27001, 37001, etc.)
- NIST cybersecurity framework
- TCFD and GRI sustainability reporting

### Use Cases

Individual prompts are designed for:
1. **Standalone Use:** Each prompt can be used independently for specific compliance need
2. **Workflow Chaining:** Follow-up prompts create multi-step investigation/implementation processes
3. **Training & Reference:** Staff training on regulatory frameworks and compliance procedures
4. **Project Planning:** Roadmap development for regulatory implementation projects
5. **Risk Assessment:** Identifying compliance gaps and control design
6. **Incident Response:** Procedures when violations are discovered or investigations commence
7. **External Communication:** Responding to regulators, auditors, and other stakeholders
