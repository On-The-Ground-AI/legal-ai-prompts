# AI-Specific Contract Clauses

## Overview
This section provides template contract clauses for AI-related agreements, including AI liability allocation, vendor agreements, IP ownership, data processing, and responsible AI obligations.

---

## SECTION 1: AI Liability & Responsibility

**PROMPT 34 — AI Liability Clauses: Allocating Risk in AI Use**

Draft comprehensive liability allocation clauses for contracts involving AI, addressing the critical question: "If AI produces an error or causes harm, who is responsible?" These clauses should address: (1) Basic liability allocation principle: (a) "The User (Client) acknowledges that AI tools, including those provided by Vendor, operate on the basis of statistical pattern matching and may produce inaccurate, incomplete, or misleading outputs, including fabricated citations, incorrect analysis, and biased conclusions. User is responsible for verifying all AI-generated content before reliance and assumes responsibility for any errors in AI outputs unless caused by Vendor's gross negligence or willful misconduct.", (2) Vendor liability limitations: (a) Vendor's liability for AI errors: "Vendor shall not be liable for any indirect, incidental, consequential, special, or punitive damages arising from inaccuracy, incompleteness, bias, or other defect in AI outputs. Vendor's total liability for any claims related to AI performance shall not exceed fees paid in the 12 months preceding the claim. In no event shall Vendor be liable for inaccuracy in AI-generated content, as User has responsibility for verification.", (b) Exceptions to limitation: "The above limitations shall not apply to: (i) claims arising from Vendor's gross negligence, recklessness, or willful misconduct, (ii) claims arising from unauthorized access to or disclosure of User's data, (iii) claims arising from Vendor's violation of applicable data protection laws, or (iv) infringement of third-party intellectual property rights.", (3) User responsibility for AI verification: (a) "User assumes all responsibility for verifying AI outputs before reliance. User shall not rely on any AI-generated content (including citations, legal analysis, factual representations, or recommendations) without independent human review and verification against primary sources. User acknowledges that AI may hallucinate, misquote sources, or present inaccurate legal conclusions.", (4) Shared responsibility model: (a) "Liability for AI-generated content shall be allocated as follows: (i) If AI error is caused by inaccuracy in Vendor's training data, tools, or infrastructure → Vendor liable, (ii) If error is caused by User's failure to verify AI outputs → User liable, (iii) If error results from User inputting inaccurate information to AI → User liable, (iv) If error results from both parties' actions → liability apportioned based on comparative fault.", (5) Professional services context: (a) "Where User is a lawyer or other professional using AI in providing services to clients, User shall not represent to clients that AI outputs are independently verified or free from risk of error unless User has actually conducted such verification. User shall maintain professional liability insurance appropriate to their use of AI and shall indemnify Vendor for any claims arising from User's failure to verify AI outputs or disclosure of AI limitations to their clients.", (6) AI-specific representations and warranties: (a) "Vendor represents and warrants: (i) AI tool meets Vendor's documented specifications, (ii) AI tool does not knowingly infringe third-party IP, (iii) Vendor will use commercially reasonable efforts to minimize hallucinations and inaccuracies, but does not warrant error-free performance. Vendor makes no representation that AI outputs are accurate, complete, or suitable for any particular purpose.", (7) Limitation of liability notice: (a) "User acknowledges receipt of and agrees to Vendor's limitation of liability provisions. User has had opportunity to negotiate these terms and is not required to accept this contract. User is strongly encouraged to consult with their own counsel about liability allocation and insurance implications before using the AI tool in professional practice.", (8) Insurance requirements: (a) "User shall maintain professional liability insurance with minimum coverage of [amount] including coverage for AI-assisted work. User shall provide Vendor with evidence of insurance upon request. User shall not use the AI tool if User cannot obtain adequate insurance coverage.", (9) Claims procedure: (a) "If User believes AI output caused harm or resulted from Vendor error, User shall: (i) notify Vendor in writing within 30 days of discovering the error, (ii) provide detailed description of the error and harm alleged, (iii) cooperate in investigating the error, (iv) allow Vendor to remedy the error or provide corrected output. User's failure to timely notify Vendor shall reduce Vendor's liability for any damages.", (10) Exclusion of consequential damages: (a) "Neither party shall be liable to the other for lost profits, lost revenue, lost business opportunity, lost data, or other consequential, indirect, incidental, special, or punitive damages, even if advised of the possibility of such damages. This exclusion applies regardless of the legal theory (contract, tort, strict liability, or otherwise).", (11) Applicability to Singapore law: adapting to Singapore's Unfair Contract Terms Act and case law on liability exclusions: (a) "To the extent permitted by Singapore law, and recognizing that exclusions of liability for gross negligence, fraud, or willful misconduct are unenforceable, the above limitations shall apply except as prohibited by law. Singapore courts may limit or override these provisions if they are found to be unconscionable or grossly disproportionate to the risk.", (12) Insurance carve-out: (a) "Notwithstanding the above limitations, if either party's professional liability or cyber insurance covers a claim, such claim may be asserted and Vendor shall cooperate with insurance defense, with Vendor's liability capped at insurance coverage limits.", (13) Proportionality: (a) "The parties acknowledge that the above liability limitations are proportionate to: (i) the price of the AI tool (which is typically lower than bespoke professional services), (ii) User's ability to verify outputs before reliance, (iii) the availability of insurance to cover the risk, (iv) the inherent limitations of AI technology. These limitations would not be reasonable for a fully bespoke professional service, but are appropriate for an AI tool.", (14) Third-party beneficiary exclusion: (a) "Vendor's liability limitations apply only to direct users of the AI tool. Vendor has no liability to third parties (clients of User, courts, opposing parties) and User shall indemnify Vendor for any claims by third parties arising from User's use of the AI tool, except where such claims arise from Vendor's gross negligence or willful misconduct." Include commentary on enforceability in Singapore and sample risk allocation scenarios.

Context: Use in all AI vendor agreements and AI tool terms of service negotiation; critical for professional service firms

**PROMPT 35 — AI-Generated Intellectual Property Ownership Clauses**

Create comprehensive IP ownership clauses addressing the critical question: "Who owns work product and other intellectual property generated by AI?" These clauses should address: (1) Foundational principle: (a) "AI tools do not independently create copyrightable work. Copyright ownership in AI-generated content depends on the level of human creativity and direction involved. Where a lawyer uses an AI tool to generate content (contract language, legal analysis, research summaries), the lawyer/law firm typically owns the copyright in the output, provided the lawyer directed the AI and applied judgment in reviewing and editing the output.", (2) User ownership—standard provision: (a) "User retains all right, title, and interest in any work product, documents, analysis, code, or other tangible output that results from User's use of the AI tool ('User Works'). User Works are the exclusive property of User and shall be deemed a work made for hire for copyright purposes. User may use User Works for any lawful purpose without restriction.", (3) Vendor rights carve-out: (a) "Vendor retains the right to: (i) use anonymized and de-identified User Works to improve the AI tool and train future versions, provided all personally identifiable information and confidential information is removed, (ii) use User feedback on the AI tool to improve performance, (iii) publish aggregate statistics about AI tool performance without reference to specific User Works. Vendor shall not use any User Work containing confidential information without prior written consent.", (4) Grant-back license: (a) "User grants Vendor a limited, non-exclusive, royalty-free license to use User Works solely for: (i) providing the AI tool service, (ii) improving the AI tool (model training), (iii) quality assurance and debugging. This license is limited to Vendor's own use and does not extend to Vendor's other clients or third parties.", (5) Client ownership in lawyer context: (a) "Where User is a lawyer using the AI tool to provide services to clients, User shall ensure that engagement letters and client agreements specify that: (i) all work product created for the client (including AI-assisted work) is owned by or belongs to the client, (ii) the client has the exclusive right to use, modify, and disclose the work product, (iii) the client owns any improvements or derivative works created from the initial work product. Vendor shall have no ownership or licensing rights in client-created work.", (6) Pre-existing IP: (a) "Nothing in this Agreement transfers ownership of any pre-existing intellectual property owned by either party before this Agreement. If Vendor's AI tool uses Vendor's pre-existing patents, copyrights, or trade secrets, Vendor retains all rights in such pre-existing IP. User's use of the AI tool includes only a limited license to use Vendor's IP tools, not ownership.", (7) Third-party IP concerns: (a) "If AI outputs contain material copied from or infringing third-party copyrighted material ('Third-Party IP'), User shall: (i) immediately cease using that output, (ii) notify Vendor, (iii) delete the infringing content. Vendor shall indemnify User for claims that the AI tool's operation or the model it was trained on infringes third-party IP, except where infringement results from User's modification of the output or use beyond Vendor's intended scope.", (8) Patent ownership: (a) "If User invents a patentable invention using the AI tool, User shall own any patent rights, provided the invention is not anticipated by the AI tool's training data or Vendor's pre-existing intellectual property. User shall not assert any patent rights against Vendor or Vendor's use of the AI tool in developing future versions.", (9) Trademarks and brand: (a) "User shall not use Vendor's trademarks, logos, or brand names in User Works without Vendor's prior written consent. User may state that work was created with the aid of Vendor's AI tool if User provides accurate attribution (e.g., 'This document was drafted with assistance from [AI Tool Name]').", (10) Work-for-hire considerations: (a) "If User is hiring another party to use the AI tool to create work product for User (e.g., User hires a contractor to draft documents using AI), the parties should execute a separate work-for-hire agreement specifying that all AI-generated work product is owned by User. Vendor makes no claims to work created by its tool regardless of whether the immediate user is an employee, contractor, or other third party.", (11) Derivative works: (a) "User owns the right to create derivative works from User Works, including: (i) modified versions of AI-generated content, (ii) combinations of AI-generated content with other materials, (iii) translations or adaptations of AI-generated content. Vendor retains no rights in derivative works created by User.", (12) Attribution: (a) "User is not required to attribute or credit the AI tool in any User Works or to disclose AI use when creating derivatives. However, if User chooses to disclose AI use (for professional ethics, client relations, or transparency purposes), User may state 'This work was created with assistance from [AI Tool Name]' and Vendor shall not object or claim that this damages Vendor's reputation.", (13) Singapore copyright law compliance: (a) "This Agreement is governed by Singapore copyright law, which provides copyright protection for original works of authorship fixed in tangible media. AI-generated content may or may not qualify for copyright protection depending on the level of human creativity involved. The parties agree that: (i) User Works created through significant human direction and editing will be treated as User's original work, (ii) purely AI-generated content with minimal human involvement may not be copyrightable, but User retains any ownership rights that may exist.", (14) Prohibition on copyright claims: (a) "Vendor shall not claim copyright ownership in any User Works or assert copyright rights against User's use of User Works. Vendor waives any moral rights or attribution rights in AI outputs." Include examples of IP ownership in different scenarios (lawyer using AI, software developer using AI, author using AI).

**PROMPT 36 — AI Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) Agreement Terms**

Create a template AI SaaS agreement addressing the specific terms relevant to law firms and legal departments licensing AI tools on a subscription basis. The agreement should include: (1) Preamble and definitions: (a) "This AI Software-as-a-Service Agreement ('Agreement') governs the licensing and use of [Vendor's AI Tool] ('Service') by [Client/Law Firm] ('Customer'). The Service is provided on a subscription basis and Customer shall comply with all terms herein.", (b) Defining key terms: "Service" = the AI tool and all associated features, "Subscription" = Customer's license to use the Service for the subscription period, "Content" = data or information Customer inputs into the Service, "Output" = results generated by the Service, (2) Subscription and license grant: (a) "Vendor grants Customer a non-exclusive, non-transferable license to use the Service during the subscription period upon payment of subscription fees. The license is limited to: (i) Customer's internal use only (no resale or redistribution), (ii) the number of users specified in the subscription (e.g., 5-50 user accounts), (iii) the features specified in the subscription plan (e.g., 'Professional' vs. 'Enterprise').", (b) Subscription term: "Initial subscription term: [1 year / other]. Renewal: automatically renews for successive [annual/monthly] terms unless either party provides 60 days' notice of non-renewal. Termination: either party may terminate with 60 days' written notice.", (3) Fees and payment: (a) "Subscription fee: $[amount] per [month/year], payable in advance. Payment method: [monthly billing / annual prepayment]. Late payment: if Customer's payment is 15 days late, Vendor may suspend Customer's access to the Service until payment is received.", (b) "Price increases: Vendor may increase subscription fees upon 60 days' notice. If Customer objects, Customer may terminate with 30 days' notice at no penalty (except payment for the current period).", (c) "Usage-based fees (if applicable): If the Service charges based on usage (e.g., per query, per document analyzed), Vendor shall provide an estimate of potential overage fees. Customer may set usage alerts/limits to control costs.", (4) Service level agreement (SLA): (a) "Vendor shall maintain [99.9%] uptime of the Service, excluding planned maintenance. If actual uptime is below [99.5%] in any month, Customer shall receive a service credit equal to [1-5%] of the monthly subscription fee for that month.", (b) "Planned maintenance: Vendor may schedule up to [4 hours] of maintenance per month, with at least [7 days'] notice to Customer, outside of business hours when possible.", (c) "Support: Vendor shall provide technical support during [business hours / 24/7] via [email / phone / portal]. Vendor's response time target is [4 hours] for critical issues, [24 hours] for non-critical.", (5) Data and security: (a) "Customer retains ownership of all Content input into the Service. Vendor shall: (i) keep Content confidential and not disclose to third parties, (ii) use commercially reasonable security measures to protect Content, (iii) not use Content to train or improve the AI tool without Customer's separate written consent, (iv) comply with Data Processing Addendum ('DPA') attached hereto, (v) encrypt all data in transit and at rest, (vi) maintain audit logs of data access.", (b) "Data retention: Vendor shall retain Content only for the duration necessary to provide the Service and shall delete upon Service termination. Customer may request deletion of specific Content at any time and Vendor shall delete within [30] days.", (6) AI output and limitations: (a) "Customer acknowledges that the Service may generate inaccurate, incomplete, biased, or misleading outputs. Customer assumes responsibility for verifying all outputs before reliance. Vendor does not warrant that outputs are accurate, complete, suitable for any particular purpose, or free from error.", (b) "Hallucination risk: Customer acknowledges the Service may 'hallucinate' by generating false citations, fabricated facts, or incorrect analysis. This is a known limitation of AI technology and Customer shall verify all critical content.", (c) "Bias: Customer acknowledges the Service may exhibit bias based on its training data, which may reflect societal biases. Customer is responsible for identifying and correcting biased outputs.", (7) Intellectual property: (a) "Output ownership: Customer retains all intellectual property rights in Outputs generated by the Service. Vendor shall not claim ownership or licensing rights in Outputs. Customer may use Outputs for any lawful purpose without restriction.", (b) "Vendor IP: Vendor retains all rights in the Service software, algorithms, models, and other Vendor intellectual property. Customer's license is limited to using the Service; Customer shall not reverse-engineer, decompile, modify, or attempt to access Vendor's underlying models.", (c) "Third-party IP: If any Output infringes third-party intellectual property, the party liable depends on the cause: if infringement is due to Vendor's training data or tools, Vendor shall indemnify Customer. If infringement is due to Customer's input or use, Customer shall indemnify Vendor.", (8) Confidentiality: (a) "Each party shall maintain the other's confidential information in strict confidence and shall not disclose without the other's consent, except as required by law. Vendor's confidential information includes the AI algorithms and training methodologies. Customer's confidential information includes all Content input.", (b) "Compelled disclosure: if either party is compelled by law (subpoena, court order) to disclose the other's confidential information, the compelled party shall: (i) promptly notify the other party, (ii) cooperate with the other party's efforts to seek protective orders, (iii) disclose only the minimum required by law.", (9) Warranties and disclaimers: (a) "Limited warranty: Vendor warrants that (i) the Service will materially conform to Vendor's written specifications, (ii) the Service does not knowingly infringe third-party IP (except where infringement results from Customer's modifications), (iii) Vendor will provide the Service in a professional manner.", (b) "Disclaimer: EXCEPT AS EXPRESSLY PROVIDED ABOVE, VENDOR DISCLAIMS ALL OTHER WARRANTIES, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED, INCLUDING MERCHANTABILITY, FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE, NON-INFRINGEMENT, AND TITLE. VENDOR DOES NOT WARRANT THAT THE SERVICE WILL BE ERROR-FREE, UNINTERRUPTED, SECURE, OR SUITABLE FOR CUSTOMER'S SPECIFIC USE.", (10) Limitation of liability: (a) "Vendor's total liability for any claims related to the Service shall be limited to the fees Customer paid in the 12 months preceding the claim. This cap does not apply to claims arising from Vendor's gross negligence, fraud, willful misconduct, or unauthorized disclosure of Customer's data.", (b) "Neither party shall be liable for indirect, incidental, consequential, or punitive damages (including lost profits, lost revenue, lost business opportunity), even if advised of the possibility of such damages.", (11) Indemnification: (a) "Vendor indemnifies Customer against third-party claims that the Service infringes any intellectual property right, subject to the liability cap above.", (b) "Customer indemnifies Vendor against third-party claims arising from Customer's input into the Service, Customer's use of Outputs, or Customer's violation of this Agreement.", (c) "Indemnification procedure: (i) The indemnified party shall promptly notify the indemnifying party of the claim, (ii) the indemnifying party shall assume defense and control of the claim, (iii) the indemnified party shall cooperate reasonably in defense.", (12) Term and termination: (a) "This Agreement continues for the subscription term and renews automatically unless either party provides 60 days' non-renewal notice.", (b) "Termination for cause: Either party may terminate immediately if the other materially breaches and fails to cure within 30 days of written notice.", (c) "Termination for convenience: Either party may terminate without cause with 90 days' written notice. Upon termination, Customer loses access to the Service and Vendor may delete all Content after [30] days.", (d) "Survival: Sections on Confidentiality, IP Ownership, Limitation of Liability, and Indemnification survive termination.", (13) Amendments and modifications: (a) "Vendor may amend this Agreement upon 60 days' notice. **PROMPT 37 — AI Disclosure and Transparency Requirements in Contracts**

Create contract clauses requiring disclosure and transparency regarding AI use, ensuring all parties understand when and how AI is being used. These clauses should address: (1) Disclosure obligation—general: (a) "Party A shall disclose to Party B in writing if AI tools or systems are used in creating, analyzing, or producing any deliverables or work product provided under this Agreement. Disclosure shall be made in the engagement letter, engagement email, or a separate disclosure statement before delivery of the work product.", (2) Timing of disclosure: (a) "Disclosure of AI use shall be made: (i) in the engagement letter for matters where AI will or may be used, (ii) within 24 hours of discovery if AI was used but not initially disclosed, (iii) in each piece of deliverable work product if AI contributed significantly (e.g., 'This analysis was generated with AI assistance').", (b) "For ongoing matters, disclosure shall be made initially and no further notice required unless AI use increases substantially or new AI tools are introduced.", (3) Content of disclosure: (a) "Disclosure shall include: (i) the name and type of AI tool(s) used (e.g., 'ChatGPT-4, a large language model'), (ii) what aspects of the work were AI-assisted (e.g., 'initial legal research,' 'contract clause drafting,' 'document review'), (iii) confirmation that a qualified human reviewed all AI outputs, (iv) representation that the work meets professional standards regardless of AI use, (v) notification of any known limitations of the AI tool relevant to this engagement, (vi) confirmation that confidentiality protections apply equally to AI-assisted work.", (4) Level of disclosure detail: (a) "Disclosure need not disclose: (i) detailed technical details of how the AI tool works, (ii) the AI tool's training data or methods, (iii) the specific prompts given to the AI, (iv) the exact percentage of work that was AI-assisted vs. human-generated. Such detail may be proprietary or irrelevant.", (b) "Disclosure should be understandable to the recipient (e.g., lay clients, not just sophisticated legal parties) and should address the recipient's likely concerns about accuracy, confidentiality, and quality.", (5) Consent provisions: (a) "If this Agreement requires Party B's consent to AI use, consent may be evidenced by: (i) written agreement to the engagement letter containing AI disclosure, (ii) email acknowledgment of AI disclosure, (iii) implied consent if Party B is sent the AI disclosure and does not object within 5 business days, (iv) standard disclosure language in all engagements where Party B is informed AI may be used and does not opt out.", (b) "Party B's consent is not required if: (i) AI use does not materially affect the cost or timeline of the engagement, (ii) AI use relates only to administrative or internal work (not work product delivered to Party B), (iii) Party B is operating in a sophisticated business context where AI is standard industry practice.", (6) For litigation and court filings: (a) "If work product will be filed in court, Party A shall disclose AI use to the court and opposing counsel if: (i) required by court rules (check applicable jurisdiction's disclosure requirements), (ii) material to the work product's reliability (if a brief relies on AI legal research, disclosure may be prudent), (iii) requested by the court or opposing counsel, (iv) required by professional conduct rules (Law Society rules).", (b) "Disclosure to court: 'This memorandum was prepared with assistance from AI legal research tools (ChatGPT-4). All legal citations have been verified against primary sources and this analysis reflects the author's independent professional judgment.'", (7) For client deliverables: (a) "If Party A is providing advice or work product to clients, disclosure of AI use shall: (i) not undermine client confidence if done appropriately ('This analysis was generated with AI tools, which we use to improve efficiency while maintaining quality'), (ii) be made in a way that allows clients to choose whether to pay less for AI-assisted work or more for purely manual work, (iii) include reassurance about quality and verification, (iv) address client data protection concerns (especially for confidential/sensitive information).", (8) For multi-party transactions: (a) "If Party A is acting as intermediary or advisor to multiple parties, AI disclosure shall be made to all affected parties. If different parties have different requirements or concerns about AI use, Party A shall comply with the most stringent requirements or obtain separate consent from each party.", (9) Regulatory disclosure: (a) "If Party B is a regulator or authority (such as Singapore's Ministry of Law), Party A may be required to disclose AI use in regulatory filings or submissions. If so, this disclosure obligation shall be included in the Agreement.", (10) Non-disclosure carve-out: (a) "Notwithstanding the above disclosure requirements, if AI use is subject to Party A's attorney-client privilege or work product protection, disclosure may be limited to avoid waiving privilege. In such cases, Party A shall disclose the fact of AI use but may withhold technical details about the AI analysis itself.", (11) Updates and amendments: (a) "If Party A changes its AI tools or practices during the engagement, Party A shall notify Party B of material changes. If the new tools present material risks not present with the original tools (e.g., switching to a vendor with weaker data protection), Party A shall obtain renewed consent.", (12) Third-party beneficiary: (a) "The disclosure requirements herein are for the benefit of Party B. Third parties (Party B's clients, opposing parties in litigation, courts) may benefit from the transparency but are not intended third-party beneficiaries entitled to enforce this provision.", (13) Singapore professional conduct alignment: (a) "This disclosure obligation is consistent with Singapore Law Society's professional conduct expectations that lawyers be transparent with clients and courts about material aspects of their services and work product." Include sample disclosure language for different contexts (engagement letters, client memos, court filings, transactional documents).

## SECTION 2: AI Data & Operational Clauses

**PROMPT 38 — AI Data Processing & Protection Clauses**

Create comprehensive data protection and processing clauses for AI-related agreements, addressing how personal data and confidential information are handled when using AI systems. These clauses should include: (1) Data classification and scope: (a) "For purposes of this Agreement, 'Customer Data' means all data, information, and content that Customer inputs into or provides to the Service, including: (i) case facts, legal documents, contract terms, (ii) client information, third-party personal data, (iii) business information, financial data, strategic information. Customer Data is Customer's property and Vendor shall handle it as confidential information.", (b) "'Personal Data' means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person, as defined by PDPA. If Customer Data contains Personal Data, additional data protection obligations apply.", (2) Data input and processing: (a) "Customer shall be responsible for: (i) ensuring Customer Data does not contain confidential information of third parties without authorization, (ii) redacting or anonymizing sensitive personal information before input where possible, (iii) obtaining necessary consents from data subjects if Customer Data contains their personal information, (iv) complying with all applicable data protection laws in collecting and providing Customer Data.", (b) "Vendor shall process Customer Data solely: (i) to provide the Service as requested by Customer, (ii) as necessary for technical support and troubleshooting, (iii) to improve the Service performance (only with Customer's separate opt-in consent), (iv) to comply with legal obligations. Vendor shall not process Customer Data for any other purpose.", (3) Data retention and deletion: (a) "Vendor shall retain Customer Data only for the period necessary to provide the Service, typically [30-90] days after the customer session ends, unless Customer requests longer retention for audit purposes. Upon Service termination or Customer's request, Vendor shall delete all Customer Data within [30] days and provide confirmation of deletion.", (b) "Copies and backups: Vendor may retain copies of Customer Data in backup systems for business continuity purposes, but shall delete backups within [90] days after Customer Data's initial deletion. Vendor shall provide evidence of backup deletion upon request.", (c) "Exceptions: Vendor may retain Customer Data longer if: (i) required by law or legal process (subpoena, court order), (ii) necessary for security or fraud investigation, (iii) required for audit or compliance purposes. Vendor shall notify Customer of extended retention.", (4) Data security obligations: (a) "Vendor shall implement and maintain technical and organizational security measures appropriate to the sensitivity of Customer Data, including: (i) encryption of all data in transit (SSL/TLS) and at rest (AES-256 or equivalent), (ii) access controls limiting who in Vendor's organization can access Customer Data, (iii) multi-factor authentication for access, (iv) regular security audits and penetration testing, (v) incident response procedures for data breaches, (vi) employee confidentiality agreements, (vii) secure disposal of hardware containing Customer Data.", (b) "Security standards: Vendor shall maintain security measures consistent with industry standards (ISO 27001 or SOC 2 Type II certification preferred) and applicable legal requirements.", (5) Data breach notification: (a) "If Vendor becomes aware of any unauthorized access to, disclosure of, or loss of Customer Data, Vendor shall: (i) immediately notify Customer in writing, (ii) provide details of the breach (what data, when, how many persons affected), (iii) describe any forensic investigation findings, (iv) explain remediation steps taken and steps Customer should take, (v) assist Customer in complying with any legal notification obligations (PDPA, etc.).", (b) "Notification timing: Vendor shall notify Customer without unreasonable delay, preferably within [24-48] hours of discovering the breach, or as soon as technically feasible. Vendor shall not delay notification to complete investigation.", (6) Personal Data protection—PDPA compliance: (a) "If Customer Data contains Personal Data as defined by Singapore's Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA), Vendor shall: (i) process Personal Data only as a 'data processor' with Customer as 'data controller', (ii) comply with all PDPA obligations including legitimate purpose, consent, accuracy, protection, retention, transfer restrictions, and data subject rights, (iii) sign a Data Processing Addendum (DPA) separate from this Agreement, setting out detailed PDPA compliance obligations.", (b) "Data subject rights: Vendor shall assist Customer in fulfilling data subject rights under PDPA: (i) access requests (providing data about a specific person), (ii) correction requests (correcting inaccurate data), (iii) deletion requests (deleting data per right to be forgotten), (iv) portability requests (transferring data to another system). Vendor shall respond to Customer's requests within [30] days.", (7) Cross-border data transfer: (a) "Customer Data may be processed and stored on Vendor's servers located in [specify regions, e.g., 'Singapore, Australia, US']. If Customer Data contains Personal Data subject to PDPA, it shall not be transferred outside Singapore or [approved regions] without Customer's prior written consent, unless: (i) the recipient country has been deemed to have adequate data protection by Singapore authorities, (ii) appropriate safeguards such as Standard Contractual Clauses are in place, (iii) Vendor is processing data as Customer's sub-processor and Customer has authorized the transfer.", (b) "Data localization: If Customer requires data to be stored only in Singapore, Vendor shall maintain data on Singapore servers with backup only in Singapore, at no additional cost [or additional cost of $X per month].", (8) Sub-processors: (a) "Vendor may use sub-processors (third-party vendors providing infrastructure, security, or support services) to process Customer Data, but only: (i) after disclosing all sub-processors to Customer, (ii) ensuring each sub-processor executes a data processing agreement with equivalent protections, (iii) notifying Customer before adding any new sub-processor, and allowing Customer [15] days to object to new sub-processors.
