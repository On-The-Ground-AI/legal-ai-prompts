# Microsoft Copilot Quick Reference for Legal Teams
### How to Use AI in Word, Outlook, Teams & Excel for Legal Work

---

> This guide is specifically for teams using **Microsoft Copilot** — the AI built into Microsoft 365 (Word, Outlook, Teams, Excel, and Copilot Chat). It explains where to type prompts, how Copilot differs from standalone AI tools, and quick tips for each app.
>
> **Data security note:** Microsoft 365 Copilot with your organisation's tenancy keeps your data within your organisation's Microsoft environment. It does not train on your data. Always confirm your organisation's Copilot data settings with your IT team before using it for client or sensitive work.

---

## Where to Type Your Prompt in Each App

| App | Where to find Copilot | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| **Word** | Click the Copilot icon in the Home ribbon, or press Alt+I | Drafts, rewrites, summarises, and edits your document |
| **Outlook** | Click "Draft with Copilot" when composing a new email | Drafts emails; can summarise long email threads |
| **Teams** | Click the Copilot icon in a chat or meeting | Summarises meetings, chats, and generates follow-ups |
| **Excel** | Click the Copilot icon in the Home ribbon | Analyses data, creates formulas, builds charts |
| **Copilot Chat** (copilot.microsoft.com) | Type directly in the chat box | General research, drafting, and question-answering |

## Word: The Most Useful Copilot Features for Lawyers

**Draft from scratch**
Click the Copilot icon → "Draft with Copilot" → paste your prompt → press Generate.
Best for: First draft of letters, memos, summaries, simple agreements.

**Rewrite / improve existing text**
Highlight the text you want to improve → click the Copilot icon → choose "Rewrite" → Copilot suggests alternatives.
Best for: Improving client-facing letters, making dense legal text clearer, adjusting tone.

**Summarise a long document**
Open the document → click the Copilot icon → type: "Summarise this document in bullet points" or "What are the key obligations in this contract?"
Best for: Getting a quick overview of a long contract, judgment, or policy document before reading in full.

**Ask a question about the document**
With your document open, ask Copilot: "What is the termination clause?" or "Does this contract mention data protection?" Copilot will search the document and answer.
Best for: Finding specific clauses quickly in a long document.

## Outlook: The Most Useful Copilot Features for Lawyers

**Draft an email from scratch**
When composing: click "Draft with Copilot" → type a short description of what the email should say → press Generate.
Example: "Draft a professional email to a client updating them that their contract has been signed and will be sent to them today."

**Summarise a long email thread**
Open a long email thread → click the Copilot icon → choose "Summary by Copilot".
Best for: Coming back to an email thread after a period away, or briefing a colleague on a client matter.

**Make it shorter / more formal / more casual**
After Copilot drafts an email: click "Regenerate" → choose length and tone options.
Best for: Adjusting formal legal correspondence to be more accessible to clients.

## Teams: The Most Useful Copilot Features for Lawyers

**Meeting summary**
During or after a Teams meeting with transcription enabled: click the Copilot icon → "Recap the key points of this meeting" or "List the action items from this meeting."
Best for: Post-meeting follow-up, producing meeting minutes, capturing action items.

**Search across chats**
In Teams Copilot Chat: ask "What did [NAME] say about [TOPIC] in our recent chats?" Copilot will search your Teams history.
Best for: Finding information shared earlier in a project without scrolling through messages.

## Excel: The Most Useful Copilot Features for Legal Operations

**Analyse a contract register**
With your contract register open: ask Copilot "Show me all contracts expiring in the next 90 days" or "Which contracts have no renewal notice date?"
Best for: Contract management and deadline monitoring.

**Create a formula**
Ask Copilot: "Write a formula to highlight any row where the contract value is over $50,000 and the expiry date is before today."
Best for: Building contract trackers and risk dashboards.

**Summarise data**
Ask Copilot: "Summarise this sheet and tell me the total contract value by vendor type."
Best for: Producing a quick legal spend or contract summary for management.

## Copilot Chat: Best Practices for Legal Research Support

Copilot Chat (copilot.microsoft.com) is the most flexible Copilot tool. It works like a general AI assistant but within Microsoft's data environment.

**What it is good at:**
- Explaining legal concepts in plain English
- Summarising legal frameworks
- Drafting letters, memos, and policies
- Generating checklists and templates
- Comparing options or scenarios

**What it cannot do:**
- Access real-time case law databases (LawNet, Westlaw, LexisNexis)
- Give jurisdiction-specific advice with certainty (always verify)
- Access documents stored outside your Microsoft 365 environment (unless integrated)

**The most effective prompt structure for Copilot Chat:**

```
Role: "Act as a [senior corporate lawyer / compliance officer / legal secretary]"
Context: "I am working on [describe the matter briefly]"
Task: "Please [describe exactly what you want — draft / summarise / analyse / create a checklist]"
Format: "Present the output as [a table / bullet points / a formal letter / a numbered list]"
Constraint: "Keep it under [word count / page count]. Use plain English."
```

## The 10 Most Useful Single-Line Copilot Prompts for Legal Teams

1. "Summarise this contract in 200 words, highlighting any unusual clauses."
2. "Draft a polite but firm follow-up email to a counterparty who has not responded."
3. "Create a compliance checklist for [TOPIC] under [JURISDICTION] law."
4. "Rewrite this clause in plain English that a non-lawyer can understand."
5. "List all the obligations my client has under this contract."
6. "What are the top 5 risks in this agreement for the [BUYER / SELLER / BORROWER]?"
7. "Draft meeting minutes based on the following notes: [PASTE NOTES]."
8. "Compare these two contract versions and list all material differences."
9. "Generate 10 interview questions for a workplace investigation about [TOPIC]."
10. "Write a board update explaining the following legal risk in non-technical language: [DESCRIBE]."

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Don't paste confidential client names into public AI tools.** If using Copilot outside your firm's Microsoft tenancy, anonymise all client details first.

**Don't trust AI-generated case citations.** Always verify any case name, citation, or statutory reference on an official database before using it in a document.

**Don't use AI output without reviewing it.** AI can produce plausible-sounding but incorrect legal statements. Every output needs a human check.

**Don't ask one huge question.** Break complex tasks into steps. Ask for the framework first, then the detail. This produces better results than one overwhelming prompt.

**Don't forget to specify the jurisdiction.** AI defaults to US/UK law if you don't specify. Always add "under Singapore law" or "[JURISDICTION] law" to every legal research prompt.
