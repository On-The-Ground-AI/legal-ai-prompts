## Architecture

This library is organised by **practice area**, then by **task type** within each area. Every prompt follows a consistent format designed for easy copy-paste into any AI tool.

### Prompt Format Standard

Every prompt in this library follows this structure:

```
[PROMPT ID] — [DESCRIPTIVE TITLE]

[The full prompt text, ready to copy and paste]

Context: [When to use this prompt]
Difficulty: [Beginner / Intermediate / Advanced]
Best AI tool: [Copilot / Claude / ChatGPT / Any]
Follow-up: [Suggested next prompt to chain with]
```

### Difficulty Levels

- **Beginner**: Simple copy-paste. One-shot prompt. No chaining needed.
- **Intermediate**: May require customisation of multiple placeholders. Better results with follow-up prompts.
- **Advanced**: Multi-step workflow. Requires prompt chaining, document uploading, or iterative refinement. Often uses system prompts or personas.

## How to Use This Library

### For Individual Lawyers
1. Find your practice area in the index above.
2. Open the relevant file.
3. Find the prompt that matches your task.
4. Copy the prompt into your AI tool (Copilot, Claude, ChatGPT, etc.).
5. Replace all [BRACKETED PLACEHOLDERS] with your details.
6. Review the output carefully before using it professionally.

### For Law Firms & Legal Departments
1. Download the full library.
2. Customise the [JURISDICTION] placeholders to your firm's primary jurisdiction.
3. Add your firm's standard positions to the contract review prompts.
4. Create a shared prompt library on your intranet or knowledge management system.
5. Train your team on the Advanced Techniques guide (V1_02).
6. Build firm-specific prompt chains using the Workflows guide (V1_03).

### For Legal Technologists
1. Use the Master Index to map prompts to your legal technology stack.
2. The prompt format is designed for integration into CLM tools, matter management systems, and AI orchestration platforms.
3. Each prompt ID is unique and can be used as a reference key in your systems.

---

> ⚠ **Important Disclaimers**
>
> **Not legal advice.** These prompts are tools, not advice. Every AI output must be reviewed by a qualified lawyer before professional use. AI can hallucinate, miss jurisdiction-specific requirements, and produce confidently wrong answers.
>
> **Data security.** Never paste confidential client information into public AI tools. Use enterprise AI tools with data protection or anonymise all details first.
>
> **Verify everything.** AI cannot access live legal databases. Always verify case citations, statutory references, and regulatory requirements on official sources.
>
> **Singapore law note.** For Singapore-specific prompts, always check current statutes on sso.agc.gov.sg and current case law on lawnet.sg.
