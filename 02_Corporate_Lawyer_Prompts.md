## Section A: Company Formation & Governance

**PROMPT C-A1 — Company Incorporation Checklist**
Act as a corporate lawyer in [JURISDICTION]. A client wants to incorporate a new [PRIVATE LIMITED / PUBLIC LISTED / LLP / OTHER] company for the following business: [DESCRIBE THE BUSINESS]. Generate a comprehensive incorporation checklist covering: 1. Steps required to incorporate the entity with [REGISTRAR / ACRA / COMPANIES HOUSE — specify]. 2. Information and documents needed (shareholders, directors, constitution, registered office, etc.). 3. Post-incorporation steps (bank account, licences, regulatory approvals, tax registrations). 4. Key decisions the client must make before incorporation (share structure, director appointment, constitution vs. model constitution). 5. Estimated timeline and fees. Flag any industry-specific licences required for [BUSINESS TYPE].

*Copilot Tip: Paste this into Copilot in Word to generate an editable checklist table. Then use "Add a column for 'Responsible Party'" to customise it.*

**PROMPT C-A2 — Constitution / Articles of Association Review**
Act as a corporate governance lawyer. Review the following company constitution / articles of association and: 1. Summarise the key provisions in a table: [Clause | Topic | Summary | Notable Features]. 2. Identify any provisions that deviate from the model constitution for [JURISDICTION]. 3. Flag any provisions that may create problems for the company or its shareholders (e.g. quorum requirements, director appointment rights, drag-along/tag-along provisions). 4. Note any clauses that may need updating to reflect current law or the company's stage of development. [PASTE CONSTITUTION TEXT OR KEY CLAUSES]

**PROMPT C-A3 — Board Resolution Drafting**
Draft a set of board resolutions for [COMPANY NAME] approving the following matters at a board meeting on [DATE]: [LIST THE MATTERS TO BE RESOLVED — e.g. approval of financial statements, appointment of auditors, authorisation of a bank account, approval of a transaction, allotment of shares]. The resolutions should: follow standard corporate drafting conventions for [JURISDICTION], include any required statutory recitals, use the proper authorisation language, and be suitable for signing as a written resolution or recording in board minutes.

**PROMPT C-A4 — Shareholders' Resolution**
Draft a shareholders' ordinary / special resolution for [COMPANY NAME] to approve the following: [DESCRIBE — e.g. alteration of constitution, change of company name, increase of share capital, approval of related party transaction]. The resolution should: state whether it is an ordinary or special resolution and why, include the precise statutory authority relied upon, use standard drafting, and note any notice period or quorum requirements that apply under [JURISDICTION] law.

---

**PROMPT C-A5 — Director's Duties Memo**
Draft a memo to a newly appointed director of [COMPANY NAME] explaining their legal duties under [JURISDICTION] company law. Cover: 1. The key statutory and common law duties (duty to act in good faith, duty of care and skill, duty to avoid conflicts, duty not to accept benefits from third parties, duty to declare interests in transactions). 2. What these duties mean in practice with concrete examples. 3. The consequences of breaching these duties (personal liability, disqualification). 4. Best practices for board meetings and record-keeping. Write in plain English, suitable for a non-lawyer director.

## Section B: Transactions & M&A

**PROMPT C-B1 — Term Sheet Review**
Act as an M&A lawyer. Review the following term sheet and: 1. Summarise the proposed transaction structure. 2. Identify the key commercial terms (price, consideration, conditions, timeline). 3. Flag any terms that are unusual, one-sided, or particularly favourable / unfavourable to my client [BUYER / SELLER / INVESTOR]. 4. Identify any key points for negotiation. 5. List the next steps and documents that will follow from this term sheet. [PASTE TERM SHEET TEXT]

---

**PROMPT C-B2 — Due Diligence Report Summary**
Act as a transactional lawyer. I am conducting legal due diligence on [TARGET COMPANY NAME] in connection with a proposed [ACQUISITION / INVESTMENT / MERGER]. Based on the following information gathered during due diligence: [SUMMARISE KEY FINDINGS OR PASTE DD CHECKLIST RESPONSES]. Produce a due diligence summary report covering: 1. Corporate structure and good standing. 2. Key contracts — material agreements, change of control provisions, consents required. 3. Intellectual property — ownership, any disputes or encumbrances. 4. Employment — key employee risks, pending disputes. 5. Litigation — any pending or threatened claims. 6. Regulatory — licences, permits, compliance history. For each area: RAG status (Red / Amber / Green) and key risks requiring attention.

---

**PROMPT C-B3 — Share Purchase Agreement — Key Clauses Checklist**
Act as an M&A lawyer. I am advising the [BUYER / SELLER] in a share purchase transaction for [COMPANY NAME]. The purchase price is [AMOUNT]. Draft a checklist of the key clauses I must review and negotiate in the Share Purchase Agreement, covering: (1) representations and warranties — key reps and any gaps, (2) disclosure schedule — adequacy of disclosures, (3) conditions precedent, (4) completion mechanics, (5) locked-box vs. completion accounts, (6) warranty and indemnity provisions, (7) MAC clause, (8) restrictive covenants on the seller, (9) W&I insurance considerations, (10) governing law and dispute resolution. For each clause, state: standard market position and my client's preferred position.

**PROMPT C-B4 — Representations & Warranties — Red Flags**

Flag any standard warranties that are missing. 3. Identify any warranties that are likely to be heavily negotiated and suggest compromise positions. 4. Note any warranties that require disclosure against the disclosure letter. [PASTE THE WARRANTIES SECTION]

**PROMPT C-B5 — Post-Acquisition Integration Checklist**
Draft a legal post-acquisition integration checklist for the acquisition of [TARGET COMPANY] by [ACQUIRING COMPANY]. The acquisition completed on [DATE]. The checklist should cover: 1. Corporate housekeeping — board changes, statutory filings, registered office updates. 2. Contract novation and assignment — identifying contracts requiring third-party consent. 3. Intellectual property — IP assignment and registration updates. 4. Employment — TUPE / employment transfer obligations (if applicable), HR integration. 5. Banking and finance — account changes, security registrations. 6. Insurance — review and update policies. 7. Regulatory — notify regulators, update licences. ## Section C: Shareholder Agreements & Joint Ventures

**PROMPT C-C1 — Shareholders' Agreement — Key Terms**
Act as a corporate lawyer. I am advising a [MAJORITY / MINORITY] shareholder in a new joint venture / company. Draft the key provisions that should be included in a shareholders' agreement for [COMPANY NAME], a [JURISDICTION]-incorporated company. The shareholders are: [NAME/PERCENTAGE each]. Cover: 1. Board composition and voting rights. 2. Reserved matters requiring shareholder consent. 3. Transfer restrictions (pre-emption rights, right of first refusal, drag-along, tag-along). 4. Dividend policy. 5. Deadlock provisions. 6. Exit mechanisms. 7. Non-compete and non-solicitation obligations. For each provision, state what a fair and balanced drafting would look like for both majority and minority shareholders.

---

**PROMPT C-C2 — Deadlock Resolution Mechanism**
My client is a 50/50 shareholder in a joint venture company. The shareholders have reached a deadlock on [DESCRIBE THE DISPUTED MATTER]. Draft: 1. An analysis of the deadlock resolution mechanisms typically used in joint venture agreements (shotgun/Russian roulette, put/call options, mediation escalation, third-party determination). 2. A recommendation on which mechanism is most suitable for my client's situation, with reasons. 3. Draft clauses for the recommended mechanism suitable for inclusion in the shareholders' agreement.

## Section D: Financing & Security

Key conditions precedent to drawdown. 4. Financial covenants — list each covenant and the test. 5. Representations and warranties — any unusual ones. 6. Events of default — list each trigger. 7. Security package. 8. Any unusual or particularly onerous provisions from the borrower's perspective. [PASTE LOAN AGREEMENT OR KEY TERMS]

Security to be taken: [LIST — e.g. fixed and floating charge over company assets, mortgage over property at [ADDRESS], share charge over [SHARES IN SUBSIDIARY], personal guarantee from [NAME]]. ## Section E: Regulatory & Licensing

**PROMPT C-E1 — Regulatory Approval Mapping**
Act as a regulatory lawyer in [JURISDICTION]. My client is proposing the following transaction: [DESCRIBE]. Identify all regulatory approvals, notifications, or clearances that may be required before or after completion, including: 1. Competition / anti-trust (threshold analysis). 2. Financial services regulators (if either party is a licensed entity). 3. Foreign investment review (if applicable). 4. Sector-specific regulators [e.g. MAS, CCCS in Singapore / FCA in UK / SEC in US — specify jurisdiction]. 5. Government or public interest approvals. For each approval: state the filing threshold, the applicable timeline, and whether completion can proceed before clearance.

**PROMPT C-E2 — Company Winding Up / Striking Off**
Act as a corporate lawyer. My client wishes to wind up / strike off [COMPANY NAME], a [JURISDICTION]-incorporated company. The company has: [DESCRIBE — assets, liabilities, employees, outstanding contracts, ongoing litigation if any]. Prepare: 1. A step-by-step procedure for voluntary winding up / members' voluntary liquidation / striking off (as appropriate). 2. The key legal requirements and statutory obligations on the directors. 3. The documents that need to be prepared and filed. 4. The estimated timeline and costs. 5. Any potential liabilities the directors should be aware of during the winding up process.
