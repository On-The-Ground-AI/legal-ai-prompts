# AI Prompt Pack for Litigation Lawyers
### Optimised for Microsoft Copilot, Claude, ChatGPT & Legal AI Tools

---

> **How to use this pack**
> Copy any prompt. Open Copilot in Word, Teams, or your browser. Paste the prompt. Replace all [BRACKETED TEXT] with your real details. Always review AI output before using it in any court document, filing, or client communication.

## Section A: Case Analysis & Strategy

**PROMPT L-A1 — Initial Case Assessment**
You are a senior litigation counsel. I am bringing a new matter to you with the following facts: [DESCRIBE THE DISPUTE IN PLAIN TERMS — WHO IS SUING WHOM, WHAT HAPPENED, WHAT IS THE CLAIMED LOSS]. Based on these facts, please provide: 1. A preliminary assessment of the legal causes of action available to the claimant. 2. The likely defences the defendant will raise. 3. The key facts and documents I will need to gather. 4. An initial view on the strength of the claim (Strong / Moderate / Weak) with reasons. 5. Any immediate time-sensitive issues (limitation periods, preservation of evidence, injunctive relief). Note any areas where the facts are unclear and you need more information.

*Copilot Tip: Use this in a new Teams chat or Word document at the start of every new matter. Follow up by asking: "What questions should I ask my client at the first meeting?"*

---

**PROMPT L-A2 — Limitation Period Check**
Act as a litigation lawyer in [JURISDICTION]. I need to check whether my client's claim is within the limitation period. Facts: [DESCRIBE THE CLAIM AND KEY DATES — when did the cause of action arise? When did my client first learn of the loss?]. Please advise: 1. The applicable limitation period under [JURISDICTION] law for each cause of action. 2. Whether any exceptions apply (e.g. fraud, deliberate concealment, disability, discovery rule). 3. The earliest and latest possible expiry dates. 4. Any steps I should take immediately to protect the limitation position. Flag if this is a close call and I should seek a second opinion.

---

**PROMPT L-A3 — Cause of Action Analysis**
Act as a litigation partner with expertise in [PRACTICE AREA, e.g. contract disputes / tort / company law]. The following facts have been given to me by a potential client: [PASTE FACT SUMMARY]. For each potential cause of action, provide: (1) the name of the cause of action, (2) the legal elements I must prove, (3) how the facts map to each element, (4) the likely evidence I will need, and (5) a RAG rating (Red/Amber/Green) for prospects of success. Present your analysis as a table.

---

**PROMPT L-A4 — Defence Strategy Memo**
I act for the defendant in the following dispute: [DESCRIBE THE CLAIM AND CLAIMANT'S ALLEGATIONS]. Draft an internal defence strategy memo covering: 1. Summary of the claimant's case as pleaded. 2. Our strongest defences (legal and factual). 3. Any counterclaims we should consider bringing. 4. Key documents and witnesses we need. 5. Our preliminary assessment of liability exposure. 6. Recommended next steps (investigate / defend / negotiate / seek to strike out). Use headings and keep the memo to 2 pages maximum.

---

**PROMPT L-A5 — Without Prejudice Settlement Letter**
Draft a Without Prejudice letter proposing settlement of the following dispute: - Acting for: [CLAIMANT / DEFENDANT] - Dispute: [DESCRIBE] - Our settlement proposal: [AMOUNT AND/OR TERMS] - Key reasons supporting our proposal: [LIST] The letter should: be marked clearly "Without Prejudice and Subject to Contract", acknowledge both parties' desire to resolve the matter commercially, present our position firmly but constructively, and invite a response within [TIMEFRAME]. Do not make any admissions of liability.

*Copilot Tip: Draft this in Word with Copilot, then use "Rewrite" to adjust the tone if needed — more or less assertive.*

## Section B: Pleadings & Court Documents

**PROMPT L-B1 — Statement of Claim / Particulars of Claim**
Act as a litigation lawyer. Draft a Statement of Claim for the following matter: - Court: [COURT NAME AND JURISDICTION] - Claimant: [NAME AND DESCRIPTION] - Defendant: [NAME AND DESCRIPTION] - Cause(s) of Action: [LIST] - Key Facts: [DESCRIBE IN CHRONOLOGICAL ORDER] - Loss and Damage Claimed: [AMOUNT AND BASIS] The pleading should: state facts only (not evidence or argument), be numbered in short paragraphs, include a Prayer for Relief at the end, and comply with [JURISDICTION] pleading rules. Flag any facts I have not provided that I will need to include.

---

**PROMPT L-B2 — Defence Pleading**
Act as a litigation lawyer drafting a Defence to the following Statement of Claim: [PASTE THE STATEMENT OF CLAIM OR SUMMARISE ITS KEY ALLEGATIONS]. Our client's position on each allegation is: [DESCRIBE]. Draft a Defence that: admits, denies, or does not admit each allegation paragraph by paragraph, sets out any positive case the defendant relies on, and pleads any limitation, contractual, or statutory defences. Highlight any paragraphs where the defendant's instructions are unclear and I need to take further instructions.

---

**PROMPT L-B3 — Affidavit Draft**
Draft an affidavit for [WITNESS NAME], a [ROLE/OCCUPATION], in support of [DESCRIBE THE APPLICATION OR PROCEEDINGS]. Facts to be deposed: [LIST THE KEY FACTS IN CHRONOLOGICAL ORDER]. The affidavit should: be written in the first person, state facts within the witness's personal knowledge, identify any facts stated on information and belief with the source, and follow the formal affidavit format required in [JURISDICTION]. Insert [EXHIBIT A], [EXHIBIT B] etc. as placeholders where documents should be exhibited.

---

**PROMPT L-B4 — Written Submissions / Skeletal Arguments**
Prepare written submissions for a [HEARING TYPE, e.g. summary judgment / interlocutory injunction / striking out] application in [JURISDICTION]. Our client's position: [CLAIMANT / DEFENDANT]. The key legal issues are: [LIST]. Our arguments on each issue: [DESCRIBE]. Please structure the submissions with: an introduction, a facts section, a legal analysis section for each issue with relevant authorities, and a conclusion with the orders sought. Note where I need to insert specific case citations.

---

**PROMPT L-B5 — Interlocutory Injunction Application**
I need to apply for an urgent interlocutory injunction in [JURISDICTION]. The situation is: [DESCRIBE — what is happening, what harm will occur, why it is urgent]. Draft the supporting affidavit addressing the three limbs of the [American Cyanamid / relevant jurisdiction] test: 1. Serious question to be tried — summarise the claim and why it is not frivolous. 2. Balance of convenience — argue why the harm of not granting the injunction outweighs the harm to the respondent. 3. Undertaking as to damages — note the applicant's ability to pay. Also draft the proposed terms of the injunction order.

## Section C: Evidence & Discovery

**PROMPT L-C1 — Document Review Summary**
Act as a litigation paralegal. I have gathered the following documents in a dispute about [DESCRIBE THE DISPUTE]. Review the document list below and for each document: (1) summarise its contents in 2-3 sentences, (2) identify its relevance to the issues in dispute, (3) note if it helps or hurts our case, and (4) flag any privilege issues. Documents: [PASTE LIST OR SUMMARIES OF DOCUMENTS].

---

**PROMPT L-C2 — Privilege Review Checklist**
I am conducting a document review for [MATTER NAME]. Generate a privilege review checklist for my team covering: 1. Categories of documents that are likely to attract legal professional privilege (legal advice privilege and litigation privilege). 2. Common situations where privilege may have been waived. 3. A step-by-step protocol for reviewing, tagging, and logging privileged documents. 4. The test for litigation privilege in [JURISDICTION] and the key date from which it applies. 5. Instructions for handling documents that may attract third-party privilege.

---

**PROMPT L-C3 — Interrogatories / Requests for Further Information**
Draft a set of interrogatories / Requests for Further Information arising from the Defence filed by the defendant in this matter. The Defence contains the following positions that require clarification: [LIST THE SPECIFIC UNCLEAR ALLEGATIONS OR DEFENCES]. For each request: state the paragraph of the Defence being interrogated, draft a precise question requiring a specific factual answer, and explain briefly why this information is relevant to the issues. Limit to the 10 most important requests.

---

**PROMPT L-C4 — Timeline of Key Events**
Create a chronological timeline of events in the following dispute. Facts provided: [PASTE THE FACTS]. The timeline should: list each event with date, brief description, and source document, be in table format with columns [Date | Event | Significance | Document Reference], highlight any gaps in the timeline where evidence is missing, and flag any dates that are in dispute between the parties.

## Section D: Hearings & Trial Preparation

**PROMPT L-D1 — Witness Preparation Questions**
I am preparing [WITNESS NAME] for cross-examination in a [TRIAL / ARBITRATION / HEARING] about [DESCRIBE THE DISPUTE]. The opposing party's case is: [SUMMARISE]. Draft 20 likely cross-examination questions the opposing counsel will ask this witness, focusing on: (1) inconsistencies with the documentary record, (2) credibility of the witness's account, and (3) any concessions they can extract. For each question, suggest the best way for the witness to answer it truthfully and clearly.

---

**PROMPT L-D2 — Opening Statement Outline**
Draft an opening statement outline for the [CLAIMANT / DEFENDANT] in a [TRIAL / ARBITRATION] about: [DESCRIBE THE DISPUTE AND KEY ISSUES]. The opening should: introduce the parties and the dispute, tell a clear narrative of what happened from our client's perspective, identify the key issues the tribunal must decide, preview our key evidence and witnesses, and conclude with the outcome we are seeking. Keep it to a 10-minute delivery. Flag where I should use visual aids or exhibits.

---

**PROMPT L-D3 — Expert Witness Instructions**
Draft a letter of instruction to an expert witness in [FIELD, e.g. forensic accounting / engineering / medical] for the following matter: - Matter: [DESCRIBE THE DISPUTE] - Expert's role: [DESCRIBE WHAT THE EXPERT WILL OPINE ON] - Key facts: [SUMMARISE THE RELEVANT FACTS] - Documents provided to the expert: [LIST] - Questions for the expert to answer: [LIST] The letter should comply with the requirements for expert evidence in [JURISDICTION] (including the expert's overriding duty to the court, not the instructing party).

---

**PROMPT L-D4 — Cost Submissions / Bill of Costs**
Draft a summary of costs for party-and-party costs in [JURISDICTION]. Our client is [CLAIMANT / DEFENDANT] and has succeeded in the proceedings. The key work done was: [DESCRIBE — hearings attended, documents prepared, time period]. Our total costs are approximately [AMOUNT]. Prepare: (1) a narrative summary of the work done justifying the costs claimed, (2) the legal basis for the costs order we are seeking, and (3) a brief response to any likely objections from the paying party.

## Section E: Shareholder Disputes (see also dedicated pack)

**PROMPT L-E1 — Oppression Claim Analysis**
Act as a corporate litigation specialist. My client is a minority shareholder in [COMPANY NAME] and believes the majority shareholders / directors have acted oppressively. The facts are: [DESCRIBE — what actions have been taken, what decisions have been made, what prejudice has my client suffered]. Analyse: 1. Whether these facts support a claim for minority shareholder oppression / unfair prejudice under [JURISDICTION] law. 2. The elements I must prove. 3. The remedies available (buyout, injunction, winding up, etc.). 4. Any time limits or procedural steps. 5. The strengths and weaknesses of my client's position.

---

**PROMPT L-E2 — Derivative Action Assessment**
My client wishes to bring a derivative action on behalf of [COMPANY NAME] against [TARGET — director / third party] for [DESCRIBE THE ALLEGED WRONG]. Under [JURISDICTION] company law: 1. What are the procedural requirements to bring a statutory derivative action? 2. What is the leave test the court will apply? 3. What evidence does my client need to satisfy the test? 4. Are there any preliminary steps required (demand on the company, etc.)? 5. What are the key risks and costs considerations?
