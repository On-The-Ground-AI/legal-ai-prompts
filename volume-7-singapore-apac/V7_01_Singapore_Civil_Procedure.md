## A. COMMENCING PROCEEDINGS

### PROMPT 001 - ORIGINATING CLAIM (FORM 8) DRAFTING

**PROMPT ID:** SCP-001-001

**Full Text:**
Draft an Originating Claim (Form 8) under Rules of Court 2021 for [CLIENT NAME] claiming [RELIEF SOUGHT - e.g., breach of contract/negligence/defamation] against [DEFENDANT(S)]. The claim arises from [FACTUAL BACKGROUND - 2-3 sentences]. [CLIENT NAME] seeks [SPECIFIC DAMAGES/REMEDY] and [OTHER RELIEF]. The claim is filed in [STATE COURTS/HIGH COURT] on the basis that [JURISDICTION BASIS - e.g., subject matter amount, nature of dispute]. Please draft concise, numbered paragraphs following best practice formatting. Include a Statement of Claim properly particularized with dates, amounts, and specific breaches or wrongs alleged. Ensure compliance with Practice Direction 3.1 (Drafting of Pleadings) and include an Indemnity on Form 6 if appropriate.

**Context:**
Form 8 is the standard originating document for commencement of proceedings in both State Courts and High Court. Proper pleading is critical as defects may lead to striking out or dismissal under O18 ROC 2021. The Statement of Claim must contain sufficient particularity to give fair notice and enable proper response.

**Difficulty:** Intermediate

**Follow-up:**
After drafting, discuss: (1) potential defences and how the pleading addresses them; (2) whether the claim should be bifurcated with liability and quantum separately; (3) service strategy and any requirement for service outside jurisdiction.

### PROMPT 005 - CONCURRENT WRITS AND RE-SERVICE

**PROMPT ID:** SCP-001-005

**Full Text:**
[CLIENT'S] Originating Claim against [DEFENDANT] was issued on [DATE], with service validity expiring on [DATE]. Service has not yet been effected due to [REASON - e.g., defendant's difficulty locating, overseas relocation]. Explain: the procedural requirements for issuing a concurrent writ; how it operates alongside the original writ; cost implications; practical considerations for service. Draft the prayer for issue of a concurrent writ, or advise on the application for extension of service validity under O6 r7 ROC 2021. **Context:**
Concurrent writs are useful when original service validity has expired and re-service is required. **Full Text:**

### PROMPT 009 - SUMMARY JUDGMENT APPLICATION

**PROMPT ID:** SCP-002-001

**Full Text:**
[CLIENT - CLAIMANT/DEFENDANT] seeks summary judgment on [CLAIM/DEFENCE] in proceedings [CASE REFERENCE]. The [OPPOSING PARTY] has filed [DEFENCE/COUNTERCLAIM] alleging [DEFENCE SUMMARY]. [CLIENT] contends that there is no triable issue of fact and [OPPOSING PARTY] has no real prospect of success because [SUBSTANTIVE ARGUMENT - 1-2 sentences]. Apply the test under O14 ROC 2021 (if State Courts) or O13 ROC 2021 (if High Court), addressing: (1) whether [OPPOSING PARTY] has a real prospect of success (not just an arguable case); (2) whether there is a triable issue of fact; (3) whether there are compelling reasons for a trial despite meeting the test; (4) the strength and credibility of affidavit evidence. Prepare a supporting affidavit with exhibits (documents, correspondence, expert opinion if available) and draft the prayer for summary judgment. Address anticipated defences and why they do not raise a triable issue.

**Context:**
Summary judgment (called "summons for directions" in some contexts) is a critical interlocutory remedy allowing parties to obtain judgment without trial when the opposing party has no real prospect of success. The threshold is high: the opposing party must have more than a fanciful case but need not be strong on the merits. Court considers whether a trial would truly add value.

**Difficulty:** Advanced

**Follow-up:**
Discuss: (1) likely grounds of opposition and counter-arguments; (2) whether partial summary judgment (on some issues) is achievable; (3) whether conditional judgment (summary judgment on liability with quantum to trial) should be sought; (4) costs implications and recovery of costs; (5) timing of application and impact on case progression.

### PROMPT 010 - STRIKING OUT PROCEEDINGS

**PROMPT ID:** SCP-002-002

**Full Text:**
[OPPOSING PARTY'S] pleading (being [Originating Claim/Defence/Counterclaim/Reply]) contains [DEFECT DESCRIPTION]. [CLIENT] seeks to strike out the pleading or parts thereof under O18 r8 ROC 2021 on the grounds that: (1) it discloses no reasonable cause of action/defence; (2) it is frivolous or vexatious; (3) it constitutes an abuse of process; and/or (4) [OTHER GROUND - e.g., procedural defect]. The pleading fails to [SPECIFIC FAILURE - e.g., "particularize the alleged breach with dates and amounts," "establish causation between the act complained of and the loss claimed"]. Draft an application to strike out with detailed affidavit evidence addressing each ground, identifying the specific paragraphs or passages that are objectionable, and explaining why each is unmaintainable. Include proposed alternative relief (striking out with leave to amend) if appropriate.

**Context:**
Striking out is a drastic remedy available only when the defect is patent and self-evident from the pleading itself, without requiring investigation of facts. It is not available merely because the pleading is weak on the merits or inadequately supported by evidence (which is addressed through summary judgment or at trial). Courts approach striking out applications cautiously to avoid denying parties their right to litigate.

**Difficulty:** Intermediate

**Follow-up:**
Address: (1) whether the defect can be cured by amendment and, if so, whether to offer leave to amend; (2) procedures and timelines for any amendment; (3) costs recovery if striking out is successful; (4) any threshold objections to jurisdiction or service that should be dealt with first; (5) whether striking out should be pursued or whether summary judgment is a more efficient remedy.

### PROMPT 013 - INTERROGATORIES APPLICATION AND RESPONSES

**Full Text:**
[CLIENT] has received interrogatories from [OPPOSING PARTY] under O26 r5 ROC 2021 requiring answers to [NUMBER] questions addressing [SUBJECT MATTER]. ### PROMPT 014 - MAREVA INJUNCTION / FREEZING ORDER APPLICATION

**PROMPT ID:** SCP-002-006

**Full Text:**
[CLIENT] has evidence that [DEFENDANT] is likely to dissipate, conceal, or remove assets from [JURISDICTION/COUNTRY] with intent to defeat any judgment that [CLIENT] may obtain in [CASE REFERENCE] or related proceedings. [CLIENT] seeks a Mareva injunction (freezing order) under O29 r3 ROC 2021 to restrain [DEFENDANT] from dealing with assets up to the value of SGD [AMOUNT]. The applicant must establish: (1) a strong prima facie case on the merits; (2) that there is a real risk of dissipation; (3) that the balance of convenience favors granting the order; (4) that there is no alternative remedy; (5) that the court's discretion should be exercised (including public interest considerations). Affidavit evidence addressing each element, including details of the defendant's conduct, asset holdings, and flight risk. The order should be proportionate and include exceptions (living expenses, legal fees, ordinary business expenses). Draft the application and supporting affidavit, addressing the test comprehensively.

**Context:**
Mareva injunctions are powerful interim remedies preventing asset dissipation pending trial. They require careful application as they are interlocutory and may affect the defendant's livelihood if too broad. The standard is demanding: "strong" prima facie case and "real risk" of dissipation (not merely possibility). Orders are often granted on a without-notice basis initially to preserve their effect, followed by inter partes hearing.

**Difficulty:** Advanced

**Follow-up:**
Discuss: (1) whether to apply without notice (ex parte) initially or on notice; (2) undertaking in damages required from the applicant; (3) scope of the order and exceptions (family living expenses, business operations); (4) disclosure obligations and updating affidavits; (5) variation or discharge applications if circumstances change; (6) ancillary orders (disclosure of assets, bank account information); (7) implications of breach of the order.

### PROMPT 015 - ANTON PILLER ORDER / SEARCH ORDER APPLICATION

**PROMPT ID:** SCP-002-007

**Full Text:**
[CLIENT] has strong evidence that [DEFENDANT] is in possession of [INFRINGING/CONFIDENTIAL/RELEVANT MATERIALS] (such as [DESCRIPTION - e.g., "counterfeit goods," "misappropriated trade secrets," "documents evidencing fraud"]) which [DEFENDANT] is likely to destroy, conceal, or remove if given notice of proceedings. [CLIENT] seeks an Anton Piller order (search order) under O29 r3 ROC 2021, which would permit [CLIENT'S] solicitors and an independent supervising solicitor to enter [DEFENDANT'S] premises and search for, photograph, and seize the materials. The applicant must establish: (1) a strong prima facie case; (2) that there is an extremely serious risk of destruction or concealment of evidence; (3) that a search order is proportionate and necessary (no less intrusive alternative available); (4) that the balance of convenience favors the order. Affidavit evidence with detailed factual basis for the seriousness and urgency. Draft the application, including the proposed order with safeguards (supervising solicitor, limit on persons present, preservation of privilege, time-limited execution). Address the applicant's undertaking in damages.

**Context:**
Anton Piller orders are the most intrusive interlocutory remedy available and are granted only in exceptional circumstances where the evidence of serious wrongdoing and imminent destruction/concealment is compelling. Courts impose strict procedural safeguards, including supervision by an independent solicitor, careful wording to avoid excessive searches, and protection of privileged documents. The applicant bears significant risk in damages if the order is later found to have been unjustified.

**Difficulty:** Advanced

**Follow-up:**
Discuss: (1) whether to proceed without notice initially to preserve effect; (2) execution logistics and timing; (3) roles of supervising solicitor and independent advisor; (4) handling of legally privileged materials discovered during search; (5) preservation and documentation of materials seized; (6) follow-up proceedings for detention, return, or use of materials; (7) counterclaims for trespass or wrongful seizure if execution is improper.

### PROMPT 016 - SECURITY FOR COSTS APPLICATION

**PROMPT ID:** SCP-002-008

**Full Text:**
[DEFENDANT/RESPONDENT] seeks security for costs in [CASE REFERENCE], arguing that [PLAINTIFF/APPLICANT] is impecunious or faces difficulties in paying any costs ordered against [PLAINTIFF/APPLICANT] if unsuccessful. [DEFENDANT/RESPONDENT] seeks SGD [AMOUNT] as security under O33 r1 ROC 2021. The applicant for security must establish: (1) that [PLAINTIFF/APPLICANT] is unable to pay costs if ordered against [PLAINTIFF/APPLICANT]; (2) that there are circumstances justifying an order (e.g., plaintiff ordinarily resident outside jurisdiction, likely to cease business, delay in prosecution). [PLAINTIFF/APPLICANT] may defend by showing: (1) actual ability to pay costs; (2) that security would stifle the claim; (3) circumstances justifying denial (e.g., defendant has security advantages, undertaking in damages); (4) ability to arrange alternative security. Prepare a detailed affidavit addressing [PLAINTIFF/APPLICANT'S] financial position, business operations, and assets. If defending, address why security should not be ordered and whether a reduced amount or alternative arrangement (banker's guarantee, undertaking by solicitor) would be appropriate.

**Context:**
Security for costs protects defendants/respondents against costs liability if plaintiffs/applicants are unable to satisfy costs orders. It is discretionary and must be balanced against the plaintiff/applicant's right to access to justice. Normally, security for costs is not ordered against a plaintiff ordinarily resident in Singapore unless other factors (e.g., insolvency) are present. Plaintiffs outside Singapore more readily face security orders. The court considers whether security would effectively stifle the proceedings.

**Difficulty:** Intermediate

**Follow-up:**
Discuss: (1) amount of security appropriate to cover likely costs; (2) form of security (cash deposit, banker's guarantee, undertaking); (3) timing of payment and release; (4) whether security should be ordered incrementally (e.g., before certain stages); (5) variation if costs substantially increase; (6) plaintiff's financial disclosure and ability to obtain security.

### PROMPT 018 - SINGLE JOINT EXPERT APPOINTMENT

**Full Text:**
[CASE REFERENCE] involves expert evidence on [EXPERT FIELD - e.g., structural engineering, valuation, medical]. The parties have agreed (or [CLIENT] proposes) to appoint a single joint expert ("SJE") under O40A ROC 2021 rather than each party instructing separate experts. ### PROMPT 019 - BIFURCATION: LIABILITY AND QUANTUM

**PROMPT ID:** SCP-003-003

**Full Text:**
[CLIENT] proposes that [CASE REFERENCE] be bifurcated, with liability issues tried separately from quantum/damages issues. The proposed structure: (1) Phase 1 - Trial of liability (whether [DEFENDANT] breached the duty/contract/caused the loss, and apportionment if relevant); (2) Phase 2 - Assessment of damages (quantum) if [CLIENT] is successful on liability, or alternatively, [DEFENDANT] may settle on quantum without contesting liability. Advantages of bifurcation include: reducing trial costs if liability is the main contested issue; settling quantum issues more easily once liability is established; enabling expert evidence to be limited to Phase 1 if quantum is not in dispute; and clarity of issues at each stage. Courts support bifurcation where it is likely to improve access to justice and reduce costs, but resist it if it creates procedural complications.

[PLAINTIFF] suffered loss comprising: [HEADS OF LOSS - e.g., "contract price paid and not recovered," "cost of repair of damaged property," "lost profits," "medical expenses," "diminution in value"]. Prepare for the damages assessment hearing addressing: (1) Quantification of each head of loss (with supporting documentation: invoices, receipts, expert valuations, financial records); (2) Expert evidence on quantum (valuer, accountant, engineer as appropriate); (3) Mitigation: Evidence that [PLAINTIFF] has taken reasonable steps to minimize loss (obtaining quotes, selling damaged property, finding alternative employment, etc.). [DEFENDANT] may argue [PLAINTIFF] failed to mitigate. (4) Causation: Establish that each loss flows directly from [DEFENDANT'S] wrongdoing. [DEFENDANT] may argue losses are too remote or speculative. (5) Heads of Loss Typically Recoverable: (a) Direct loss (cost of performance, repair, replacement); (b) Consequential loss (lost profits, increased costs) if foreseeable; (c) Loss of opportunity; (d) Emotional distress (in limited circumstances); (e) Aggravated damages (if [DEFENDANT'S] conduct was particularly egregious). Prepare detailed submissions on each head, with supporting evidence and case law on quantification of similar losses.

**Context:**
Damages assessment is a technical hearing focusing on quantification of loss. Unlike liability, damages assessment often proceeds more smoothly if liability is clear. Expert evidence is critical for quantum. Disputes often arise on mitigation, foreseeability, and causation of loss.

**Difficulty:** Advanced

**Follow-up:**
Discuss: (1) expert evidence on valuation and quantum; (2) likely defendant objections to causation or remoteness of loss; (3) mitigation efforts and whether they are sufficient; (4) settlement opportunities if quantum is negotiable; (5) interest on damages and time value of money; (6) enforcement of any damages award.

## F. APPEALS

### PROMPT 035 - NOTICE OF APPEAL DRAFTING

**PROMPT ID:** SCP-006-001

**Full Text:**
[PARTY] wishes to appeal the judgment of [JUDGE NAME] delivered on [DATE] in [CASE REFERENCE]. File a Notice of Appeal under O57 r4 ROC 2021 within 14 days of judgment (strict deadline, subject to limited extensions for good cause). The Notice of Appeal must identify: (1) The date of judgment appealed; (2) The court appealed from (District Court, High Court, or SICC); (3) The parties to the appeal; (4) The grounds of appeal (stated concisely; detailed arguments reserved for Appellant's Case). Typical grounds include: (a) Errors of law (misapplication of statute or case law, wrong legal test, misdirection); (b) Errors of fact (findings unsupported by evidence, drawing of inferences not open on the evidence, misreading of documents); (c) Errors in procedural fairness (denial of opportunity to present case, bias, breach of natural justice); (d) Errors regarding discretionary orders (sentence, costs, custody). The grounds must be specific and identify the particular issue(s) in dispute; generic allegations of error are unhelpful. Draft a Notice of Appeal with clear, focused grounds. Thereafter, prepare the Appellant's Case (detailed submissions on each ground, with authorities and references to the trial record).

**Context:**
The appeal threshold is demanding: appellate courts will not interfere with findings of fact unless they are clearly unsupported by evidence or the judge has misdirected themselves on the law. Appellate courts respect trial judge's assessment of credibility and factual findings based on seeing witnesses and assessing evidence. Errors of law are more readily overturned than errors of fact.

**Difficulty:** Advanced

**Follow-up:**
Discuss: (1) merits of each ground of appeal; (2) estimated strength of the appeal; (3) costs implications of appeal; (4) whether conditions of appeal (e.g., security for costs, stay of execution) will apply; (5) likelihood of success; (6) settlement negotiations during appeal; (7) post-appeal options if appeal fails or only partially succeeds.

### PROMPT 036 - APPELLANT'S CASE STRUCTURE

**PROMPT ID:** SCP-006-002

**Full Text:**
Prepare the Appellant's Case for [APPELLANT]'s appeal of the [LOWER COURT] judgment in [CASE REFERENCE]. The Appellant's Case is the detailed written submissions to be filed in the appellate court, addressing the grounds of appeal. Structure as follows: (1) Introduction: Identify the appeal, parties, court appealed from, and date of judgment. (2) Summary of Proceedings: Brief outline of the lower court proceedings, issues in dispute, and outcome. (3) For Each Ground of Appeal: (a) Identify the specific error of law or fact alleged; (b) Reference the relevant statutory provision, case law, or principle; (c) Explain what the lower court did wrong; (d) Cite the trial record (AEIC paragraphs, cross-examination transcripts, exhibits) showing the basis for the alleged error; (e) Explain what the correct position is and why the lower court erred; (f) Explain why this error is significant (affects the outcome). (4) Remedies: Specify the relief sought on appeal (allow appeal, set aside judgment, order new trial, vary damages, etc.). (5) Authorities: Append list of cases and statutes cited with page references. Appellant's Cases should be focused on the genuine points of appeal; padding with weak arguments diminishes credibility. Typical length: 50-100 pages depending on complexity.

**Context:**
The Appellant's Case is the critical submission at the appellate stage. It must identify genuine, arguable errors; weak or frivolous arguments will damage credibility. Appellate courts are deferential to trial courts on findings of fact and will overturn only if manifestly unsupported by evidence. Errors of law are more readily overturned.

**Difficulty:** Advanced

**Follow-up:**
Discuss: (1) strength of each ground of appeal; (2) likely counter-arguments from the Respondent; (3) additional evidence or arguments that may be permitted on appeal (fresh evidence requires permission under O57 r13 ROC 2021); (4) procedural compliance (filing deadlines, format, length); (5) settlement discussions during appeal process; (6) costs implications and potential uplift for frivolous appeals.

### PROMPT 037 - RESPONDENT'S CASE STRUCTURE

**PROMPT ID:** SCP-006-003

**Full Text:**
The Appellant has filed an appeal in [CASE REFERENCE]. Prepare the Respondent's Case on behalf of [RESPONDENT], responding to the Appellant's allegations of error. The Respondent's Case should: (1) Introduction: Identify the appeal and respondent's role in defending the lower court judgment. (2) Summary of Proceedings: Brief outline of lower court proceedings and findings. (3) Response to Each Ground of Appeal: (a) Summarize the appellant's allegation of error; (b) Explain why the appellant's argument is incorrect; (c) Defend the lower court's reasoning and findings; (d) Cite authorities supporting the lower court's approach; (e) Explain that the evidence supported the lower court's findings (if factual ground); (f) Confirm the lower court correctly applied law (if legal ground). (4) Alternative Arguments: If the Respondent cannot defend the lower court's primary reasoning, present alternative grounds supporting the judgment (but only if in Respondent's favor; Respondent should not attack its own successful position). (5) Costs: Seek costs of the appeal. (6) Relief: Confirm that the appeal should be dismissed and the judgment affirmed. The Respondent's Case should be well-reasoned and persuasive; overstated defenses diminish credibility. Typical length: 40-80 pages.

**Context:**
The Respondent's Case is an important opportunity to oppose the appeal and defend the lower court judgment. Respondents should not be passive; a strong, well-reasoned response significantly aids the appellate court. Respondents can also seek costs of the appeal if the appeal is found to be without merit or frivolous.

**Difficulty:** Advanced

**Follow-up:**
Discuss: (1) merits of each ground of appeal and likelihood of success for appellant; (2) weaknesses in the lower court's reasoning and alternative arguments; (3) procedural compliance with appeal deadlines and format; (4) settlement discussions or opportunity for appeal to be withdrawn; (5) costs of the appeal and recovery prospects; (6) preparation for oral argument before the appellate court.

### PROMPT 038 - APPLICATION FOR LEAVE TO APPEAL

**PROMPT ID:** SCP-006-004

**Full Text:**
In some circumstances, an appeal requires leave (permission) from the appellate court. This applies to: (a) Appeals to the Court of Appeal from the High Court in cases where the appellate court's jurisdiction is limited (O57 r2 ROC 2021); (b) Appeals on questions of law of general public importance; (c) Fresh evidence on appeal (O57 r13 ROC 2021). Prepare an Application for Leave to Appeal addressing: (1) The grounds requiring leave (identify whether leave is required as a matter of law for this appeal). (2) The proposed grounds of appeal (stated concisely). (3) Why leave should be granted: (a) Whether the ground raises a question of law of general public importance; (b) Whether the ground has a reasonable prospect of success; (c) Whether there is a sufficient public interest in the appeal; (d) Whether the decision is inconsistent with prior case law. (4) Supporting affidavit evidence establishing the basis for seeking leave. (5) The Applicant's track record and credibility (credible appellants are more likely to be granted leave). If leave is granted, proceed with full appeal by Notice of Appeal and Appellant's Case. Advise on the timing and prospects of leave applications; some judges are more amenable to granting leave on novel legal issues.

**Context:**
Leave requirements act as a gatekeeper to the appellate court, filtering out weak or frivolous appeals while allowing genuine legal development. Leave applications on questions of law of general public importance are more readily granted than applications based purely on factual disputes.

**Difficulty:** Advanced

**Follow-up:**
Discuss: (1) merits and novelty of the legal question; (2) likelihood of leave being granted; (3) whether the applicant should proceed with full appeal preparation while leave application is pending; (4) costs of leave application and appeal combined; (5) alternative remedies (settlement, judicial review for procedural unfairness) if leave is not granted.

## G. ENFORCEMENT

### PROMPT 039 - EXAMINATION OF JUDGMENT DEBTOR

**PROMPT ID:** SCP-007-001

**Full Text:**
[CREDITOR] has obtained judgment for SGD [AMOUNT] against [DEBTOR] in [CASE REFERENCE] but [DEBTOR] has failed to pay. [CREDITOR] wishes to understand [DEBTOR'S] financial position to identify assets available for enforcement. Under O28 r2 ROC 2021 (State Courts) and O48 r1 ROC 2021 (High Court), [CREDITOR] may apply for an Order for Examination of Judgment Debtor, requiring [DEBTOR] to attend court and answer questions on oath regarding [DEBTOR'S] assets, liabilities, income, and employment. Prepare: (1) Application for examination order, explaining that judgment remains unpaid and enforcement through examination is necessary. (2) Proposed questionnaire for examination addressing: (a) Identity and employment; (b) Assets (real property, vehicles, bank accounts, investments, business interests); (c) Liabilities and debts; (d) Income and earning capacity; (e) Recent dispositions of assets (gifts, transfers, sales); (f) Reasons for non-payment. (3) Affidavit confirming judgment, failure to pay, and efforts at collection. The examination is conducted before a judicial officer or examiner. [DEBTOR'S] non-attendance or refusal to answer may result in contempt findings and further court orders.

**Context:**
Examination of judgment debtor is a discovery tool used to identify assets before pursuing specific enforcement mechanisms (writ of seizure and sale, garnishee proceedings, etc.). It does not directly enforce the judgment but provides information necessary for effective enforcement. Debtors must attend and answer truthfully or face contempt proceedings.

**Difficulty:** Intermediate

**Follow-up:**
Discuss: (1) asset information disclosed at examination and likely enforcement targets; (2) next steps for enforcement (writ of seizure and sale for identified assets, garnishee orders for income, examination of third parties holding debtor's property); (3) protection against asset dissipation if debtor appears uncooperative or attempting to hide assets; (4) costs of enforcement and recovery prospects; (5) bankruptcy/insolvency proceedings if debtor is clearly unable to pay.

### PROMPT 040 - WRIT OF SEIZURE AND SALE

**PROMPT ID:** SCP-007-002

**Full Text:**
Following examination of judgment debtor [DEBTOR], [CREDITOR] has identified specific assets available for enforcement. [CREDITOR] seeks to execute the judgment through a Writ of Seizure and Sale ("WSS") to seize and sell [IDENTIFIED ASSET(S) - e.g., motor vehicle, jewelry, furniture]. Under O47 ROC 2021, prepare: (1) Application for WSS, identifying the judgment, amount due, and asset(s) to be seized; (2) Description of the asset(s) including registration details (e.g., vehicle registration number, real property address and title reference); (3) Affidavit confirming the judgment, amount outstanding, and identification of the asset; (4) Undertaking by the judgment creditor to pay enforcement costs (sheriff's fees, storage, auctioneering costs); (5) Procedure for execution: Court issues WSS, directed to the Sheriff, who seizes the asset, stores it, and arranges sale by auction or public tender. Proceeds of sale are applied to satisfy the judgment, with any surplus returned to [DEBTOR]. If the asset value is insufficient to cover the judgment, further enforcement actions may be required. Advise on: timing of enforcement (4-week pause before sale to allow debtor redemption opportunity); costs of enforcement (Sheriff's fees typically 10-15% of sale value); and recovery prospects given asset values.

**Context:**
Writ of Seizure and Sale is the primary mechanism for enforcement against movable property. It is effective but may be delayed if the asset is small, difficult to locate, or costly to seize relative to its value. For real property, different enforcement mechanisms apply (charging order, Mareva injunction on sale proceeds). Costs of enforcement can be significant.

**Difficulty:** Intermediate

**Follow-up:**
Discuss: (1) identified assets and their likely market value; (2) costs of enforcement vs. amount recoverable; (3) whether other enforcement mechanisms (garnishee orders for income, examination of third parties, charging orders) are more efficient; (4) debtor's attempts at redemption before sale; (5) post-sale proceeds distribution and remaining balance on judgment.

### PROMPT 041 - GARNISHEE PROCEEDINGS

**PROMPT ID:** SCP-007-003

**Full Text:**
[CREDITOR] has obtained judgment for SGD [AMOUNT] against [DEBTOR] and seeks to enforce the judgment by garnisheeing income or funds held by third parties for [DEBTOR]. [DEBTOR] is employed by [EMPLOYER] (garnishee of wages) or has bank accounts with [BANK] (garnishee of funds). Under O47 r1 ROC 2021, [CREDITOR] may apply for a Garnishee Order to redirect [DEBTOR'S] wages or bank funds to satisfy the judgment. Prepare: (1) Application for Garnishee Order Nisi, identifying: (a) The judgment and amount due; (b) The garnishee (third party) holding the debtor's funds/income; (c) The nature of the debt (employment wages, bank deposits); (d) The quantum of income/funds expected to be available. (2) Affidavit evidence confirming: (a) The judgment; (b) Non-payment by debtor; (c) The garnishee's relationship to debtor (employer, bank); (c) Other enforcement attempts and why garnishment is necessary. (3) Service on both debtor and garnishee. (4) Hearing: At the Garnishee Order Nisi hearing, the court issues an interim order requiring the garnishee to show cause why a Final Garnishee Order should not be issued. (5) The garnishee may defend by showing the debt does not exist, the garnishee has no funds/authority, or set-offs/priorities apply (e.g., taxes, statutory deductions from wages). (6) If the Garnishee Order is made Final, the garnishee must pay specified portions of the debtor's income/funds directly to the creditor until the judgment is satisfied.

**Context:**
Garnishee proceedings are effective for capturing regular income or bank deposits. They are simpler than WSS for movable property and have lower execution costs. However, garnishee orders may be resisted if the garnishee claims set-offs, priorities, or insolvency. Wages are subject to statutory caps on deduction (debt does not extend to essential living expenses).

**Difficulty:** Intermediate

**Follow-up:**
Discuss: (1) identified source of funds (employer, bank) and regularity of income/deposits; (2) expected monthly garnishment and time to full satisfaction of judgment; (3) likelihood of garnishee defense (lack of funds, set-offs, priorities); (4) garnishee's compliance and procedures for payment to creditor; (5) variation or discharge if debtor's financial situation changes; (6) statutory limits on wage garnishment; (7) combined enforcement strategies (garnishee + WSS).

### PROMPT 042 - BANKRUPTCY AND WINDING UP ON JUDGMENT DEBT

**PROMPT ID:** SCP-007-004

**Full Text:**
[CREDITOR] holds an unsatisfied judgment for SGD [AMOUNT] against [DEBTOR]. If [DEBTOR] is an individual unable or unwilling to pay, [CREDITOR] may petition for [DEBTOR]'s bankruptcy under the Insolvency Act (Cap. 353). If [DEBTOR] is a company, [CREDITOR] may petition for [DEBTOR'S] winding up on the ground of insolvency or failure to pay a debt due. Advise on: (1) Bankruptcy Petition (individual debtor): (a) Requirement that the judgment debt is at least SGD [AMOUNT, typically SGD 10,000]; (b) Service of a statutory demand on debtor for payment within 21 days (O72 ROC 2021); (c) If debtor fails to satisfy the demand or set it aside, petition for bankruptcy may be filed; (d) Procedure and hearing before a Registrar or Judge; (e) Making of a Bankruptcy Order; (f) Appointment of Official Assignee to manage debtor's assets and distribute to creditors. (2) Winding Up Petition (company debtor): (a) Service of statutory demand for debt due (Section 254, Companies Act); (b) 21-day payment period; (c) If unpaid, filing of winding up petition; (d) Court hearing and assessment of insolvency; (e) Making of winding up order; (f) Appointment of liquidator. (3) Advantages: Bankruptcy/winding up may compel payment from debtor's assets, prevent further asset dissipation, and deter future default. (4) Disadvantages: Lengthy process, costs of petition, debtor's defenses (disputed debt, cash-flow insolvency vs. balance-sheet insolvency), and recovery likely limited if debtor has few assets. Consider whether bankruptcy/winding up is proportionate given the judgment amount and likely recovery.

**Context:**
Bankruptcy and winding up are enforcement mechanisms of last resort, appropriate when the debtor is truly insolvent or judgment-proof and other enforcement mechanisms (WSS, garnishee) are ineffective. They are lengthy and costly but provide access to the debtor's assets through the Official Assignee or liquidator's investigative powers.

**Difficulty:** Advanced

**Follow-up:**
Discuss: (1) debtor's solvency position and likelihood of successful bankruptcy/winding up petition; (2) defenses available to debtor (disputed debt, solvency despite cash-flow issues); (3) procedural steps and timelines for bankruptcy/winding up; (4) costs of petition and likely recovery from debtor's assets; (5) implications for other creditors (unsecured, secured) competing for assets; (6) alternative strategies (settlement, assignment of judgment debt to recovery specialist).
