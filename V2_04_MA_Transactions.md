## Due Diligence - Legal

**PROMPT 026 — Legal Due Diligence Checklist (Target Company)**

Prepare a comprehensive legal due diligence checklist for acquisition of a Singapore private company, organized by functional area: (1) Incorporation & Corporate Documents: (a) certificate of incorporation and status, (b) current constitution and all amendments, (c) board resolutions authorizing sale, (d) shareholder approvals obtained or required, (e) capitalization table and share register accuracy; **PROMPT 035 — Warranties & Indemnities — Drafting Deep Dive**

Prepare comprehensive guidance on drafting representations, warranties, and indemnification provisions for M&A transaction documents, including: (1) Key Distinction: (a) representations = statements of fact made as of a specific date (signing or closing), (b) warranties = ongoing obligations (typically broader, but sometimes used interchangeably), (c) indemnification = compensation mechanism if representations breach occurs; (2) Seller Representation Categories: (a) Authority Reps: seller has right to convey shares/assets, no conflicts with charter/bylaws/agreements, all necessary consents obtained, (b) Title Reps: seller owns shares free and clear of liens/encumbrances/claims, (c) Financial Reps: financial statements fairly present financial condition, GAAP/SFRS compliance, no undisclosed liabilities, (d) Business Operations Reps: contracts listed are accurate, no material defaults, licenses/permits current, intellectual property owned/licensed as described, (e) Compliance Reps: laws/regulations/permits complied with, no litigation, environmental compliance, (f) Related Party Reps: all related party transactions disclosed; (3) Knowledge Qualifiers: (a) "actual knowledge" narrowest (individual's direct knowledge without investigation), (b) "knowledge after reasonable inquiry" broader (expectation of some diligence), (c) "deemed knowledge" (management should know from normal operations); (4) Materiality Thresholds: (a) materiality qualifiers in reps ("other than amounts not exceeding X individually or Y in aggregate"), (b) ordinary course of business exception ("except as required in ordinary course"), (c) disclosed exceptions (reps subject to exceptions listed in disclosure schedules); (5) Survival Periods: (a) general reps: 12-18 months post-closing (standard market), (b) fundamental reps (authority, title, capitalization): 36 months or longer, (c) tax reps: 60+ months (tax statute of limitations period), (d) environmental reps: indefinite (environmental liability can emerge years later), (e) key employee/customer reps: 12-24 months; (6) Indemnification Mechanics: (a) indemnifying party (seller typically, unless indemnifying for assumed liabilities) compensates indemnified party (buyer), (b) indemnification baskets: (i) de minimis threshold (per-claim threshold, e.g., $25K, below which no claims payable), (ii) tipping basket (once aggregate exceeds threshold, only amounts above basket payable, e.g., $250K aggregate), (iii) zero basket for fundamental reps (no threshold); (c) caps on indemnification: (i) individual claim cap (if breach exceeds X, whole amount indemnified), (ii) aggregate cap (total indemnification capped at % of purchase price, e.g., 10-20%), (iii) caps per rep category if negotiated separately; (7) Escrow Security: (a) percentage of purchase price held in escrow (10-20% typical), (b) escrow duration (12-24 months typical), (c) escrow agent role and release mechanics, (d) escrow used to satisfy indemnification claims; (8) Indemnification Procedure: (a) notice of claim timing and detail required, (b) indemnified party mitigation obligations (duty to minimize damages), (c) indemnifying party defense rights (if not fraud), (d) settlement approval rights; (9) Calculation of Damages: (a) net of taxes (tax indemnification typically net of tax benefit), (b) insurance proceeds and other recovery offsets, (c) duty to mitigate, (d) consequential/indirect damages typically excluded; (10) Exclusive Remedy: (a) indemnification is exclusive remedy for rep breaches (no separate lawsuit), (b) exceptions for fraud, knowing misrepresentation, environmental liabilities; (11) Market-Standard Variations: (a) seller-favorable: high thresholds, low caps, short survival, knowledge qualifiers, (b) buyer-favorable: low thresholds, high caps, long survival, no qualifiers, (c) middle market practice: balanced approach with carve-outs for fundamental reps. Include sample language for key representations (financial statements, contracts, IP ownership, compliance) and indemnification provisions with variations for different risk profiles and deal sizes.
