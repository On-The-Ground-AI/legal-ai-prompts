# AI Prompt Pack for Shareholder Disputes & Corporate Litigation
### Optimised for Microsoft Copilot, Claude, ChatGPT & Legal AI Tools

---

> **Who this is for:** Lawyers advising minority shareholders, majority shareholders, companies, and directors in shareholder disputes, deadlocks, oppression claims, and related corporate litigation.
>
> ⚠ **Disclaimer:** These prompts are starting points, not legal advice. Shareholder disputes are highly fact-specific and jurisdiction-sensitive. Every output must be reviewed by a qualified lawyer. Nothing here constitutes legal advice.

## Section A: Assessing the Dispute

**PROMPT SD-A1 — Initial Shareholder Dispute Assessment**
Act as a corporate litigation specialist. My client has a shareholder dispute in [JURISDICTION]. The key facts are: [DESCRIBE — who are the shareholders, what are their respective holdings, what is the company's business, what has happened to cause the dispute, what does my client want to achieve]. Provide: 1. A preliminary map of the available legal routes (oppression claim, derivative action, breach of shareholders' agreement, winding up, etc.). 2. Which route(s) are most likely to succeed based on the facts. 3. What evidence and documents my client will need. 4. The key risks of each route. 5. Whether there are any urgent steps required (interim injunction, preservation of assets, urgent company law applications). 6. Whether negotiation or mediation should be explored first and why.

---

**PROMPT SD-A2 — Shareholder Agreement Breach Analysis**
Act as a corporate litigation lawyer. I am advising a shareholder whose rights under a Shareholders' Agreement have allegedly been breached. The Shareholders' Agreement contains the following relevant provisions: [PASTE OR DESCRIBE KEY CLAUSES]. The alleged breach is: [DESCRIBE — what the other party has done or failed to do]. Analyse: 1. Whether the conduct described constitutes a breach of the Shareholders' Agreement. 2. What remedies are available (damages, injunction, specific performance, buyout). 3. Whether any notice or cure provisions must be followed before bringing a claim. 4. The limitation period for the claim. 5. Whether arbitration or court proceedings apply under the dispute resolution clause. 6. Strengths and weaknesses of my client's position.

**PROMPT SD-A3 — Valuation Methodology for Minority Buyout**
Act as a corporate finance and legal specialist. My client's shares are to be purchased as part of a minority shareholder buyout ordered by the court / agreed between the parties. The company is: [DESCRIBE — industry, stage, profitability, whether it is a trading company or holding company]. I need to understand the main valuation methodologies that might apply: 1. Describe each methodology (DCF, comparable company analysis, net asset value, earnings multiple). 2. Which methodology is most appropriate for this type of company and why. 3. What discount (if any) should apply to a minority shareholding, and how do courts in [JURISDICTION] typically approach this? 4. What financial information will be needed for the valuation? 5. Draft a brief letter of instruction to a forensic accountant / valuation expert.

## Section B: Oppression & Unfair Prejudice

**PROMPT SD-B1 — Oppression Claim Merits Assessment**
Act as a litigation lawyer specialising in company law. My client is a minority shareholder holding [X%] of [COMPANY NAME], incorporated in [JURISDICTION]. The majority shareholders and/or directors have done the following: [LIST THE ALLEGED ACTS — e.g. excluded my client from management, failed to pay dividends, paid excessive remuneration to connected parties, diluted my client's shareholding unfairly, diverted business opportunities to related companies]. Assess: 1. Whether these facts support a claim for minority shareholder oppression / unfair prejudice under [JURISDICTION] law (e.g. Section 216 Companies Act (Singapore) / Section 994 Companies Act 2006 (UK)). 2. The elements that must be proved. 3. The remedies likely available (buyout order, injunction, winding up, other). 4. The strength of each aspect of the claim (Strong / Moderate / Weak). 5. Any defences the majority is likely to raise. 6. Recommended next steps.

---

**PROMPT SD-B2 — Oppression — Evidence Gathering Checklist**
I am gathering evidence for a minority shareholder oppression claim. The key allegations are: [LIST]. Draft a comprehensive evidence-gathering checklist covering: 1. Corporate documents — board minutes, shareholder meeting minutes, financial statements, management accounts. 2. Communications — emails, WhatsApp messages, board papers, presentations. 3. Financial records — bank statements, director loan accounts, related party transactions. 4. Third-party records — contracts with connected parties, payroll records. 5. Witnesses — who can give evidence and on what. For each category: explain why the evidence is relevant and how to obtain it (from the company, from third parties, via discovery). Flag any documents that may be at risk of destruction and require urgent preservation steps.

---

**PROMPT SD-B3 — Director Misconduct Analysis**
Act as a corporate litigation specialist. I am investigating possible misconduct by [DIRECTOR NAME], a director of [COMPANY NAME]. The alleged conduct is: [DESCRIBE — e.g. self-dealing, diverting business opportunities, paying improper remuneration, causing the company to enter into transactions at undervalue, authorising fraudulent transactions]. Analyse: 1. Whether the alleged conduct constitutes a breach of the director's duties under [JURISDICTION] company law. 2. The specific duty or duties breached. 3. The remedies available to the company (disgorgement of profits, equitable compensation, injunction, disqualification). 4. Whether a derivative action or a direct company action is the appropriate vehicle. 5. Any limitation or procedural hurdles. 6. Whether there may be criminal liability (fraud, dishonesty, market misconduct).

## Section C: Deadlock & Exit Mechanisms

**PROMPT SD-C1 — Deadlock Analysis & Exit Options**
Act as a corporate lawyer. My client is a [X%] shareholder in a [50/50 / other split] joint venture that has reached a deadlock on [DESCRIBE THE DEADLOCK ISSUE]. The Shareholders' Agreement [DOES / DOES NOT] contain a deadlock provision. The existing provision states: [PASTE IF APPLICABLE]. Advise on: 1. Whether the existing deadlock mechanism is triggered. 2. If no mechanism exists, what options are available under the general law (court-ordered winding up, negotiated buyout, mediation). 3. The pros and cons of each exit route for my client. 4. Whether my client should trigger a "shoot-out" / Russian roulette provision (if available) and the risks of doing so. 5. What my client's minimum acceptable outcome looks like and how to position for it.

---

**PROMPT SD-C2 — Just and Equitable Winding Up**
Act as a company law specialist. My client wishes to petition for the winding up of [COMPANY NAME] on just and equitable grounds under [Section 254 Companies Act (Singapore) / Section 122(1)(g) Insolvency Act 1986 (UK) — specify]. The facts supporting the petition are: [DESCRIBE]. Assess: 1. Whether the facts support a just and equitable winding up petition (consider: breakdown of mutual trust, deadlock, quasi-partnership, loss of substratum). 2. Whether the court is likely to grant the order or instead grant a lesser remedy (buyout order). 3. The key cases in [JURISDICTION] that will be relevant. 4. The procedural steps to bring the petition. 5. The risks for my client (security for costs, being found to have acted in bad faith, etc.).

## Section D: Interim Remedies in Shareholder Disputes

**PROMPT SD-D1 — Freezing / Mareva Injunction Application**
I am advising a shareholder who believes the opposing party is dissipating or removing assets from the company to frustrate any future judgment. The facts supporting urgency are: [DESCRIBE]. Draft: 1. A summary of the legal test for a freezing / Mareva injunction in [JURISDICTION]. 2. The evidence needed to satisfy the court that there is a real risk of dissipation. 3. An affidavit outline addressing each element of the test. 4. The proposed terms of the injunction (what assets, what value, what exceptions for ordinary business and legal costs). 5. The undertakings the applicant must give. 6. Any practical steps to take before the ex parte hearing.

---

**PROMPT SD-D2 — Derivative Action — Leave Application**
My client wishes to bring a statutory derivative action on behalf of [COMPANY NAME] against [WRONGDOER — director / third party] under [Section 216A Companies Act (Singapore) / Part 11 Companies Act 2006 (UK) — specify]. The basis of the claim is: [DESCRIBE]. Draft a framework for the leave application covering: 1. The statutory test the court will apply. 2. The evidence my client must file (notice to company, bona fides, prima facie case). 3. Likely arguments the company or wrongdoer will raise in opposition. 4. How to address those arguments. 5. What happens to costs if leave is granted — who pays?
