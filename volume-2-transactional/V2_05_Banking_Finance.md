## Loan Facility Agreements

**PROMPT 040 — Term Loan Facility Agreement (Corporate Borrower)**

Prepare a comprehensive term loan facility agreement for a Singapore corporate borrower, including: (1) Loan Particulars: (a) lender identification and facility agent role, (b) loan amount and currency (SGD, USD), (c) drawdown mechanics (single/multiple drawdowns, notice period, conditions precedent), (d) interest rate (fixed vs floating SIBOR/SORA-linked), (e) interest calculation and payment schedule (quarterly, semi-annual), (f) commitment fee on undrawn balance; (2) Drawdown Mechanics: (a) loan request notice (3-5 business days advance), (b) representations true at drawdown, (c) no default or material adverse change, (d) completion of legal due diligence, (e) delivery of documentation (compliance certificates, board resolutions); (3) Use of Proceeds: (a) specific permitted uses (capital expenditure, acquisition, working capital, refinancing), (b) restrictions on alternative uses (no related party lending, no acquisitions without consent), (c) drawdown limitations if use of proceeds not achieved; (4) Repayment Terms: (a) term to maturity (3-5-7 year tenor), (b) amortization schedule (equal quarterly/semi-annual installments typical), (c) bullet maturity (full repayment at end), (d) prepayment rights and prepayment penalties (make-whole premium if applicable), (e) mandatory prepayment triggers (asset sales, equity issuance, insurance proceeds); (5) Financial Covenants: (a) leverage ratio (total debt/EBITDA, maximum ratio e.g., 3.5x), (b) interest coverage ratio (EBITDA/interest, minimum e.g., 2.5x), (c) minimum cash balance, (d) debt service coverage ratio, (e) capital expenditure cap, (f) testing frequency (quarterly, semi-annual); (6) Negative Covenants: (a) restrictions on additional debt (no new borrowing above threshold), (b) restrictions on asset disposals (no sale-leaseback, no asset sales above threshold), (c) restrictions on mergers/acquisitions (requiring lender consent), (d) restrictions on dividend payments (permitted only if ratios complied with), (e) restrictions on related party transactions; (7) Affirmative Covenants: (a) financial reporting (audited annual accounts within 4 months, quarterly management accounts within 60 days), (b) compliance with laws and permits, (c) maintenance of insurance (property, liability, key person), (d) compliance with environmental laws, (e) notification of material adverse change, (f) facility agent inspection rights; (8) Representations & Warranties: (a) organization and good standing, (b) authority and non-contravention of loan agreement, (c) financial statements accuracy and fairness, (d) no litigation threatening repayment, (e) compliance with laws, (f) no material adverse change since baseline date; (9) Conditions Precedent: (a) due diligence completion and lender satisfaction, (b) representations true and correct at closing, (c) no material adverse change, (d) execution of definitive facility agreement and security documents, (e) third-party consents obtained (shareholder approval if required), (f) legal opinions delivered; (10) Events of Default: (a) payment default (failure to pay principal/interest on time), (b) financial covenant breach (leverage, coverage ratios), (c) cross-default (default under other financing), (d) material misrepresentation (breach of reps/warranties), (e) change of control (without lender consent), (f) insolvency or bankruptcy, (g) litigation threatening repayment; (11) Remedies upon Default: (a) acceleration of full loan amount, (b) increased interest rate (default interest typically 200-300 bps over coupon), (c) enforcement of security (sale of collateral), (d) injunctive relief to prevent dissipation of assets, (e) expense recovery (legal, accounting, investigation costs); (12) Security & Guarantee: (a) first ranking charge over specified assets (real property, equipment, IP, shares of subsidiaries), (b) personal guarantee by borrower's founder/shareholder if SME, (c) subsidiary guarantee if group borrowing, (d) pledge of shares in holding company; (13) Fees: (a) facility establishment fee (1-2% of loan amount, paid upfront), (b) commitment fee on undrawn balance (0.5-1.0% per annum), (c) early repayment penalties (make-whole if applicable, waived for certain prepayments), (d) amendment/consent fees (if lender consent required for covenant waiver); (14) Assignment & Transfer: (a) lender right to assign loan to other financial institutions, (b) restrictions on borrower assignment, (c) notice of assignment to borrower; (15) Governing Law & Dispute Resolution: (a) Singapore law, (b) Singapore courts jurisdiction, (c) arbitration optional (SIAC arbitration); (16) Relationship with Other Agreements: (a) cross-default to other financing, (b) pari passu security ranking with other secured debt. Include detailed schedules: (a) financial reporting requirements, (b) information to be provided pre-closing, (c) definition of material terms (leverage ratio, EBITDA calculation, material adverse change), (d) properties and assets schedule. Provide guidance on negotiation points and market variations based on deal size and borrower risk profile.

**PROMPT 043 — Financial Covenants Deep Dive & Covenant Waivers**

Prepare comprehensive guidance on designing, calculating, and managing financial covenants in loan facilities, including: (1) Leverage Ratio (Total Debt / EBITDA): (a) definition of total debt (all principal amounts outstanding, excluding trade payables), (b) definition of EBITDA (earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, amortization, add-backs for one-time items), (c) typical leverage ranges (3.5x for stable businesses, 2.0-2.5x for lower risk), (d) testing frequency (quarterly, semi-annual, annual), (e) stepped covenants (declining leverage ratios as company matures); (2) Interest Coverage Ratio (EBITDA / Interest Expense): (a) definition of interest expense (cash interest on all debt), (b) typical minimums (2.0-3.0x depending on industry), (c) add-backs for non-cash interest, (d) relevance for serviceable debt capacity; (3) Debt Service Coverage Ratio (Operating Cash Flow / Debt Service): (a) total debt service includes principal + interest, (b) operating cash flow before working capital changes, (c) minimum ratios (1.25x-1.50x typical), (d) lender comfort with ability to service debt; (4) Minimum Liquidity Covenant: (a) unrestricted cash balance minimum (e.g., $1M), (b) ensures borrower has working capital for operations, (c) prevents asset stripping before insolvency; (5) Capital Expenditure Covenant: (a) annual cap on CapEx spending, (b) ensures cash retention for debt repayment, (c) flexibility for essential maintenance vs growth capex; (6) Asset Disposal Restrictions: (a) no sale of material assets without lender consent, (b) proceeds from asset sales mandatory prepayment of debt; (7) Calculation Mechanics: (a) GAAP/IFRS consistency with financial statements, (b) quarterly financial data: actual for completed quarters, projected for quarters not yet closed, (c) rolling twelve month (LTM) calculations for consistency, (d) pro forma adjustments for acquisitions/divestitures completed within measurement period; (8) Add-Backs & Adjustments: (a) one-time costs (restructuring, M&A related), (b) non-recurring items (litigation settlements, asset write-downs), (c) normalized run-rate EBITDA for startups/recent acquisitions, (d) removal of goodwill impairments, e-commerce inventory adjustments; (9) Covenant Package Variations by Borrower Type: (a) Stable Mature Company: tighter covenants (leverage < 2.5x, coverage > 3.0x), looser testing (semi-annual), (b) Growth Company: looser leverage (< 4.0x), tighter coverage (> 2.0x), quarterly testing, (c) Venture-Backed Startup: focus on liquidity/runway, EBITDA not relevant, revenue-based covenants if profitable, (d) Turnaround Situation: asset-based lending tied to borrowing base; (10) Covenant Holiday Periods: (a) covenant waiver for first 6-12 months post-closing, (b) allows time for integration/stabilization, (c) partial waiver (testing suspended but remains defined); (11) Covenant Waivers & Amendments: (a) if breach occurs, lender can waive breach or demand cure, (b) waiver rights: (i) automatic waiver for immaterial breaches < $100K, (ii) lender discretion for material breaches, (iii) amendment to covenant terms if anticipated ongoing breach; (c) waiver procedure: borrower notice of breach, request for waiver, lender discretion to grant, (d) waiver fees: lender charges amendment fee (0.25%-1% of facility) to compensate for covenant reset; (12) Cure Rights: (a) after notice of breach, borrower has X days to cure (typically 5-10 days for payment defaults, 15-30 days for covenant breaches), (b) examples: reduce CapEx to comply with cap, prepay debt to lower leverage, inject equity to improve ratio; (13) Documenting Covenants: (a) detailed definitions schedule, (b) examples of calculation for representative periods, (c) certificate requirements (quarterly officer certificates certifying compliance), (d) lender right to conduct audit/recomputation if disputed; (14) Covenant Triggers for Lender Actions: (a) leverage > X triggers acceleration, (b) interest coverage < Y triggers mandatory prepayment from cash generation, (c) liquidity < Z triggers asset sale mandate; (15) Practical Enforcement: (a) lender monitoring through financial reporting, (b) proactive management discussion with borrower if approaching threshold, (c) covenant waivers often negotiated in advance if anticipated breach, (d) amendment fees and fees for waivers commonplace in market. Include detailed covenant calculation examples with actual EBITDA reconciliations, covenant compliance tracking spreadsheets, and sample covenant waiver request templates.

(4) Guarantor Defenses: (a) guarantor remains liable even if borrower claims illegality/lack of authority (guarantor cannot raise borrower's defenses), (b) guarantor remains liable even if borrower claims modification of loan (e.g., interest rate increase), (c) guarantor remains liable even if borrower's assets depleted through fraud/mismanagement, (d) only defenses available: (i) payment/satisfaction of debt, (ii) material modification of loan terms without guarantor consent, (iii) fraudulent conduct by lender; (5) Guarantor's Liability: (a) joint and several liability (each guarantor liable for full amount if multiple guarantors), (b) guarantor remains liable until loan fully discharged, (c) liability continues even if guarantee period expires (if principal amount increases); (6) Release of Guarantee: (a) guarantor's written request for release (lender typically reluctant to release), (b) release upon loan repayment/satisfaction, (c) release if guarantor is released from share ownership (change of control, shares sold), (d) partial release if loan principal reduced; (8) Financial Covenants Binding Guarantor: (a) if borrower violates covenants, guarantor's liability triggered, (b) guarantor's obligation to provide updated financial information, (c) guarantor's obligation to inject capital if borrower's financial condition deteriorates; (9) Multiple Guarantors: (a) joint and several liability (each liable for full amount), (b) guarantor cross-indemnities (if one guarantor forced to pay, right of contribution from co-guarantors), (c) surviving guarantors' liability if one guarantor dies/becomes incapacitated; (10) Subordinated Guarantees: (a) guarantor subordinates its claims against borrower to lender's claims, (b) guarantor agrees to stand behind other creditors in priority for recovery; (11) Limited Guarantees (Alternative Structure): (a) guarantee limited to % of principal or fixed amount cap, (b) guarantee limited to specific assets (personal residence exempted), (c) guarantee expires on specific date (e.g., 2 years post-loan repayment); (12) Guarantor Enforcement: (a) lender can sue guarantor directly upon borrower's payment default, (b) lender can take enforcement action against guarantor's assets (personal bank account, home if not protected), (c) guarantor has burden of proving payment/discharge; (13) Guarantor's Personal Circumstances: (a) guarantor's marital status and spouse consent (if applicable in jurisdiction), (b) guarantor's insolvency and personal creditor rights, (c) guarantor's defenses based on personal hardship (typically not recognized legally). Include guarantee template language with variations for different guarantor risk profiles and suggested guarantor protective provisions (e.g., guarantor carve-outs for specific items, limited exposure for guarantor's spouse).
