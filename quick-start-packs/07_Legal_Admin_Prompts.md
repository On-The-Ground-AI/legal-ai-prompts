# AI Prompt Pack for Legal Administrators, Paralegals & Legal Support Staff
### Optimised for Microsoft Copilot in Word, Outlook, Teams & Excel

---

> **Who this is for:** Legal secretaries, paralegals, legal operations coordinators, office managers at law firms, and administrative staff supporting in-house legal teams. You do not need to be a lawyer to use these prompts — they are designed to help you produce high-quality, professional work faster.
>
> **The golden rule:** AI is your assistant, not your replacement. Always have a supervising lawyer review any legal document before it is sent, filed, or used. For general administrative outputs (calendars, emails, summaries), use your own judgement.
>
> ⚠ **Disclaimer:** Do not paste confidential client information into public AI tools (free ChatGPT, free Claude.ai, etc.). Use your firm's approved enterprise AI tool, Microsoft Copilot with your firm's data boundaries, or anonymise all details before using any AI. When in doubt, ask your supervising lawyer.

## Section A: Document Management & File Organisation

**PROMPT A-A1 — File Opening Checklist**
Generate a new client / new matter file opening checklist for a [LAW FIRM / LEGAL DEPARTMENT]. The checklist should cover: 1. Client identity verification and KYC documents required. 2. Conflict of interest check — who to check and how to record the outcome. 3. Engagement letter / client care letter — to be sent before work starts. 4. Fee arrangement documentation. 5. File reference and billing code setup. 6. Document management system setup (folder structure, access permissions). 7. Any court or registry registration required. 8. Matter-specific considerations for [TYPE OF WORK, e.g. litigation / conveyancing / corporate]. Present as a numbered checklist that can be printed and signed off by the responsible lawyer and admin.

*Copilot Tip: Open a new Word document and use this prompt in Copilot to create an editable checklist. Save it as your firm's standard template.*

---

**PROMPT A-A2 — Document Naming Convention Guide**
Create a document naming convention and folder structure guide for the legal department / law firm's document management system. The team handles: [DESCRIBE — types of work: contracts, litigation, corporate, property]. The naming convention should: be consistent and easy to sort chronologically, identify the matter / client, document type, version number, and date, work across Word, PDF, and Excel documents, and be short enough to be practical. Provide: (1) a sample naming convention formula, (2) 10 examples applying the formula to common legal documents, and (3) a recommended folder structure for a typical matter file.

---

**PROMPT A-A3 — Matter Status Summary**
I need to prepare a matter status update for [LAWYER NAME]. The matter is [DESCRIBE BRIEFLY]. Key events so far: [LIST IN DATE ORDER — what happened, what was filed, what was received, what is outstanding]. Prepare a concise one-page matter status summary table with: [Date | Event | Description | Next Step | Deadline | Responsible]. Highlight any overdue items in the "Next Steps" column.

*Copilot Tip: Use this in Copilot in Excel to create a live matter tracker table.*

---

**PROMPT A-A4 — File Closure Checklist**
Create a file closure checklist for [MATTER TYPE] at [FIRM / DEPARTMENT NAME]. The checklist should confirm: 1. All work has been completed and the client/business has been notified. 2. Final invoice has been issued and any outstanding fees resolved. 3. Original documents have been returned to the client or stored per policy. 4. Originals and copies retained in the file per the firm's retention policy. 5. Electronic files are archived and access is restricted. 6. Any court or registry records have been updated or closed. 7. Client satisfaction / feedback obtained. 8. Lessons learned noted for future matters. Present as a sign-off checklist with spaces for initials and dates.

## Section B: Correspondence & Communication

**PROMPT A-B1 — Professional Email Drafting**
Draft a professional email on behalf of [LAWYER NAME], [TITLE], at [FIRM / COMPANY NAME]. The email is to: [RECIPIENT NAME], [TITLE], at [ORGANISATION]. The purpose of the email is: [DESCRIBE — e.g. to acknowledge receipt of their letter, to request a document, to arrange a meeting, to follow up on outstanding action]. Key points to include: [LIST]. Tone: [Professional and courteous / Firm and direct / Warm and relationship-building — choose one]. Keep the email under 150 words. Use a professional sign-off.

---

**PROMPT A-B2 — Meeting Request with Agenda**
Draft a meeting request email and agenda for a [TYPE OF MEETING — e.g. client update call / contract negotiation / internal legal team meeting / court preparation session]. Meeting participants: [LIST — names and organisations]. Proposed date and time: [DATE/TIME AND TIME ZONE]. Duration: [e.g. 60 minutes]. The meeting will cover: [LIST AGENDA ITEMS]. Attach an agenda document that includes: meeting title, date/time, participants, agenda items with estimated time for each, pre-reading or preparation required, and action items from last meeting (if applicable).

---

**PROMPT A-B3 — Client Update Letter**
Draft a formal client update letter from [LAWYER NAME] at [FIRM NAME] to [CLIENT NAME] regarding [MATTER NAME]. The letter should: open with a brief matter reference, explain in plain English what has happened recently in the matter [DESCRIBE RECENT DEVELOPMENTS], state clearly what the next steps are and what the client needs to do (if anything), give an approximate timeline for next developments, and close professionally. Maximum 1 page. Use formal letter format with date, reference number, and sign-off. Avoid legal jargon.

---

**PROMPT A-B4 — Follow-Up Chaser Email**
Draft a polite but firm follow-up email for [LAWYER NAME] to send to [RECIPIENT] who has not responded to a previous communication sent on [DATE] about [TOPIC]. The email should: reference the previous communication, state clearly what response or action is needed, give a specific deadline for response, and indicate the consequence if no response is received [e.g. we will proceed on the assumption you have no objection / we will be required to escalate]. Tone: professional and direct, not aggressive.

## Section C: Scheduling, Deadlines & Diary Management

**PROMPT A-C1 — Court Deadline Calculator Framework**
Act as a legal administrator. I need to track all procedural deadlines in [MATTER NAME]. The key trigger event was [DESCRIBE — e.g. claim was served on [DATE] / judgment was delivered on [DATE] / contract was signed on [DATE]]. The applicable procedural rules are [ROC 2021 Singapore / English CPR / other — specify]. Please list all the key deadlines that now run from this event, including: deadline type, number of days/weeks allowed, due date, and whether weekends and public holidays are excluded. Present as a table. Flag any deadline that falls within the next 14 days.

*Note: Always verify procedural deadlines against the actual court rules. AI tools may not have the most current version of procedural rules. Never rely solely on AI for court deadlines — these are non-negotiable.*

---

**PROMPT A-C2 — Hearing Preparation Checklist**
Create a hearing preparation checklist for [HEARING TYPE — e.g. trial / CMC / interlocutory hearing / arbitration] scheduled for [DATE] in [MATTER NAME] before [COURT / TRIBUNAL]. The hearing is in [COURTROOM / CHAMBERS / VIRTUAL]. Prepare a checklist covering: 1. Documents and bundles required — authorities bundle, agreed bundle of documents, opening submissions. 2. Physical preparation — number of copies, pagination, tabs, ring binders. 3. Logistics — location, parking, robing requirements (if applicable), IT equipment for virtual hearings. 4. Personnel — who is attending, any witnesses to be briefed. 5. The night before and morning of the hearing — final checks. Format as a tickable checklist.

---

**PROMPT A-C3 — Limitation Date Reminder System**
I manage deadlines for a law firm / legal department handling [TYPES OF MATTERS]. Create a limitation date monitoring policy and reminder template covering: 1. How to identify and record the primary limitation date for each new matter. 2. A tiered reminder schedule (e.g. 3 months before, 1 month before, 2 weeks before, 1 week before, 3 days before). 3. Who is responsible for monitoring each reminder. 4. What action is required at each reminder stage. 5. An escalation procedure if the supervising lawyer does not acknowledge a reminder. 6. How to document that the reminder was sent and acknowledged.

## Section D: Research & Summarisation Support

**PROMPT A-D1 — Document Summary for Lawyer Review**
I need to prepare a summary of the following document for the supervising lawyer's review. Document: [PASTE OR DESCRIBE — e.g. a long contract, a court judgment, a regulatory notice, a client letter]. Please provide: 1. A one-paragraph executive summary (what is this document about and why does it matter). 2. A table of key points: [Section / Topic | Summary | Action Required (Y/N)]. 3. Any deadlines, dates, or figures that stand out. 4. Any sections the lawyer should read personally in full. Keep the summary under 1 page.

*Copilot Tip: Use this in Copilot in Word. Paste the document, then ask Copilot to summarise it using this structure.*

---

**PROMPT A-D2 — Comparative Table from Multiple Documents**
I have [NUMBER] documents that I need to compare. Documents: [DESCRIBE — e.g. 3 supplier quotes / 4 NDAs from different counterparties / 5 employment contracts for different employees]. For each document, extract the following information and present it in a comparison table: [LIST THE FIELDS — e.g. Parties | Effective Date | Term | Key Price | Payment Terms | Termination Clause | Governing Law | Any unusual terms]. Highlight in the table any significant differences between the documents.

*Copilot Tip: This works very well in Copilot in Excel. Generate the table, then use Excel's filter and sort to identify the most important variations.*

---

**PROMPT A-D3 — Meeting Notes / Action Items**
I attended [OR: I have a recording/transcript of] a meeting on [DATE] about [TOPIC]. The participants were: [LIST]. Here are my rough notes from the meeting: [PASTE NOTES OR TRANSCRIPT]. Please: 1. Clean up and organise the notes into a professional meeting record. 2. Produce a clear action items table: [Action | Owner | Deadline]. 3. Identify any decisions made during the meeting. 4. Flag any issues that were raised but not resolved and need follow-up. Format the output as a formal meeting record I can circulate to participants.

## Section E: Templates & Standard Documents

**PROMPT A-E1 — Engagement Letter Template**
Draft a client engagement letter template for [FIRM NAME], a [JURISDICTION] law firm practising [AREAS OF LAW]. The letter should be suitable for individual clients [AND / OR] corporate clients. Cover: 1. Scope of engagement — what the firm is and is not being instructed to do. 2. Fee arrangement — hourly rates / fixed fee / contingency [as applicable]. 3. Billing terms — when invoices are issued, payment terms, interest on late payment. 4. Responsibilities of the client. 5. Confidentiality and data protection. 6. File retention policy. 7. Complaints procedure. 8. Regulatory information [Law Society of Singapore / SRA / relevant regulator]. Note: leave [SQUARE BRACKETS] for firm-specific information to be inserted.

---

**PROMPT A-E2 — Invoice / Bill Narrative Drafting**
Draft a professional invoice narrative for legal services rendered in [MATTER NAME]. The work done during [BILLING PERIOD] included: [LIST THE KEY TASKS — e.g. drafting contract, attending client meeting, reviewing due diligence documents, preparing court submissions]. Total hours: [NUMBER] at [RATE] per hour = [AMOUNT]. The narrative should: describe the work in professional but plain terms, justify the time spent, be suitable for a client to read and understand, and not disclose privileged details. Maximum 3-4 sentences per line item.
