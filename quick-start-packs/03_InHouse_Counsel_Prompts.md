# AI Prompt Pack for In-House Counsel, General Counsel & Legal Operations
### Optimised for Microsoft Copilot, Claude, ChatGPT & Legal AI Tools

---

> **Who this is for:** General Counsel, Deputy General Counsel, Senior Legal Counsel, Legal Operations Managers, and any in-house lawyer advising a business.
>
> **How to use this pack:** Copy any prompt. Open Copilot in Word, Teams, Outlook, or your browser. Paste the prompt. Replace all [BRACKETED TEXT] with your real information. Always review AI output before using it professionally.
>
> ⚠ **Disclaimer:** These prompts are starting points, not legal advice. AI can make mistakes, miss jurisdiction-specific requirements, or generate inaccurate legal analysis. Every output must be reviewed by a qualified lawyer. The organisation using these prompts is solely responsible for any work product produced. Nothing here constitutes legal advice.

## Section A: Contract Management

**PROMPT IH-A1 — Contract Playbook Builder**
Act as a senior in-house counsel with 15 years of experience. Create a contract negotiation playbook for our [CONTRACT TYPE, e.g. SaaS subscription / vendor services / professional services / distribution agreement]. For each of the following clauses, provide three columns: (a) Our Preferred Position, (b) Our Fallback Position, (c) What We Will Never Accept. Clauses to cover: 1. Limitation of liability 2. Indemnification 3. IP ownership and licence grants 4. Data protection and processing obligations 5. Confidentiality 6. Term and termination 7. Payment terms and late payment 8. Dispute resolution 9. Governing law 10. Warranties and representations. Format as a table. Note where your positions differ between supplier-side and customer-side contracts.

*Copilot Tip: Paste this into Copilot in Word. Then ask Copilot: "Add a column for 'Business Justification' explaining why this position matters to us."*

---

**PROMPT IH-A2 — Inbound Contract Fast Review**
I am in-house counsel and have received a contract from a counterparty. I need a rapid review in the next 30 minutes. Review the following contract and flag: 1. The top 5 provisions that deviate unfavourably from market standard. 2. Any clauses that are missing but should be standard (e.g. limitation of liability, IP ownership, GDPR/data processing, termination for convenience). 3. Any unlimited liability exposure or unusual indemnities. 4. Key commercial terms (payment, term, renewal, auto-renewal traps). 5. A one-paragraph recommendation: Approve as-is / Negotiate / Reject. [PASTE CONTRACT OR KEY CLAUSES]

---

**PROMPT IH-A3 — Contract Abstraction for Database**
Act as a legal operations specialist. I need to extract key data from the following contract for our contract management system. Extract into a structured table: Contract Title | Contract Type | Parties | Effective Date | Expiry Date | Auto-Renewal (Y/N) | Notice Period for Termination | Governing Law | Dispute Resolution | Key Commercial Terms | Payment Terms | Liability Cap | Data Processing Agreement Required (Y/N) | Jurisdiction-Specific Compliance Notes. [PASTE CONTRACT]

*Copilot Tip: Use this prompt in Copilot in Excel to extract data from multiple contracts and build a contract register.*

---

**PROMPT IH-A4 — Vendor Contract Risk Tiering**
Act as a legal risk officer. I need to classify our vendor contracts by risk level to prioritise my team's review. Based on the following contract summary information: [LIST VENDOR NAME, CONTRACT TYPE, VALUE, DATA PROCESSED, SERVICES PROVIDED for each vendor]. Tier each contract as Tier 1 (High Risk — requires full legal review), Tier 2 (Medium Risk — standard review with checklist), or Tier 3 (Low Risk — light-touch or automated review). Explain your rationale for each tier based on: financial exposure, data risk, regulatory exposure, and strategic importance. Present as a prioritised table.

**PROMPT IH-A5 — Contract Renewal Decision Brief**
I need to decide whether to renew, renegotiate, or terminate the following vendor contract. Contract details: [DESCRIBE — vendor, services, current terms, contract value, expiry date]. Relevant context: [DESCRIBE — performance issues, pricing concerns, market alternatives, strategic importance to the business]. Draft a one-page decision brief covering: 1. Summary of current contract and performance. 2. Pros and cons of renewing vs. renegotiating vs. terminating. 3. Key negotiating points if we renew. 4. Notice period and steps required to terminate. 5. Recommended course of action with rationale.

## Section B: Legal Risk Management

**PROMPT IH-B1 — Legal Risk Register Entry**
Create a legal risk register entry for the following issue: - Issue: [DESCRIBE THE LEGAL RISK OR DISPUTE IN PLAIN TERMS] - Business Unit Affected: [NAME] - How the risk arose: [DESCRIBE] - Likelihood of adverse outcome: [%] - Estimated financial exposure: [RANGE — best case / worst case] - Current status: [DESCRIBE] - Mitigating actions already taken: [DESCRIBE] - Proposed next steps: [DESCRIBE] - Owner: [NAME / ROLE] - Next review date: [DATE]. Assign an overall risk rating (Red / Amber / Green) with justification. Suggest any additional mitigation steps not already listed.

---

**PROMPT IH-B2 — Legal Function Dashboard Summary**
Act as a legal operations manager. Prepare a monthly legal dashboard summary for the following data: [DESCRIBE OR PASTE DATA — number of contracts reviewed, average turnaround time, matters opened and closed, external legal spend, headcount, any significant regulatory developments]. The summary should be suitable for the CFO and COO. Present as a one-page narrative with: headline metrics in a table, 3 key highlights, and 3 items requiring leadership attention. Keep it jargon-free.

*Copilot Tip: Use this in Copilot in Word or PowerPoint. Ask Copilot to "Convert to a presentation slide" after drafting.*

---

**PROMPT IH-B3 — Escalation Memo to Board**
Draft an escalation memo from the General Counsel to the Board of Directors regarding the following legal matter: [DESCRIBE — nature of the issue, when it arose, what has been done so far, current status]. The memo should: summarise the issue in non-technical language, state the potential financial and reputational exposure, outline the options available, identify the decision the Board is being asked to make, and recommend a course of action. Maximum 1 page.

---

**PROMPT IH-B4 — Pre-Transaction Legal Health Check**
Act as an in-house counsel preparing for a [FUNDRAISING / ACQUISITION / PARTNERSHIP / IPO]. My company is [DESCRIBE — stage, industry, jurisdiction]. Produce a legal health check framework covering the key areas investors / acquirers / partners will scrutinise: 1. Corporate structure and governance — any issues? 2. Cap table and share history — any gaps or disputes? 3. Key contracts — change of control provisions, consents required. 4. Intellectual property — ownership clear and registered? 5. Employment — key employee agreements, equity plans, disputes. 6. Regulatory and licences — current and in good standing? 7. Data and privacy — PDPA / GDPR compliance status. 8. Litigation — any pending or threatened matters. Rate each area Red / Amber / Green with notes on what needs to be fixed before the transaction.

## Section C: Regulatory & Policy Compliance

**PROMPT IH-C1 — Regulatory Change Impact Assessment**
A new regulation has been published: [NAME OF REGULATION / DESCRIBE KEY REQUIREMENTS]. My company operates in [INDUSTRY] in [JURISDICTION]. Assess the impact on our business: 1. Which parts of our business / operations does this regulation affect? 2. What are the key obligations we must comply with, and by when? 3. What changes to our policies, contracts, or processes will be needed? 4. Are there any exemptions or safe harbours we may be able to rely on? 5. What is the penalty for non-compliance? 6. Recommend a compliance action plan with owners and deadlines. Note if you need more information about our specific business operations to complete this assessment.

---

**PROMPT IH-C2 — Internal Policy Drafting**
Draft an internal [POLICY NAME, e.g. AI Use Policy / Whistleblowing Policy / Gifts and Entertainment Policy / Conflicts of Interest Policy] for [COMPANY NAME]. The company has [NUMBER] employees in [JURISDICTION(S)]. The policy should: state its purpose and scope, set out the rules clearly in plain English, give practical examples of what is and is not permitted, describe the approval or reporting process for borderline situations, state the consequences of non-compliance, and include an employee acknowledgement section. Note any [JURISDICTION]-specific legal requirements this policy must reflect.

*Copilot Tip: After drafting, ask Copilot: "Rewrite this policy at a Year 10 reading level" to make it accessible to all staff.*

---

**PROMPT IH-C3 — Regulatory Response / Regulator Letter**
Act as in-house counsel. I need to respond to the following query / notice from [REGULATOR NAME]: [DESCRIBE OR PASTE THE REGULATOR'S LETTER / QUERY]. Our position is: [DESCRIBE]. Draft a formal response letter that: acknowledges the regulator's query professionally, provides a clear and accurate factual account of our position, attaches or references the relevant supporting documents, addresses each question raised by the regulator, and adopts a cooperative tone while protecting our legal position. Note any facts or documents I need to supply before finalising.

## Section D: Employment & People Legal Matters

**PROMPT IH-D1 — Employment Contract Review Memo**
Review the following employment contract for a senior hire and prepare an internal memo: 1. Summary of key terms (role, compensation, benefits, notice period, start date). 2. Any terms that deviate from our standard employment contract. 3. Any terms that create unusual obligations for the company. 4. Non-compete and non-solicitation provisions — are they enforceable in [JURISDICTION]? Are they reasonable in scope? 5. IP assignment clause — does it adequately protect the company's ownership of work product? 6. Recommendation: Approve / Seek amendments / Flag for senior review. [PASTE CONTRACT OR KEY CLAUSES]

---

**PROMPT IH-D2 — Workplace Investigation Framework**
Act as an employment lawyer. I have received a complaint from [COMPLAINANT ROLE] against [RESPONDENT ROLE] regarding: [DESCRIBE THE ALLEGATION — e.g. harassment, discrimination, misconduct]. Draft a workplace investigation framework covering: 1. The steps to be taken in the investigation (in order). 2. Who should conduct the investigation and why. 3. What documents and records to gather. 4. Interview questions for the complainant, respondent, and any witnesses. 5. How to assess credibility and reach findings. 6. What outcome options are available if the complaint is substantiated / unsubstantiated. 7. Record-keeping and confidentiality obligations.

---

**PROMPT IH-D3 — Retrenchment / Redundancy Process Checklist**
Act as an employment lawyer in [JURISDICTION]. My company needs to conduct a retrenchment exercise affecting approximately [NUMBER] employees in [DEPARTMENT / LOCATION]. Generate a step-by-step legal compliance checklist covering: 1. Notification obligations to [MINISTRY OF MANPOWER / RELEVANT AUTHORITY]. 2. Selection process — fair and non-discriminatory criteria. 3. Notice period and payment in lieu of notice obligations. 4. Retrenchment benefit entitlements under [JURISDICTION] law. 5. CPF / superannuation obligations. 6. Outplacement obligations (if any). 7. Documentation required (redundancy letters, separation agreements). 8. Communication plan — what must be communicated, to whom, and when. 9. Any union or works council consultation requirements. Include key [JURISDICTION] statutory references.

## Section E: Commercial Advice & Business Partnering

**PROMPT IH-E1 — Business Unit Legal Briefing**
Draft a plain-English legal briefing for [BUSINESS UNIT, e.g. the Sales Team / Procurement Team / Marketing Team / HR Team] on the following topic: [LEGAL TOPIC, e.g. rules on giving gifts to clients / how to handle personal data under PDPA / what contracts the team can sign without legal sign-off / how to handle a customer complaint]. The briefing should: be written for a non-lawyer audience, use bullet points and plain English, give practical do's and don'ts with examples, state when the team must escalate to the legal team, and be no longer than one A4 page. Avoid legal jargon.

---

**PROMPT IH-E2 — Legal Sign-Off Framework**
Design a contract sign-off authority matrix for [COMPANY NAME]. The company's business involves [DESCRIBE]. Consider the following factors in setting thresholds: contract value, contract type (standard vs. non-standard), counterparty risk, data processing, and regulatory sensitivity. The matrix should specify: who can approve contracts at each level (e.g. business manager / legal counsel / General Counsel / Board), what supporting documentation or legal review is required at each level, and what types of contract require legal review regardless of value. Present as a table.

---

**PROMPT IH-E3 — Vendor Due Diligence Questionnaire**
Create a vendor due diligence questionnaire for [COMPANY NAME] to use when onboarding new third-party vendors. The company operates in [INDUSTRY] and is subject to [RELEVANT REGULATIONS, e.g. PDPA / MAS guidelines / GDPR]. The questionnaire should cover: 1. Company information and financial stability. 2. Data security and privacy practices. 3. Sub-contractor and supply chain management. 4. Business continuity and disaster recovery. 5. Anti-bribery and sanctions compliance. 6. Insurance coverage. 7. Right to audit and incident notification obligations. Format as a questionnaire with Yes/No/NA answers plus space for explanation.

## Section F: Reporting & Communication

**PROMPT IH-F1 — Quarterly Legal Report for Management**
Draft a quarterly legal report for [COMPANY NAME]'s management team / ExCo covering: - Period: [QUARTER AND YEAR] - Key matters handled: [LIST — new matters, closures, significant developments] - Contract pipeline: [DESCRIBE — volume and types] - External legal spend: [AMOUNT, vs budget] - Regulatory developments: [DESCRIBE new laws or guidance relevant to the business] - Litigation update: [DESCRIBE active matters and status] - Upcoming legal milestones: [LIST — contract renewals, regulatory deadlines, audit dates]. Write in a structured format suitable for a busy executive audience. Use plain English. Flag any matters requiring management decision.

---

**PROMPT IH-F2 — Legal Team Annual Review**
Help me prepare the legal department's annual review for [COMPANY NAME]. Draft a narrative covering: 1. Highlights and achievements of the legal team this year. 2. Key transactions and matters handled. 3. Risk issues identified and mitigated. 4. Legal spend vs. budget and key drivers of any variance. 5. Team developments (new hires, skills development, process improvements). 6. Key legal and regulatory changes affecting the business this year. 7. Priorities and goals for the coming year. Tone: professional but readable. Audience: CEO and Board. Length: 3-4 pages.
