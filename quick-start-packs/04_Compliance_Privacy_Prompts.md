# AI Prompt Pack for Compliance, Regulatory & Privacy Lawyers
### Optimised for Microsoft Copilot, Claude, ChatGPT & Legal AI Tools

---

> **Who this is for:** Compliance officers, privacy lawyers, data protection officers, regulatory lawyers, and anyone advising on PDPA (Singapore), GDPR (EU), MAS regulations, or corporate compliance programmes.
>
> **How to use this pack:** Copy any prompt. Open Copilot in Word, Teams, Outlook, or your browser. Paste the prompt. Replace all [BRACKETED TEXT] with your real information. Always review AI output before using it.
>
> ⚠ **Disclaimer:** These prompts are starting points, not legal advice. Regulatory requirements change frequently. Every output must be reviewed by a qualified compliance professional or lawyer before use. Nothing here constitutes legal or regulatory advice.

## Section A: Data Protection & Privacy (PDPA / GDPR / CCPA)

**PROMPT CP-A1 — Privacy Gap Analysis**
Act as a data protection lawyer specialising in [PDPA (Singapore) / GDPR (EU) / CCPA (California) — specify]. Conduct a structured gap analysis for the following organisation: - Organisation type: [e.g. SME, tech startup, MNC, financial institution] - Industry: [DESCRIBE] - Data processed: [DESCRIBE — types of personal data, volumes, sensitive data categories] - Current privacy practices: [DESCRIBE — how data is collected, stored, used, shared, retained] - Third parties involved: [DESCRIBE — cloud providers, processors, overseas transfers]. Identify: 1. Key compliance gaps with [REGULATION]. 2. Risk level for each gap (Critical / High / Medium / Low). 3. Recommended remediation action. 4. Suggested timeline and priority order. 5. Any mandatory notifications or registrations with the [PDPC / ICO / relevant authority] required. Present as a structured table.

*Copilot Tip: Use this in Copilot in Word. After generating the gap analysis, ask Copilot: "Turn this into a project plan with milestones and owners."*

---

**PROMPT CP-A2 — Data Processing Agreement (DPA) Review**
Act as a data protection lawyer. Review the following Data Processing Agreement (DPA) between [DATA CONTROLLER] and [DATA PROCESSOR]: [PASTE DPA OR KEY CLAUSES]. Assess: 1. Whether the DPA meets the mandatory requirements of [GDPR Article 28 / PDPA Second Schedule / specify]. 2. Any clauses that place unusual or unreasonable obligations on the [controller / processor]. 3. Whether the sub-processor provisions are adequate. 4. Whether the security obligations and breach notification timelines are appropriate. 5. Whether international data transfer mechanisms (SCCs, BCRs, PDPA adequacy) are properly addressed. Produce a redline recommendation table: [Clause | Issue | Recommended Change].

---

**PROMPT CP-A3 — Data Subject Access Request Response**
Act as a data protection officer. My organisation has received a Data Subject Access Request (DSAR) from [DATA SUBJECT — describe their relationship with the organisation, e.g. customer, ex-employee]. Request received: [DATE]. Summary of what they are asking for: [DESCRIBE]. Help me: 1. Confirm the applicable deadline for responding under [PDPA / GDPR — specify jurisdiction and timeline]. 2. Draft an acknowledgement letter to the data subject. 3. Create an internal checklist of steps to locate, review, and compile the data. 4. Identify any exemptions or redactions that may apply (legal professional privilege, third-party data, disproportionate effort). 5. Draft the final response letter. Flag any information I need from my IT or HR team before we can respond.

---

**PROMPT CP-A4 — Personal Data Breach Response Protocol**
Act as a data protection officer. My organisation has just discovered a potential personal data breach: [DESCRIBE — what happened, when it was discovered, what data is involved, how many data subjects affected, cause of breach]. Help me: 1. Assess whether this constitutes a notifiable breach under [PDPA / GDPR — specify]. 2. Confirm the notification deadline to [PDPC / ICO / relevant authority] if triggered. 3. Draft the regulator notification. 4. Assess whether affected individuals must be notified and draft that notification. 5. Produce a breach response action log covering the next 72 hours. 6. Suggest immediate containment and remediation steps. Note any facts that would change your assessment of notification obligations.

---

**PROMPT CP-A5 — Privacy Impact Assessment (PIA / DPIA) Framework**
Act as a privacy lawyer. I need to conduct a [Privacy Impact Assessment / Data Protection Impact Assessment] for the following new project or processing activity: [DESCRIBE THE PROJECT — what personal data will be processed, why, how, by whom, for how long]. Produce a DPIA/PIA framework covering: 1. Description of the processing activity. 2. Necessity and proportionality assessment — is this the least privacy-invasive way to achieve the goal? 3. Risk identification — what risks does this processing create for data subjects? 4. Risk assessment — likelihood and severity for each risk. 5. Mitigation measures. 6. Residual risk and whether it is acceptable. 7. Consultation requirements (e.g. with the DPO, data subjects, or regulator). Format as a structured template my team can complete.

---

**PROMPT CP-A6 — Privacy Policy Drafting**
Draft a privacy policy for [ORGANISATION NAME] that complies with [PDPA (Singapore) / GDPR (EU) / specify]. The organisation: - Industry: [DESCRIBE] - Data collected: [LIST — e.g. name, email, phone, payment data, location data, health data] - Purposes of processing: [LIST] - Legal basis for processing: [LIST for GDPR or describe for PDPA] - Third parties data is shared with: [LIST] - Overseas transfers: [DESCRIBE] - Retention periods: [DESCRIBE] Write in plain, accessible English. Include all mandatory disclosures under [REGULATION]. Add a cookie notice section. Note any areas where I need to provide additional information to complete the policy.

## Section B: Financial Services Regulatory (MAS / FCA / SEC)

**PROMPT CP-B1 — MAS Regulatory Obligations Mapping (Singapore)**
Act as a financial services regulatory lawyer specialising in Singaporean law. My client is a [DESCRIBE ENTITY — e.g. fund manager, fintech, payment service provider, insurance intermediary] seeking to operate in Singapore. Map the applicable MAS regulatory framework: 1. Which MAS Act(s) apply — MAS Act, SFA, FAA, PSA, Insurance Act? 2. What licence or exemption applies to my client's activities? 3. What are the key ongoing compliance obligations (capital, conduct, reporting, AML/CFT)? 4. What are the MAS notification / approval requirements for material changes? 5. What are the penalties for operating without the appropriate licence? Note if you need more information about the specific business model to complete this analysis.

---

**PROMPT CP-B2 — AML/CFT Policy Review**
Act as an AML/CFT compliance specialist. Review the following AML/CFT policy / compliance programme: [PASTE OR DESCRIBE KEY ELEMENTS]. Assess whether it meets the requirements of: 1. [MAS Notice on AML / FATF Recommendations / applicable framework — specify]. 2. Customer Due Diligence (CDD) — standard, simplified, and enhanced. 3. Politically Exposed Persons (PEP) screening. 4. Suspicious Transaction Reporting (STR) obligations. 5. Ongoing monitoring. 6. Staff training requirements. For each area: state the requirement, assess our current practice (Compliant / Partially Compliant / Gap), and recommend improvements.

---

**PROMPT CP-B3 — Regulatory Horizon Scanning Briefing**
Act as a regulatory intelligence analyst. Produce a quarterly regulatory horizon scan for a [INDUSTRY] company operating in [JURISDICTION(S)]. Cover: 1. Regulatory changes that have taken effect in the last 3 months — summarise the key requirements. 2. Regulations or guidance in consultation or pipeline — what is coming and when. 3. Recent regulatory enforcement actions in our sector — key lessons. 4. International developments that may affect our business (e.g. EU AI Act, US SEC rules, FATF updates). Present as a structured briefing with: Effective Now | Coming Soon | Watch List. Highlight any items requiring immediate action.

## Section C: Corporate Compliance & Ethics

**PROMPT CP-C1 — Anti-Bribery & Corruption Policy**
Draft an Anti-Bribery and Corruption (ABC) Policy for [COMPANY NAME], a company operating in [JURISDICTIONS]. The policy must address the requirements of [UK Bribery Act / US FCPA / Singapore Prevention of Corruption Act — specify]. Cover: 1. Scope and who the policy applies to (employees, contractors, third parties). 2. What is and is not permitted — gifts, hospitality, facilitation payments, political donations. 3. The approval process for gifts and hospitality above [THRESHOLD]. 4. Third-party due diligence obligations. 5. Reporting mechanisms (speak-up / whistleblowing). 6. Consequences of breach. 7. Training requirements. Write in plain English with practical examples.

---

**PROMPT CP-C2 — Compliance Training Script**
Create a 20-minute compliance training script on [TOPIC, e.g. data protection / anti-bribery / conflicts of interest / insider trading] for [AUDIENCE, e.g. all staff / sales team / finance team / senior management] at [COMPANY NAME]. The script should: open with a real-world scenario that illustrates why this topic matters, explain the relevant rules clearly and in plain language, include 3 interactive questions or scenarios for audience participation, state clearly what employees must do (and not do), explain how to report concerns, and close with a summary of key takeaways. Avoid legal jargon. Tone: engaging and practical, not lecture-style.

---

**PROMPT CP-C3 — Conflicts of Interest Register & Management**
Act as a compliance officer. Draft a conflicts of interest identification and management framework for [ORGANISATION TYPE, e.g. law firm / financial institution / regulated company]. Cover: 1. How to identify conflicts of interest — a checklist of common scenarios in our business. 2. The disclosure and escalation process. 3. How conflicts will be managed (recusal, information barriers, disclosure to client / counterparty, declining the matter). 4. Record-keeping — what must be logged in the conflicts register. 5. Annual refresh and review process. 6. Training requirements. Also draft a sample conflicts register entry template.

---

**PROMPT CP-C4 — Whistleblowing Policy**
Draft a whistleblowing / speak-up policy for [COMPANY NAME]. The company has [NUMBER] employees in [JURISDICTIONS]. The policy should comply with [relevant employment law / Protected Disclosures Act / MOM requirements — specify]. Cover: 1. The purpose and importance of speaking up. 2. What kinds of concerns can be reported. 3. How to report (named and anonymous channels). 4. How reports will be investigated. 5. Protections for the person who reports (confidentiality, non-retaliation). 6. What happens after an investigation. 7. Record-keeping. Write in plain English. Include a FAQ section with 5 common questions employees might have.

## Section D: Competition Law & Antitrust

**PROMPT CP-D1 — Competition Law Risk Assessment**
Act as a competition lawyer in [JURISDICTION, e.g. Singapore — CCCS / EU — European Commission / UK — CMA]. My client is proposing the following business arrangement or conduct: [DESCRIBE — e.g. an information-sharing agreement with a competitor / a distribution agreement with resale price maintenance / a merger with a competitor / pricing conduct]. Assess: 1. Does this arrangement raise competition law concerns? 2. What is the relevant legal framework ([Competition Act / Article 101 TFEU / etc.])? 3. What are the key risk factors? 4. Are there any safe harbours, block exemptions, or guidance that apply? 5. What is the recommended approach to mitigate risk? 6. Should this be notified to the relevant authority?

---

**PROMPT CP-D2 — Dawn Raid Response Protocol**
Act as a competition law specialist. Draft a dawn raid (unannounced regulatory inspection) response protocol for [COMPANY NAME]. The protocol should cover: 1. Immediate steps when investigators arrive at your premises. 2. Who to call first (legal counsel, management). 3. Rights of the company and its employees during an inspection. 4. How to document what the investigators do and take. 5. What employees should and should not say. 6. Privilege — how to protect legal advice documents. 7. Post-inspection steps — notification, legal privilege review, communication strategy. Format as a step-by-step crisis response guide suitable for the front desk and IT team, not just lawyers.
