# Legal AI Prompt Library: V3_02 Pleadings & Court Documents

## SECTION A: STATEMENTS OF CASE

### PROMPT ID: CD-001
**TITLE:** Statement of Claim (Pleading)
**FULL TEXT:** Draft a Statement of Claim on behalf of [CLAIMANT NAME] against [DEFENDANT NAME] for [TYPE OF CLAIM] claiming damages of [AMOUNT]. The pleading must: (1) clearly identify all parties with their capacities, (2) provide concise chronological narrative of material facts leading to the claim, (3) specify the date and nature of loss suffered, (4) identify the breach/wrongful act giving rise to liability with legal basis, (5) detail causation (how the breach caused the loss), (6) calculate quantum of loss by individual head of damage, (7) reference key documents in schedules, (8) plead applicable contractual, statutory, or common law principles, (9) include a statement of truth, and (10) state prayer for relief (damages, interest, costs). Keep narrative concise, avoiding evidence and rhetorical argument.
**CONTEXT:** Initial pleading opening proceedings; comprehensive claim articulation
**DIFFICULTY:** Advanced
**FOLLOW-UP:** Have we adequately pleaded every element of the cause of action? Are there any ambiguities the defendant could exploit?

---

### PROMPT ID: CD-002
**TITLE:** Defence Pleading (Response to Claim)
**FULL TEXT:** Draft a Defence pleading on behalf of [DEFENDANT NAME] in response to the Statement of Claim filed by [CLAIMANT NAME] on [DATE]. The Defence must: (1) admit or deny each allegation in the claim (use formulaic admissions where appropriate), (2) state alternative facts showing no breach occurred or alternative version of events, (3) plead legal defenses (limitation, contributory negligence, novation, discharge, frustration, force majeure), (4) admit liability but challenge quantum separately, (5) identify gaps in the claimant's case, (6) disclose any counterclaims in alternative format or separately, (7) reference supporting documents, (8) include statement of truth, and (9) state relief sought (dismissal, reduced damages, counterclaim relief). Be precise with admissions to avoid waiver; deny ambiguous allegations.
**CONTEXT:** Primary defensive pleading; establishing contestable issues
**DIFFICULTY:** Advanced
**FOLLOW-UP:** Which allegations should we admit to manage costs and focus on key disputes? What counterclaims should be included?

---

### PROMPT ID: CD-003
**TITLE:** Counterclaim Pleading
**FULL TEXT:** Draft a Counterclaim form part of the Defence filed by [DEFENDANT NAME] against [CLAIMANT NAME] for [TYPE OF COUNTERCLAIM] claiming [AMOUNT]. The Counterclaim must: (1) be stated as alternative relief within the Defence, (2) set out factual basis for the counterclaim with dates and events, (3) clearly distinguish facts giving rise to counterclaim from facts in main claim, (4) identify the breach or wrongful conduct complained of, (5) plead causation and quantifiable loss, (6) reference supporting documents, (7) break down damages by head, (8) include statement of truth on the counterclaim, and (9) state relief sought. Structure as continuation of Defence pleading with clear heading. Consider whether counterclaim is defensive (set-off) or offensive (new claim).
**CONTEXT:** Defensive claims for cost recovery or damages; shifting claim focus
**DIFFICULTY:** Advanced
**FOLLOW-UP:** Will our counterclaim complicate settlement negotiations? Should we pursue it separately if main claim settles?

---

### PROMPT ID: CD-004
**TITLE:** Reply Pleading (Claimant's Response to Defence)
**FULL TEXT:** Draft a Reply pleading on behalf of [CLAIMANT NAME] responding to the Defence filed by [DEFENDANT NAME] on [DATE]. The Reply must: (1) address defenses raised point-by-point, (2) either accept the defense, refute it with factual/legal argument, or clarify the claimant's position, (3) set out any factual allegations in response to new facts pleaded in the Defence, (4) if a counterclaim is raised, deny it on grounds of merit or provide defense to it, (5) identify any admissions in the Defence that are helpful, (6) clarify ambiguous points from the Statement of Claim, (7) highlight factual concessions by the Defendant where evident, (8) include statement of truth, and (9) be concise - do not re-plead the entire claim. Reference the Defence paragraphs by number.
**CONTEXT:** Narrowing issues for trial; responding to new defenses/counterclaims
**DIFFICULTY:** Intermediate to Advanced
**FOLLOW-UP:** Can we extract any useful admissions from the Defence? Should we file a defence to the counterclaim separately?

---

### PROMPT ID: CD-005
**TITLE:** Rejoinder Pleading (Defendant's Response to Reply)
**FULL TEXT:** Draft a Rejoinder pleading on behalf of [DEFENDANT NAME] responding to the Reply filed by [CLAIMANT NAME] on [DATE]. The Rejoinder must: (1) address points raised in the Reply that require clarification or refutation, (2) provide brief responses to new factual allegations in the Reply, (3) reinforce key defenses with additional facts if necessary, (4) respond to any new legal arguments raised, (5) address defense to any counterclaim raised, (6) avoid simply re-stating previous pleadings, (7) be narrowly focused on matters requiring clarification, (8) include statement of truth if any new facts pleaded, and (9) identify issues now agreed/narrowed. Rejoinders should be brief; courts discourage lengthy responses. Only file if truly necessary to resolve material disputes.
**CONTEXT:** Finalizing pleading exchange; clarifying final positions
**DIFFICULTY:** Intermediate
**FOLLOW-UP:** Is this Rejoinder necessary or will it just enlarge the dispute? What issues remain genuinely disputed?

### PROMPT ID: CD-006
**TITLE:** Request for Further & Better Particulars (FBP)
**FULL TEXT:** Prepare a Request for Further and Better Particulars to be served on [OPPOSING PARTY] regarding their pleading dated [DATE]. The request must seek clarification on: (1) vague or ambiguous allegations by reference to specific paragraph and request clear statement, (2) unpleaded facts that would assist understanding of the claim (dates, locations, specific acts), (3) the factual basis for legal conclusions, (4) details of damages calculations (broken down by head with supporting figures), (5) identification of documents referred to in pleading, (6) identification of witnesses with knowledge of specific allegations, (7) the basis for any expert opinions stated, (8) timescales and sequences of events, and (9) clarification of legal bases relied upon. Number each request and keep language precise and courteous. Do not seek to obtain evidence or documents.
**CONTEXT:** Pleading clarification; preparing case for trial
**DIFFICULTY:** Intermediate
**FOLLOW-UP:** Which paragraphs are most vulnerable to FBP requests? Should we be concerned about their FBP requests to us?

### PROMPT ID: CD-007
**TITLE:** Response to Further & Better Particulars Request
**FULL TEXT:** Prepare a response to a Request for Further and Better Particulars served by [OPPOSING PARTY] on [DATE]. For each request, provide: (1) a clear answer directly responsive to the question, (2) admission if facts are not in dispute, (3) clarification of ambiguous allegations, (4) identification of relevant documents, (5) details of witnesses with knowledge, (6) quantification of damages claims (with supporting calculations where requested), (7) timelines and sequences with specific dates, or (8) objection if request seeks evidence, legal argument, or documents (explaining basis of objection). Use same numbering as the request. Provide sufficient detail to narrow issues without disclosing litigation strategy or evidence. Serve within specified timeline (usually 14-21 days).
**CONTEXT:** Responding to opponent's clarification requests; case management
**DIFFICULTY:** Intermediate
**FOLLOW-UP:** Have we disclosed anything that weakens our case? Should we seek further FBPs from them?

### PROMPT ID: CD-008
**TITLE:** Amendment to Pleading
**FULL TEXT:** Prepare an application and supporting affidavit to amend [PLEADING TYPE - e.g., Statement of Claim] to [DESCRIBE PROPOSED AMENDMENT - add new paragraphs, delete allegations, substitute new facts]. The application must: (1) identify the exact amendments (using marked version with tracked changes), (2) explain the reason for late amendment (mistake, new evidence, clarification), (3) address whether the amendment adds new cause of action or is within existing scope, (4) assess prejudice to the other party from the amendment, (5) reference any delay in seeking amendment and explanation, (6) indicate willingness to adjourn trial/adjust timeline if necessary, (7) address any costs implications, and (8) cite applicable court rules on amendments. Supporting affidavit should explain the circumstances necessitating amendment and confirm no delay attributable to negligence.
**CONTEXT:** Late pleading corrections; responding to new information
**DIFFICULTY:** Intermediate to Advanced
**FOLLOW-UP:** Will the other party consent to this amendment or should we prepare for contested hearing? What prejudice should we concede?

### PROMPT ID: CD-009
**TITLE:** Application to Strike Out (Pleading Challenge)
**FULL TEXT:** Prepare an application with supporting affidavit to strike out [IDENTIFIED PARAGRAPHS/CLAIM ELEMENT] from [OPPOSING PARTY]'s pleading on the grounds that: (1) it discloses no reasonable cause of action, (2) it is an abuse of process (duplicative claim already decided, frivolous), (3) it is embarrassing (inflammatory, irrelevant, prejudicial without probative value), (4) it is prolix (excessive detail irrelevant to issues), or (5) it breaches procedural rules (improper pleading format, missing key elements). The application must: specify the exact language to be struck, articulate the legal ground clearly, provide case law authority, explain prejudice from allowing the allegation, suggest alternative if any, and propose costs allocation. Include supporting affidavit confirming factual assumptions. Prepare for two-stage test (legal sufficiency; if passed, discretionary factors).
**CONTEXT:** Cost-efficient claim elimination; procedural housekeeping
**DIFFICULTY:** Advanced
**FOLLOW-UP:** Is a strike-out application the best use of resources or should we just defend on merits? What's our fallback if the application fails?

### PROMPT ID: CD-010
**TITLE:** Summary of Pleadings and Issues Register
**FULL TEXT:** Prepare a summary of pleadings and issues register consolidating the Statement of Claim (filed [DATE]), Defence (filed [DATE]), Reply (filed [DATE]), and any Counterclaim. The summary should: (1) list the parties and their roles, (2) identify all causes of action pleaded with paragraph references, (3) create a table of essential facts showing: (a) which facts are admitted, (b) which are denied, (c) which are not addressed, (4) identify legal bases for each claim (statute, contract term, common law principle), (5) list defenses pleaded with legal basis, (6) summarize any counterclaims, (7) list issues genuinely in dispute, (8) list issues seemingly conceded, (9) identify gaps in pleadings, and (10) note procedural issues unresolved. Use this as trial preparation tool and for case management conference.
**CONTEXT:** Case consolidation; trial readiness; judicial case management
**DIFFICULTY:** Intermediate
**FOLLOW-UP:** Are there issues we can narrow before trial? What facts should we seek admissions on?

## SECTION B: AFFIDAVITS & STATUTORY DECLARATIONS

### PROMPT ID: CD-011
**TITLE:** Supporting Affidavit for Interlocutory Application
**FULL TEXT:** Prepare an affidavit sworn by [DEPONENT NAME - job title/capacity] supporting an application for [TYPE OF APPLICATION - e.g., interim relief, summary judgment, extension of time]. The affidavit must: (1) have clear heading identifying the case and application, (2) commence with oath/affirmation and deponent information, (3) provide background and context for the application, (4) set out facts supporting the legal test for the relief sought (e.g., serious issue to be tried; balance of convenience for interim relief), (5) reference and exhibit key documents (contracts, emails, invoices, photographs), (6) explain why immediate/urgent relief is necessary, (7) address any prejudice to the other party, (8) include deponent's personal knowledge or information sources, (9) comply with formatting rules (paragraphs, page numbers, exhibits), and (10) end with statement of truth. Be detailed on facts; reserve legal argument for submissions.
**CONTEXT:** Supporting urgent interlocutory applications; establishing factual foundations
**DIFFICULTY:** Intermediate to Advanced
**FOLLOW-UP:** What documents must we exhibit to strengthen this affidavit? How will the other party respond to these facts?

### PROMPT ID: CD-012
**TITLE:** Affidavit of Evidence in Chief (AEIC)
**FULL TEXT:** Prepare an Affidavit of Evidence in Chief (AEIC) sworn by [DEPONENT NAME - capacity in case, role in events] providing evidence on [SPECIFIED ISSUES/EVENTS]. The AEIC must: (1) be formatted per court rules (paragraph numbering, page numbers, exhibits), (2) set out deponent's background and credibility, (3) provide comprehensive narrative of events within deponent's knowledge with dates and participants, (4) cover all material facts relevant to [SPECIFIED ISSUES], (5) distinguish between personal knowledge, information received, and belief, (6) reference supporting documents and exhibits (organized by tab), (7) address any inconsistencies with other evidence proactively, (8) answer anticipated cross-examination questions, (9) be factual and avoid argument/opinion (except expert opinion if qualified), (10) include clear statement of truth, and (11) be organized logically. Courts treat AEICs as evidence-in-chief; quality impacts credibility at trial.
**CONTEXT:** Primary trial evidence; evidence in chief presentation
**DIFFICULTY:** Advanced
**FOLLOW-UP:** How will the other party cross-examine this deponent? Have we addressed all contested facts adequately?

### PROMPT ID: CD-013
**TITLE:** Affidavit of Service (Procedural Proof)
**FULL TEXT:** Prepare an Affidavit of Service sworn by [PERSON WHO SERVED DOCUMENTS - typically process server or party] certifying service of [DOCUMENT TYPE AND DESCRIPTION] on [SERVED PARTY NAME AND ADDRESS] on [DATE OF SERVICE]. The affidavit must: (1) identify the documents served by description and exhibit (attach copy if required), (2) specify the date, time, and method of service (personal service, address, email, courier), (3) identify the person served (name, capacity, relationship to party if relevant), (4) confirm the person served was authorized to accept service, (5) state the address where service effected, (6) address any obstacles encountered in service, (7) confirm postal address for service was correct, (8) state where applicant obtained address information, (9) include statement of truth, and (10) be sworn by the actual person who effected service. This affidavit is critical proof of procedural compliance; be precise.
**CONTEXT:** Proving procedural compliance; establishing notice
**DIFFICULTY:** Intermediate
**FOLLOW-UP:** If service was disputed, what alternative evidence of service do we have? Should we proceed if service is uncertain?

### PROMPT ID: CD-014
**TITLE:** Affidavit of Non-Compliance (Procedural Breach)
**FULL TEXT:** Prepare an affidavit sworn by [DEPONENT NAME] evidencing non-compliance by [OTHER PARTY] with court orders or procedural rules in relation to [SPECIFIC ORDER/RULE - e.g., disclosure order dated [DATE], expert evidence rule]. The affidavit must: (1) identify the order/rule and its key obligations, (2) confirm deponent's role in requesting compliance, (3) set out the steps taken to enforce compliance (correspondence, reminders with dates), (4) specify what compliance obligations remain unmet, (5) detail the prejudice caused by non-compliance to deponent's case, (6) provide timeline of communications regarding non-compliance, (7) exhibit correspondence (letters, emails) requesting compliance, (8) state whether deponent has suffered financial loss from non-compliance (costs of delays, expert fees ongoing), (9) address any explanations offered by non-compliant party, and (10) include statement of truth. This affidavit supports applications for sanctions/contempt or case stay.
**CONTEXT:** Enforcing procedural compliance; supporting sanctions applications
**DIFFICULTY:** Intermediate
**FOLLOW-UP:** Should we seek costs sanctions, case stay, or contempt proceedings? What remedy is proportionate?

### PROMPT ID: CD-015
**TITLE:** Affidavit in Opposition (Responding Affidavit)
**FULL TEXT:** Prepare an affidavit in opposition sworn by [DEPONENT NAME - representing responding party] to oppose [APPLICANT]'s application for [TYPE OF APPLICATION]. The affidavit must: (1) clearly state that deponent opposes the application and briefly state grounds, (2) provide factual response to allegations in the applicant's affidavit, (3) provide alternative version of disputed facts based on deponent's knowledge, (4) address the legal test for the relief sought and why it is not satisfied, (5) reference supporting documents and exhibits, (6) explain any delay or prejudice caused by the application, (7) set out deponent's position on alternative relief, (8) address proportionality and costs implications, (9) confirm key facts in applicant's affidavit that are either admitted or disputed, and (10) include statement of truth. Keep focused on responding directly to applicant's case; avoid introducing entirely new issues.
**CONTEXT:** Defending interlocutory applications; presenting responsive facts
**DIFFICULTY:** Intermediate to Advanced
**FOLLOW-UP:** What is our best ground for opposing this application? Should we offer alternative proposals?

### PROMPT ID: CD-016
**TITLE:** Statutory Declaration (Non-Sworn Affidavit)
**FULL TEXT:** Prepare a statutory declaration by [DEPONENT NAME - capacity/role] regarding [SUBJECT MATTER - facts to be declared]. The statutory declaration must: (1) have standard heading for statutory declarations in the relevant jurisdiction, (2) state deponent's full name, address, occupation, and capacity in which declaration is made, (3) set out facts within deponent's personal knowledge, distinguishing from information received or belief, (4) be organized logically with numbered paragraphs, (5) reference supporting documents and exhibits, (6) include date and place of declaration, (7) be signed by deponent (or mark if unable to sign), and (8) include statement that the deponent understands the declaration carries legal consequences for false statement. Use statutory declarations where: affidavit is not required, deponent unable to give oath, or procedural rules permit. Statutory declarations carry same weight as affidavits in most jurisdictions. Useful for obtaining evidence from overseas deponents.
**CONTEXT:** Evidence gathering; overseas witness statements; informal proceedings
**DIFFICULTY:** Intermediate
**FOLLOW-UP:** Will courts accept statutory declaration for this purpose? Should we use affidavit instead for formal proceedings?

### PROMPT ID: CD-017
**TITLE:** Affidavit Verifying List of Documents (Disclosure)
**FULL TEXT:** Prepare an affidavit verifying [PARTY]'s list of documents in response to disclosure obligations in [CASE NAME]. The affidavit must be sworn by [DEPONENT NAME - typically managing lawyer or client representative with document knowledge] and must: (1) confirm the list is a true and complete list of documents within party's control, (2) identify the date range of documents listed, (3) describe the search methodology used (custodians identified, systems searched, keywords used), (4) confirm all reasonable steps taken to identify documents within scope of disclosure order, (5) explain any gaps or limitations in search (accessibility, loss of documents), (6) identify categories of documents listed (by title/date range), (7) explain any documents destroyed or disposed of and why, (8) confirm privilege assertions are accurate (identify privileged documents by category), (9) address any issues with document identification or location, and (10) include statement of truth. This is critical for completeness; inadequate disclosure risks sanctions/contempt proceedings.
**CONTEXT:** Disclosure phase; establishing procedural compliance
**DIFFICULTY:** Advanced
**FOLLOW-UP:** Have we identified all relevant documents? Are our privilege claims properly categorized and justified?

## SECTION C: WRITTEN SUBMISSIONS

### PROMPT ID: CD-018
**TITLE:** Skeletal Arguments (Concise Legal Submission)
**FULL TEXT:** Prepare skeletal arguments for [APPLICANT/RESPONDENT] in support of an application for [TYPE OF APPLICATION] to be heard before [COURT/JUDGE]. Skeletal arguments must: (1) briefly state the relief sought and its basis, (2) identify the applicable legal test (statute section, case law), (3) set out key legal propositions in numbered format (each 1-3 lines), (4) provide case law authority for each proposition with full citation, (5) apply propositions to the facts, (6) address anticipated counter-arguments, (7) be logically organized (usually: legal test, satisfied elements, disputed elements, relief sought), (8) use clear signposting and formatting, (9) include all essential authority (avoid excessive cases), and (10) be concise (typically 5-15 pages for interlocutory applications). Format as outline rather than prose. Attach page-referenced core bundle of authorities.
**CONTEXT:** Interlocutory application preparation; efficiency-focused legal argument
**DIFFICULTY:** Advanced
**FOLLOW-UP:** Which cases are most critical to our legal argument? Should we expand on any element?

---

### PROMPT ID: CD-019
**TITLE:** Written Submissions for Interlocutory Hearing (Detailed Legal Argument)
**FULL TEXT:** Prepare detailed written submissions for [APPLICANT/RESPONDENT] in support of an application for [TYPE OF APPLICATION] to be heard before [COURT NAME] on [HEARING DATE]. Submissions must: (1) include introduction briefly stating the case and relief sought, (2) set out the legal test applicable to the application, (3) provide comprehensive case law analysis supporting each element of test, (4) apply law to the facts with reference to affidavit evidence, (5) address each ground relied upon separately with authorities, (6) anticipate and respond to counter-arguments in advance, (7) address any procedural issues or jurisdictional questions, (8) discuss proportionality and discretionary factors where relevant, (9) include focused section on urgency if interim relief sought, (10) conclude with summary of relief sought, and (11) include properly formatted authorities bundle with page references. Structure logically with clear headings and numbering.
**CONTEXT:** Formal interlocutory applications; detailed legal argument development
**DIFFICULTY:** Advanced
**FOLLOW-UP:** Have we addressed all likely counter-arguments? Are our cited authorities on point or distinguishable?

### PROMPT ID: CD-020
**TITLE:** Closing Submissions (Trial)
**FULL TEXT:** Prepare closing submissions for [PARTY NAME] summarizing the case at the conclusion of trial evidence in [CASE NAME]. Submissions must: (1) begin with concise summary of the case, legal claims, and relief sought, (2) walk through evidence systematically addressing each key fact in issue, (3) assess credibility of witnesses against their evidence and documents, (4) explain how evidence proves/fails each element of the legal test, (5) address any gaps in evidence and draw available inferences, (6) respond to opponent's closing submissions anticipated arguments, (7) apply law to established facts and explain why relief should be granted, (8) address statutory interpretation issues if relevant, (9) discuss damages calculations with evidence support, (10) address any admissions or concessions by opponent, and (11) end with clear summary of relief sought. Organize by issue/cause of action. Reference evidence by page numbers of transcripts/exhibits. Use headings for clarity. Include statement of law with authorities.
**CONTEXT:** Trial conclusion; final evidence synthesis and persuasion
**DIFFICULTY:** Advanced
**FOLLOW-UP:** Have we addressed all facts in dispute? Are our legal conclusions properly supported by evidence?

### PROMPT ID: CD-021
**TITLE:** Reply Submissions (Addressing Opponent's Written Submissions)
**FULL TEXT:** Prepare reply submissions for [PARTY] in response to [OPPONENT]'s written submissions filed on [DATE] regarding [APPLICATION/HEARING]. Reply submissions must: (1) briefly summarize [OPPONENT]'s key arguments, (2) identify specific errors in [OPPONENT]'s legal analysis, (3) provide corrective case law authority, (4) explain how [OPPONENT] has mischaracterized the evidence, (5) address factual errors or omissions in [OPPONENT]'s submissions, (6) clarify any misunderstandings of our position from [OPPONENT]'s response, (7) respond to new arguments raised in opponent's submissions, (8) avoid simply re-arguing points already made in principal submissions, (9) be concise and focused on material points of difference, and (10) provide updated authorities if new law has emerged. Reply submissions should not exceed 30% of principal submission length. Use clear headings identifying which opponent argument is being addressed.
**CONTEXT:** Responding to opponent's challenge to our submissions
**DIFFICULTY:** Advanced
**FOLLOW-UP:** Are there critical points we must address in reply? Can we concede any of their arguments without material damage?

### PROMPT ID: CD-022
**TITLE:** Submissions on Costs (Cost Awards & Allocation)
**FULL TEXT:** Prepare submissions on costs on behalf of [SUCCESSFUL/UNSUCCESSFUL PARTY] following judgment in [CASE NAME]. Submissions must: (1) state the party's position on who should bear costs (party and party vs. solicitor and own client; percentage liability), (2) reference the judgment and relief granted/refused to support costs allocation, (3) address the general rule in the jurisdiction (loser pays; no order; by agreement), (4) if party was partially successful, address proportionate costs allocation, (5) explain any conduct warranting different costs order (offers made, unreasonable positions, obstruction), (6) address any agreed facts regarding costs, (7) reference any Calderbank offers or formal settlement proposals indicating reasonableness, (8) discuss proportionality of costs to amounts in dispute or value of claims, (9) address any grounds for indemnity costs (fraud, abuse of process), and (10) provide proposed costs order language. Include affidavit evidence of costs incurred if required.
**CONTEXT:** Post-judgment cost determination; allocating litigation expenses
**DIFFICULTY:** Advanced
**FOLLOW-UP:** Should we seek costs on an indemnity basis? How will the judge likely allocate costs given the outcome?

### PROMPT ID: CD-023
**TITLE:** Submissions on Assessment of Damages
**FULL TEXT:** Prepare written submissions on assessment of damages on behalf of [CLAIMANT] for consideration by [JUDGE/ARBITRATOR] at damages hearing in [CASE NAME]. Submissions must: (1) set out the legal framework for damages assessment in [JURISDICTION] (contract breach, tort, statutory remedy), (2) identify each head of damage claimed with legal basis, (3) explain the factual causation (how breach caused this loss), (4) provide financial calculations for each head with supporting evidence, (5) address mitigation obligations and steps claimant took to minimize loss, (6) distinguish between loss actually suffered (provable) versus speculative/remote loss, (7) reference supporting expert evidence (accountant, valuer) where applicable, (8) address loss of opportunity damages and probability assessments, (9) explain interest calculations and compound interest if applicable, (10) address any deductible factors (insurance proceeds, benefit received), and (11) provide detailed schedule of total damages claimed. Use clear formatting with separate sections per damage head.
**CONTEXT:** Damages trial; quantifying compensation claims
**DIFFICULTY:** Advanced
**FOLLOW-UP:** Which damage heads are most vulnerable to challenge? What evidence best supports our valuations?

## SECTION D: COURT CORRESPONDENCE

### PROMPT ID: CD-024
**TITLE:** Letter to Court/Registry (Case Management)
**FULL TEXT:** Draft a letter to [COURT NAME/REGISTRY] from [PARTY] through [PARTY'S SOLICITOR] regarding [SUBJECT MATTER - e.g., requesting directions, notifying court of settlement, extending time). The letter must: (1) clearly identify the case by name, case number, and judge (if assigned), (2) briefly state the matter requiring court direction or action, (3) identify the parties' positions (if joint application vs. application by one party), (4) explain the reason for the request or notification, (5) specify any urgent timing considerations, (6) reference applicable court rules or any existing court orders, (7) propose a solution or seek the court's directions, (8) confirm any agreement with the other party, (9) set out any practical implications (adjournment, costs), and (10) request confirmation of the court's directions. Keep tone respectful and formal; reference case number in all correspondence. Copy other party unless communication is confidential (e.g., settlement discussions).
**CONTEXT:** Case management communications; inter-partes court contact
**DIFFICULTY:** Intermediate
**FOLLOW-UP:** Should we copy the other party or mark this as "without notice"? What directions do we anticipate the court will give?

### PROMPT ID: CD-025
**TITLE:** Request for Directions from Court
**FULL TEXT:** Prepare a letter to [COURT NAME] requesting court directions on [SPECIFIC PROCEDURAL ISSUE - e.g., extension of disclosure deadline, expert report exchange, case management]. The letter must: (1) identify the case clearly (name, number, judge), (2) explain the procedural issue requiring directions, (3) set out the relevant procedural rule and current deadline, (4) explain why extension/modification is sought (practical constraints, complexity, agreement with opponent, unforeseen circumstances), (5) propose specific alternative directions (new deadline, modified procedure), (6) confirm the other party's position (agreed/opposed), (7) address any prejudice from proposed modification, (8) indicate whether directions are sought on an urgent basis, (9) propose next steps/timetable if directions granted, and (10) request the court's confirmation of proposed directions. Indicate whether consent of all parties has been obtained or specify contested nature. Be respectful and avoid blaming the other party.
**CONTEXT:** Managing procedural delays; seeking judicial guidance
**DIFFICULTY:** Intermediate
**FOLLOW-UP:** Will the court likely grant this extension? Should we approach the other party to seek consent first?

### PROMPT ID: CD-027
**TITLE:** Unless Order Response (Demonstrating Compliance)
**FULL TEXT:** Prepare a response to an "Unless Order" imposed by [COURT] on [DATE] requiring [SPECIFIED COMPLIANCE - e.g., provide disclosure by [DATE], file expert evidence by [DATE], or the claim/defence will be struck out]. Response must: (1) immediately confirm whether compliance with the "unless" condition has been achieved, (2) if compliant: provide evidence of compliance (file the document required, exhibit proof if delivery required), explain any delay experienced, and request that the "unless" order be lifted, (3) if non-compliant: explain why compliance could not be achieved (genuine impossibility, force majeure, other good cause), (4) provide evidence of efforts to comply, (5) set out the new date by which compliance will be achieved (if requesting further extension), (6) address any prejudice to other party from non-compliance, (7) offer concessions or relief to mitigate prejudice (costs, extended other deadlines), and (8) file affidavit evidence supporting explanation if non-compliant. **CONTEXT:** Responding to strict compliance deadlines; avoiding strike-out sanctions
**DIFFICULTY:** Intermediate
**FOLLOW-UP:** If we cannot comply, what is the court's likely response? Can we negotiate a revised deadline?

### PROMPT ID: CD-028
**TITLE:** Adjournment Request (Delay of Hearing/Trial)
**FULL TEXT:** Prepare a letter to [COURT] requesting adjournment of [HEARING/TRIAL] scheduled for [DATE].
