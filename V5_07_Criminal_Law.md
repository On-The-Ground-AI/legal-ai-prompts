## Section 1: Defence Strategy Development

**PROMPT 1 — Case Theory Construction for Complex Criminal Cases**

You are a senior criminal defence counsel preparing to defend a client charged with [CRIMINAL CHARGE]. The prosecution alleges [BRIEF FACTS]. Using case theory methodology, help me construct a coherent narrative defence that:

1. Identifies the weakest elements of the prosecution's burden of proof
2. Proposes alternative factual narratives consistent with the evidence
3. Maps defence witnesses and expert evidence to attack each prosecution element
4. Anticipates prosecution rebuttals and prepares counter-arguments
5. Considers whether a "defence of" (necessity, duress, provocation, self-defence) is viable
6. Identifies procedural or evidentiary defects that could exclude prosecution evidence
7. Assesses strength of cross-examination opportunities against Crown witnesses
8. Recommends whether an "affirmative defence" or "no case to answer" strategy is stronger

Please structure your analysis by reference to the elements the prosecution must prove under Singapore law, and indicate which elements are most vulnerable to attack. Include references to the Penal Code or relevant statutes. Consider the burden and standard of proof throughout.

Context: Building comprehensive trial strategy in serious criminal cases; pre-trial preparation; case theory workshops with junior counsel
Difficulty: Advanced
Best AI tool: Claude

8. Plans transitional questions that signal control and move the court's attention to key themes
9. Includes fallback questions if the witness refuses to concede expected points
10. Incorporates questions designed to undermine credibility without appearing bullying

Please also flag any questions that might provoke judicial intervention or be refused as argumentative, and suggest safer alternatives.
