## A. PATENTS

### PROMPT 1 — PATENT INFRINGEMENT ANALYSIS

Review the [ACCUSED PRODUCT/TECHNOLOGY] for potential infringement of [PATENT NUMBER(S)]. Analyze each claim element and determine whether the accused product meets or reads on the claimed elements. Consider both literal infringement and doctrine of equivalents. Identify any potential defenses such as invalidity, non-infringement, or inequitable conduct before the USPTO. Provide a detailed claim-by-claim analysis with likelihood of infringement assessment (low/medium/high). Highlight specific technical features and their correspondence to patent claim elements.

**Context:** Use when evaluating whether a competitor's product or technology infringes your client's patent or assessing infringement risk.

**Difficulty:** Intermediate

**Follow-up:** PROMPT 8 (Patent Validity Challenge Assessment), PROMPT 3 (Prior Art Search Strategy)

## C. COPYRIGHT

### PROMPT 17 — COPYRIGHT INFRINGEMENT ASSESSMENT

Assess whether [ACCUSED WORK] infringes [COPYRIGHT OWNER]'s [ORIGINAL WORK] copyright. Determine: (1) validity of the copyright (registration, authorship, originality); (2) infringer's access to the original work; (3) similarity between works (compare structure, sequence, characters, expression); (4) analyze both literal copying and substantial similarity in protected expression (distinguish copying of unprotectable ideas, facts, procedures); (5) potential defenses (fair use, independent creation, public domain); (6) damages exposure (statutory damages $750-$30,000, or up to $150,000 for willful infringement); (7) strength of evidence and likelihood of infringement. Provide a verdict with confidence level (low/medium/high likelihood of infringement).

**Context:** Use when investigating copyright infringement claims or defending against allegations.

**Difficulty:** Intermediate

**Follow-up:** PROMPT 18 (Fair Use / Fair Dealing Analysis), PROMPT 19 (Copyright Ownership Chain Analysis)

### PROMPT 18 — FAIR USE / FAIR DEALING ANALYSIS

Analyze whether [ALLEGED INFRINGING USE] qualifies as fair use under 17 U.S.C. § 107 (or fair dealing in other jurisdictions). Evaluate the four statutory factors: (1) purpose and character of use (commercial vs. transformative); (2) nature of the copyrighted work; (3) amount and substantiality of the portion used; (4) effect on the market for or value of the original work. Provide qualitative analysis of each factor with case law references. Consider transformative use principles established in cases like Google Books and Andy Warhol. Assess whether use is parody, criticism, commentary, teaching, scholarship, or news reporting. Provide a conclusion on fair use likelihood with supporting legal authority.

**Context:** Use when defending against infringement allegations or assessing licensing necessity.

**Difficulty:** Intermediate

**Follow-up:** PROMPT 17 (Copyright Infringement Assessment), PROMPT 20 (DMCA Takedown Notice Draft)

---

### PROMPT 19 — COPYRIGHT OWNERSHIP CHAIN ANALYSIS

Trace the ownership chain for [COPYRIGHT IN WORK] from creation through present day. Document: (1) original author/creator and date of creation; (2) any work-made-for-hire designations and documentation; (3) assignments or transfers of copyright ownership (with dates, parties, scope); (4) licenses granted to third parties and their scope; (5) renewal registrations (for pre-1978 works); (6) any encumbrances or claims against the copyright; (7) current registered owner; (8) whether all assignments are properly executed and recorded. Identify gaps, missing documentation, or clouded title. Recommend registration/recordation steps to perfect ownership. Assess whether ownership is clear or disputed, and whether additional documentation is needed for licensing or enforcement.

**Context:** Use before licensing copyrights or enforcing infringement claims.

**Difficulty:** Intermediate

**Follow-up:** PROMPT 21 (Copyright Licensing Agreement Review), PROMPT 17 (Copyright Infringement Assessment)

### PROMPT 24 — TRADE SECRET IDENTIFICATION AND AUDIT

Identify and audit trade secrets for [COMPANY NAME]. Identify: (1) technical trade secrets (formulas, processes, specifications, source code); (2) business trade secrets (customer lists, pricing strategies, financial data, business plans); (3) current protection measures (access controls, confidentiality agreements, physical security); (4) adequacy of protective measures; (5) any past disclosures or potential compromises; (6) documentation and evidence of trade secret status. Provide a trade secret inventory with classification by criticality. Assess protection adequacy and recommend enhancement measures. Establish procedures for ongoing identification of new trade secrets and protection protocols.

**Context:** Use periodically to ensure robust trade secret protection program.

**Difficulty:** Intermediate

**Follow-up:** PROMPT 25 (Trade Secret Protection Programme Design), PROMPT 26 (Misappropriation Claim Assessment)
