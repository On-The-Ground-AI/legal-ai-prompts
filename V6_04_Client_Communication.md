# AI Prompts for Client Communication

Professional, empathetic, and strategic communication that builds trust, manages expectations, and strengthens client relationships throughout the engagement lifecycle.

---

## SECTION 1: STATUS UPDATE EMAILS

**PROMPT 1.1 — Routine Matter Status Update Email**

I need to send a professional status update to our [CLIENT NAME] regarding their [MATTER TYPE] matter. The matter has been progressing steadily with [GENERAL PROGRESS DESCRIPTION], and I want to keep them informed without creating unnecessary alarm.

Draft a professional status update email addressing: (1) opening greeting acknowledging their matter and showing genuine engagement, (2) a clear summary of progress to date on [KEY MILESTONES], explaining what has been accomplished and what tasks have been completed, (3) current status of the matter (e.g., "we are currently in the [PHASE] phase"), (4) identification of the next 2-3 key milestones or deliverables with realistic timeframes (e.g., "we expect to complete [TASK] by [DATE]"), (5) any client action items required with deadlines and explanation of why timing matters, (6) confirmation of key contact details and how client can reach you with questions or concerns, (7) transparent discussion of any risks or challenges if relevant (e.g., "we are monitoring [ISSUE] and will update you immediately if this changes"), and (8) professional closing confirming your commitment to the matter and your availability for discussion.

The email should be approximately 200-300 words, use clear plain English (not legal jargon), and should be suitable for sending to a [CLIENT TYPE]. Avoid legal terminology that client won't understand; explain legal concepts in practical terms. Include specific dates and deliverables rather than vague references. Maintain professional but warm tone that shows genuine engagement with the matter. Format with clear paragraphs and white space for readability.

Context: When matters are progressing normally and client communication is routine; sent at regular intervals (monthly/quarterly) or following significant milestones.
Difficulty: Beginner
Best AI tool: Any

**PROMPT 1.2 — Significant Milestone or Achievement Status Update**

I need to communicate positive news and significant progress to [CLIENT NAME] regarding their [MATTER TYPE] matter. We have just achieved/completed [SIGNIFICANT DEVELOPMENT], and I want to emphasize the positive progress while keeping perspective on remaining work.

Draft a celebratory yet professional status update email that: (1) opens with clear statement of the achievement (e.g., "We are pleased to confirm that [POSITIVE DEVELOPMENT] has been successfully completed"), (2) explains what this achievement means in practical terms for the client and their business, (3) details the work that went into achieving this (showing the value of our engagement without excessive self-promotion), (4) clearly describes how this milestone advances the matter toward ultimate objectives, (5) outlines the implications: what doors does this open, what becomes possible now, (6) identifies any risks or complications that client should understand (balanced positivity with realism), (7) outlines the next phase of work with timeline, (8) invites client to discuss the achievement and next steps, and (9) thanks client for their cooperation and trust.

The email should be approximately 250-350 words, maintain a tone that is genuinely pleased without being self-congratulatory, use clear practical language, and should be suitable for various [CLIENT TYPES]. Include specific examples of work performed. Balance celebration with realistic acknowledgment of remaining work. Include next steps clearly rather than leaving client uncertain about what comes next.

Context: When major milestones are achieved, significant legal victories occur, or substantial progress is made; appropriate for sending to keep client morale high and reinforce relationship value.
Difficulty: Beginner
Best AI tool: Any

**PROMPT 1.3 — Matter Status Update During Delay or Setback**

I need to inform [CLIENT NAME] of a delay or setback in their [MATTER TYPE] matter. [SPECIFIC DELAY OR SETBACK] has occurred, and I want to communicate transparently about what happened, impact on timeline, and how we're addressing it.

Draft a transparent and reassuring status update email that: (1) opens directly with clear statement of the issue (e.g., "I wanted to personally update you on a recent development affecting your matter"), avoiding sugarcoating while maintaining professionalism, (2) explains what happened in plain language: [SPECIFIC EVENT], (3) clearly states the cause: was this within our control or external? (e.g., "This resulted from [CAUSE], which [was/was not] within our control"), (4) frankly assesses the impact on timeline: how much has the matter been delayed, (5) explains any financial impact if relevant (additional costs, revised budget), (6) demonstrates active management: what we are doing to address this and minimize further impact, (7) provides revised timeline with realistic assessment of remaining work, (8) shows accountability: acknowledging any role we played and outlining measures to prevent recurrence, (9) confirms your commitment to protecting their interests and achieving best possible outcome, and (10) invites discussion and question-answering.

The email should be approximately 250-350 words, demonstrate genuine concern for client while projecting confidence and competence, use honest direct language (avoid defensiveness), and be suitable for [CLIENT TYPE]. Show concrete actions being taken rather than vague reassurances. Demonstrate understanding of client's concerns and frustrations. Provide realistic timelines rather than optimistic guesses.

Context: When matters experience delays (opposing counsel non-cooperation, court delays, client action delays), setbacks (adverse rulings, unfavorable developments), or complications (cost increases, scope expansion).

**PROMPT 1.4 — Action Required Status Update**

I need to notify [CLIENT NAME] that we require action or information from them to progress their [MATTER TYPE] matter. We need [SPECIFIC INFORMATION OR ACTION], and I want to clearly explain why it's needed, when it's needed, and consequences if not provided.

Draft a clear and action-oriented status update email that: (1) opens by stating what we need: [SPECIFIC INFORMATION OR ACTION REQUIRED], (2) clearly explains why this information is essential and what it enables (e.g., "This information is critical because [REASON]"), (3) details exactly what documents or information is needed: be specific (e.g., "We need: 1) [ITEM], 2) [ITEM], 3) [ITEM]") rather than vague, (4) explains the format and delivery requirements (e.g., "Please provide in electronic format, saved as PDFs, with filenames showing document description"), (5) clearly states the deadline with explanation of why timing matters (e.g., "We need this by [DATE] because [REASON]"), (6) explains consequences if deadline cannot be met (e.g., "If we don't have this information by [DATE], we will need to request a court extension, which may increase costs and delay resolution"), (7) offers alternative solutions if client is struggling (e.g., "If you cannot locate [DOCUMENT], we can request it from [SOURCE]"), (8) emphasizes that client action is within their control and time is limited, and (9) provides clear contact details and invitation to discuss questions or concerns.

The email should be approximately 250-350 words, be specific and directive without being rude, use numbered lists for clarity, and be suitable for [CLIENT TYPE]. Avoid legal jargon. Make clear why urgency matters from client's perspective (cost, timeline, outcome impact), not just from firm logistics perspective. Provide specific deadline dates, not vague "soon" references.

Context: When client information or action is needed, when documents must be obtained, when client decisions must be made, or when deadlines require acceleration.
Difficulty: Beginner
Best AI tool: Any

**PROMPT 1.5 — Interim Advice and Guidance Email**

I need to provide interim guidance on a matter issue affecting [CLIENT NAME]'s [MATTER TYPE] matter. I want to provide practical advice on [ISSUE] without waiting for formal advice letter, while maintaining appropriate documentation for the file.

Draft a professional interim guidance email that: (1) addresses a specific issue or question the client has raised, (2) provides clear practical guidance on the issue (e.g., "Based on the current circumstances, we recommend [RECOMMENDATION]"), (3) explains the reasoning behind the recommendation in plain language, (4) identifies any risks or downsides to the recommended approach, (5) identifies alternative approaches if they exist, with pros and cons of each, (6) clearly states what client needs to decide (decision point), (7) explains timeline for decision (e.g., "We need your decision by [DATE] because [REASON]"), (8) provides specific guidance on next steps once decision is made, (9) confirms this is interim guidance and indicates whether formal advice letter will follow, and (10) invites client to discuss or request clarification.

The email should be approximately 200-300 words, be practically useful while maintaining legal precision, use structured format (numbered points, bullet lists) for clarity, and be suitable for [CLIENT TYPE]. Avoid abstract legal discussion; focus on practical "what does this mean for you" approach. Be clear about what we're advising and what remaining uncertainty exists. Include timeline for decisions and next steps so client understands urgency.

Context: When clients have urgent questions, when interim guidance is needed before formal advice, or when quick guidance helps matter progression.

## SECTION 2: ADVICE LETTERS

**PROMPT 2.1 — Advice Letter on Matter Issues and Legal Questions**

I need to provide comprehensive written legal advice to [CLIENT NAME] on a significant issue affecting their [MATTER TYPE] matter. The issue is [SPECIFIC ISSUE], which requires detailed analysis and clear recommendations.

Draft a comprehensive advice letter that: (1) opens with a clear statement of the legal question being addressed (e.g., "You have asked us to advise on [QUESTION]"), (2) sets out the relevant facts as understood (summarizing key circumstances), (3) identifies any assumptions we're making about facts or circumstances, (4) provides clear legal analysis of the issue, (5) cites relevant law: cases, statutes, regulations applicable in [JURISDICTION], (6) discusses how the law applies to your specific circumstances, (7) addresses any counterarguments or alternative interpretations of law, (8) provides clear conclusion on the legal question, (9) sets out practical implications of the law for client's situation (what this means in practical terms), (10) provides clear recommendations for client action, (11) identifies any risks, uncertainties, or areas where law is unclear, (12) explains any next steps required or decisions client needs to make, and (13) invites follow-up discussion if client has questions or circumstances change.

The letter should be approximately 800-1,500 words depending on complexity, use plain English explanations of legal concepts, structured with clear headings and sections for navigation, use numbered paragraphs for legal opinions, and conclude with clear recommendations. Include citations to supporting authorities. Include appropriate limitations and qualifications to manage liability. Address practical "what does this mean for your business" implications, not just abstract legal discussion.

Context: When significant legal issues require written analysis, when client decisions depend on legal advice, when documented advice is important for the file, or when complex analysis requires structured presentation.
Difficulty: Advanced
Best AI tool: Claude

**PROMPT 2.2 — Risk Assessment Letter on Legal Exposure**

I need to provide [CLIENT NAME] with a frank assessment of the legal risks and potential exposure in their [MATTER TYPE] matter. We have reviewed the matter thoroughly and identified [SPECIFIC RISKS], and I want to provide honest assessment while maintaining client confidence.

Draft a risk assessment letter that: (1) confirms our thorough review and analysis of the matter, (2) provides honest assessment of legal prospects: realistic evaluation of likelihood of success or failure, (3) assesses potential financial exposure: what client could lose if matter proceeds negatively, (4) identifies specific weaknesses in client's position and why they present risk, (5) identifies strengths in client's position and why these support positive outcome, (6) discusses uncertainty factors: areas where law is unclear or facts are disputed, (7) compares risk against alternative approaches (e.g., settlement vs. litigation), (8) provides quantified risk assessment where possible (e.g., "we assess 40% likelihood of judgment of [AMOUNT]"), (9) recommends risk mitigation strategies, (10) discusses insurance coverage or indemnification if relevant, and (11) recommends decision points: when to reassess risk if circumstances change.

The letter should be approximately 600-1,000 words, use clear language to communicate risk without being unnecessarily alarming, structure analysis by risk category, provide honest assessment even if unfavorable, and include appropriate qualifications (e.g., "subject to obtaining further information, our assessment is..."). Balance candor about risks with professional confidence in addressing them. Include discussion of what scenarios would increase or decrease risk. Emphasize client's role in decision-making about accepting/mitigating risk.

Context: When significant legal risks exist, when clients need realistic assessment of matter prospects, when risk decisions are required, or when litigation prospects need evaluation.
Difficulty: Advanced
Best AI tool: Claude

The letter should be approximately 1,000-1,500 words, organize recommendations by priority level (urgent, important, good practice), provide specific step-by-step guidance for each compliance requirement, cite specific regulatory provisions and authoritative sources, and include cost implications where relevant. Include practical examples showing compliant vs. non-compliant approaches. Include timelines and milestones for achieving compliance. Address both legal requirements and industry best practices.

Context: When regulatory obligations need assessment, when compliance issues are identified, when regulatory changes require business adjustment, or when regulatory risk exists.

**PROMPT 2.4 — Strategic Options Letter**

I need to advise [CLIENT NAME] on strategic options available for their [MATTER TYPE] matter. We have completed our analysis and want to present options with pros/cons to enable informed decision-making.

Draft a strategic options letter that: (1) confirms the client's primary objectives and constraints, (2) presents [3-4] distinct strategic options available (e.g., Option 1: pursue litigation aggressively; Option 2: seek negotiated settlement; Option 3: pursue regulatory complaint), (3) for each option, provides: description of approach, advantages/benefits, disadvantages/risks, likely timeline and costs, likely outcome probability assessment, (4) compares options against each other, showing relative advantages, (5) provides recommendation: which option best serves client interests and why, (6) discusses hybrid approaches: combining elements of different options, (7) identifies decision factors that should drive client's choice, (8) explains decision timeline: when client must decide and why timing matters, (9) describes next steps under each option, and (10) confirms your readiness to implement whichever option client selects.

The letter should be approximately 800-1,200 words, use clear comparative structure (table format can help), discuss each option objectively even if recommending one, provide realistic assessment of likely outcomes, and include cost and timeline estimates. Make the letter decision-enabling rather than decision-forcing: give client clear information to decide. Address client's specific circumstances and priorities in recommending option. Include discussion of what information or developments would change the recommendation.

Context: When significant strategic decisions are required, when multiple approaches are available, when client needs to choose direction, or when business strategy affects legal approach.

## SECTION 3: ENGAGEMENT LETTERS & RETAINERS

**PROMPT 3.1 — Comprehensive Engagement Letter**

I need to prepare an engagement letter for [CLIENT NAME] engaging our firm to provide [LEGAL SERVICES] for their [MATTER TYPE] matter. The engagement is for [SPECIFIC SCOPE], and I want to clearly communicate terms, scope, fees, and client responsibilities.

Draft a comprehensive engagement letter that covers: (1) introduction and confirmation of engagement (confirming that firm will provide services on stated terms), (2) services to be provided (detailed description of what we will do, with specific services listed: e.g., "draft initial submissions; review opposing counsel responses; attend 2 hearings"), (3) services excluded (clearly stating what we will NOT do, e.g., "this engagement does not include tax advice" or "criminal matter representation"), (4) scope limitations (defining any boundary limitations, e.g., "limited to [JURISDICTION]" or "limited to [SPECIFIC ISSUE]"), (5) client responsibilities (what client must provide: instructions, information, documents, decisions; client's role in timeline and decision-making), (6) fee structure (explaining how we charge: hourly rates, fixed fees, contingency, mixed; providing rate schedule if applicable; explaining billing frequency), (7) disbursements and costs (explaining what additional costs client may incur: court fees, expert fees, court transcripts, etc.), (8) billing and payment terms (invoice frequency, payment deadline, late payment consequences, retainer requirements if applicable), (9) trust account information (if retainer is required, explaining how retainer is held and managed), (10) termination (explaining how either party can terminate engagement and on what notice), (11) limitation of liability (including any liability caps or exclusions), (12) confidentiality and privilege (explaining what client information is protected and what information client must keep confidential), (13) conflicts of interest (confirming no conflicts, or explaining any disclosed conflicts), (14) insurance (explaining our professional indemnity insurance), (15) dispute resolution (explaining how disputes about billing or services will be resolved), (16) privacy and data protection (confirming PDPA compliance), and (17) execution block.

The letter should be approximately 1,200-1,500 words, use clear plain English, be suitable for [CLIENT TYPE], include specific examples rather than vague statements (e.g., "We expect to charge [ESTIMATED RANGE]" rather than "fees vary"), and be organized with clear headings and sections for navigation. Include bracketed placeholders for customization. Include signature block for both client and firm representative. Ensure letter is clear about what client is consenting to and what terms are binding. Consider whether engagement letter should be signed or is purely informational.

Context: When engaging new clients, when formalizing scope, when clarifying fee arrangements, or when establishing engagement terms.
Difficulty: Intermediate
Best AI tool: Claude
Follow-up: PROMPT 1.1 — Routine Matter Status Update Email

**PROMPT 3.2 — Fee Estimate and Proposal Letter**

I need to provide [CLIENT NAME] with a fee estimate and proposal for [LEGAL SERVICES] addressing their [MATTER TYPE] matter. I want to provide realistic cost estimate while remaining competitive, and help client make informed decision about engaging our firm.

Draft a fee estimate and proposal letter that: (1) acknowledges client's inquiry and confirms we understand the scope of required services, (2) provides a summary of services we propose to provide, (3) explains our fee structure (hourly, fixed, contingency, hybrid), (4) provides a detailed fee estimate or range broken down by service component (e.g., "Document review: $[X]; Drafting: $[X]; Meetings/calls: $[X]"), (5) provides total estimated fee for the matter or engagement, (6) explains assumptions underlying the estimate (e.g., "This estimate assumes [ASSUMPTIONS]"), (7) explains what would increase or decrease the estimate (e.g., "If we must attend more than 2 court hearings, additional hearing fees of $[X] per hearing would apply"), (8) confirms what is/is not included in the estimate (disbursements, court fees, expert fees), (9) explains timing: when fees will be incurred and over what period, (10) explains payment terms and retainer requirements if applicable, (11) provides comparison with alternative fee structures if relevant (e.g., "fixed fee of $[X] alternatively available"), (12) addresses any value-adds: what makes our firm the right choice, (13) confirms our next steps if client approves the proposal, and (14) provides deadline for proposal validity (e.g., "This proposal is valid until [DATE]").

The letter should be approximately 400-600 words, provide specific numbers rather than vague estimates, be transparent about what drives costs, and be suitable for [CLIENT TYPE]. Include examples showing how different scenarios would affect cost. Avoid legal jargon. Frame pricing as value proposition: show what client gets for their investment. Include comparison to alternative approaches if relevant (e.g., "DIY approach vs. professional assistance").

Context: When clients are requesting fee estimates, when competing against other firms, when deciding whether to engage services, or when cost is a major decision factor.

The letter should be approximately 400-600 words, clearly distinguish included services from excluded services, use examples of typical matters covered by the retainer, and be suitable for [CLIENT TYPE]. I need to inform [CLIENT NAME] of bad news regarding their [MATTER TYPE] matter. [SPECIFIC BAD DEVELOPMENT] has occurred, and I want to communicate clearly about what happened, why it matters, and what we do next.

Draft a bad news communication email or letter that: (1) opens directly with clear statement of the bad news (e.g., "I need to inform you of an unfavorable development in your matter"), without burying the lead, (2) clearly explains what happened in plain language, (3) explains why this is problematic and what it means for the matter, (4) honestly assesses impact on prospects (e.g., "This makes our prospects more challenging"), (5) explains how this happened, distinguishing between circumstances beyond our control vs. any role we played, (6) if we contributed, takes appropriate responsibility and explains how we're addressing it, (7) demonstrates active management: what we're doing to address the situation and minimize further impact, (8) provides options for moving forward (if multiple options exist), (9) outlines next steps and timeline, (10) confirms our continued commitment to protecting client interests, and (11) invites discussion and questions.

The tone should be professional and factual, honest about negative news without being unnecessarily alarming, demonstrate competence and active management despite bad news, and maintain client confidence. The email should be approximately 300-500 words. Lead with the bad news rather than burying it. Show what we're doing to address it, not just what went wrong. Provide client with options and recommended path forward.

Context: When adverse court rulings occur, when opposing positions strengthen, when deadlines are missed, when strategies fail, or when matters take negative turns.

I need to address a client concern about fees or billing in their matter. [CLIENT CONCERN ABOUT FEES] has arisen, and I want to address the concern transparently while defending the fees charged.

If fees were appropriate, explain why firmly but professionally. If fees were excessive, consider offering modest adjustment to maintain relationship. Offer to review future billing practices if there's concern about cost control.

## SECTION 5: CLIENT FEEDBACK AND SERVICE

**PROMPT 5.1 — Client Feedback Request and Service Evaluation**

I need to request feedback from [CLIENT NAME] regarding their recent engagement with our firm on their [MATTER TYPE] matter. I want to understand their experience and identify areas for improvement while maintaining the relationship.

Draft a client feedback request that: (1) thanks client for engaging our firm and confirms matter conclusion or status, (2) explains that we value client feedback and use it to continuously improve our service, (3) provides structured feedback questions addressing: quality of legal advice, responsiveness and communication, cost-value assessment, overall satisfaction, specific areas of strength, areas for improvement, likelihood to recommend us to others, (4) offers multiple feedback methods: online form, brief call, email response to questions, (5) confirms that feedback is genuinely valued and will be read carefully, (6) offers anonymity option if client prefers not to be identified, (7) provides deadline for feedback (but not so urgent that client feels pressured), (8) thanks client in advance for feedback, and (9) includes contact information for any questions about the feedback process.

Consider offering incentive for feedback (e.g., discount on future services). Use feedback to identify firm-wide improvement opportunities.

Context: When matters are concluded, periodically during ongoing engagements, when service has been exceptional or problematic, or when soliciting testimonials.

**PROMPT 5.3 — Service Recovery After Complaint or Concern**

I need to address a client concern or complaint about service quality in their [MATTER TYPE] matter. [SPECIFIC COMPLAINT: missed deadline, poor communication, inadequate advice] has caused client dissatisfaction, and I need to address the concern and restore confidence.
